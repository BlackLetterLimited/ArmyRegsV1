Article 87 – JUMPING FROM VESSEL

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), the accused jumped from (state the name or description of the vessel), a vessel in use by the armed forces, into the water; and

(2) That such act by the accused was wrongful and intentional.

Definitions:

“In use by” means any vessel operated by or under the control of the armed forces. This offense may be committed at sea, at anchor or in port.

“Wrongful” means without legal justification or excuse.

“Intentional” means deliberately or on purpose.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE, were you onboard or attached to a vessel?

1b: What was the vessel?

1c: Do you agree this vessel was operated by or under the control of the armed forces?

1d: Where was the vessel located? Was the vessel underway?

1e: Did you jump from this vessel into the water? Describe what you did.

2a: Why did you jump from this vessel?

2b: Did you deliberately jump from the vessel? It was your intent to jump from the vessel?

2c: Did you have any legal justification or excuse to jump from the vessel?

2d: Did anything make you jump from the vessel?

2e: Did you have any mental condition that caused you to jump? (Was this a suicide attempt?)
