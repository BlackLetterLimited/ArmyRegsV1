Article 94 – FAILURE TO REPORT A MUTINY OR SEDITION

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), an offense of (mutiny) (sedition) among (state the description of those engaged in the mutiny or sedition, as alleged) was taking place;

(2) That you (knew) (or) (had reason to believe) that the offense was taking place; and

(3) That you failed to take all reasonable means to inform (his) (her) superior commissioned officer or (his) (her) commanding officer that the (mutiny) (sedition) was taking place.

Definitions:

The elements of the offense of (mutiny) (sedition) are as follows:

A failure to take “all reasonable means” to inform a superior includes the failure to take the most expeditious means available. (You can be said to have had “reason to believe” that (mutiny) (sedition) was taking place when the circumstances which were known you were such as would have caused a reasonable person in the same or similar circumstances to believe that a (mutiny) (sedition) was taking place.)

Proof that you actually participated in the (mutiny) (sedition) or that the offense was committed in your presence is unnecessary for you to be found guilty of this offense if you had pled not guilty.

A “superior commissioned officer" means a superior commissioned officer in your chain of command.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On (date) at (place), was an offense of mutiny or sedition taking place?

1b: Was it mutiny by creating violence or a disturbance? Or was it mutiny by refusing to follow orders or to perform duty? Or was it sedition?

1c: For mutiny by creating violence or a disturbance:

Were the individual(s) creating violence or a disturbance?

How so?

Did they intend to usurp or override military authority when they were creating this violence or disturbance?

1d: For mutiny by refusing to follow orders or to perform duty:

Were the individuals refusing to obey the orders of _________? Or were they refusing to perform their duty(ies)?

Was _______ a person with the authority to give them orders? How so?

Were they required to follow those orders?

What were those orders (or what was their duty)?

Were they acting in concert?

Did they intend to usurp or override military authority when they were taking this act (these actions)?

1e: For sedition:

Were the individuals creating a (revolt)(violence)(disturbance) against lawful civil authority?

How so?

Were they acting in concert?

Did they intend to cause the (overthrow) (destruction) (overthrow and destruction) of lawful civil authority namely (name of lawful civil authority).

2: Did you know or have reason to believe that the offense was taking place? Which one?

3a: Did you fail to take all reasonable means to inform your commanding officer that the offense of (mutiny) (sedition) was taking place?

3b: Did anyone or anything cause you to fail to take all reasonable means to inform your commanding officer that the offense of (mutiny) (sedition) was taking place?
