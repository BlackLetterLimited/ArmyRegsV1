Article 77 - AIDING, ABETTING, COUNSELING, COMMANDING, OR PROCURING

Elements:

(1) That [give the elements of the offense charged indicating that the actual perpetrator, and not the accused, is the one who is alleged to have committed the offense]; and

(2) That (state the name of ##the accused##) ((aided and abetted) (counseled) (commanded) (procured) (caused)) (state the name of the actual perpetrator) ((to commit) (in committing)) the offense of (state the alleged offense) by (state the manner alleged).

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE, did PERPETRATOR commit the offense of OFFENSE?

1b: In doing so, did PERPETRATOR commit every element of OFFENSE as I have described those elements for you?

1c: DISCUSS.

2b: Did you aid and abetted, counsel, command, procure, or caused PERPETRATOR to commit OFFENSE?

2c: Which one or ones did you do?

2d: How did you do so?
