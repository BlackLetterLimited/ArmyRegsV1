Article 120 – SEXUAL ASSAULT BY THREAT, FRAUDULENT REPRESENTATION, OR ARTIFICE

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), the accused committed (a) sexual act(s) upon (state the name of the alleged victim), by causing penetration, however slight, of (state the name of the alleged victim)’s (vulva) (anus) (mouth) by (the accused’s (penis) (state other body part)) (a (state object)); (and)

(2) That you did so by

(a) threatening or placing (state the name of the alleged victim) in fear;

(b) making a fraudulent representation that the sexual act served a professional purpose;

(c) inducing a belief by artifice, pretense, or concealment that the accused was another person; (and)

NOTE 1: Penetration by object or body part other than penis. If the penetration occurs by something other than the Accused’s penis, add the following element.

[(3)] That the accused did so with an intent to abuse, humiliate, harass, or degrade any person or to arouse or gratify the sexual desire of any person.

Definitions:

“Threatening or placing a person in fear” means a communication or action that is of sufficient consequence to cause a reasonable fear that non-compliance will result in the alleged victim or another person being subjected to the wrongful action contemplated by the communication or action.

"Wrongful action," as used here, includes abuse of military rank, position, or authority in order to engage in sexual act with an alleged victim. This includes, but is not limited to, threats to initiate an adverse personnel action unless the alleged victim submits to your requested sexual act; and, threats to withhold favorable personnel action unless the alleged victim submits to your requested sexual act. Superiority in rank is a factor in, but not dispositive of, whether a reasonable person in the position of the alleged victim would fear that his or her noncompliance with your desired sexual act would result in the threatened wrongful action contemplated by the communication or action.

In proving that a person made a threat, it need not be proven that the person actually intended to carry out the threat or had the ability to carry out the threat.

The threat or fear in this case must be that the alleged victim or another person would be subjected to the wrongful action.

A “fraudulent representation” is a representation of fact, which you know to be untrue, which is intended to deceive, which does in fact deceive, and which causes the other person to engage in the sexual act(s).

The fraudulent representation that the sexual act served a professional purpose need not have been made by you to (state the name of the alleged victim). It is sufficient if you made such a fraudulent representation to any person, which thereby caused (state the name of the alleged victim) to engage in the sexual act.

“Consent” means a freely given agreement to the conduct at issue by a competent person. An expression of lack of consent through words or conduct means there is no consent. Lack of verbal or physical resistance or submission resulting from the use of force, threat of force, or placing another person in fear does not constitute consent. A current or previous dating or social or sexual relationship by itself or the manner of dress of the person involved with the accused in the conduct at issue shall not constitute consent.

A person cannot consent to a sexual act while under threat or in fear.

A person cannot consent to a sexual act when believing, due to a fraudulent representation, that the sexual act served a professional purpose, or when believing, due to artifice, pretense, or concealment that the accused was another person.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On or about DATE, did you commit a sexual act upon VICTIM?

1b: What specifically did you do?

1c: Did you actually penetrate VICTIM’s vulva, anus, or mouth?

1d: What body part specifically caused this penetration?

[Threat or Placing in Fear]

2a: Did you make VICTIM believe that if he/she did not comply with your requested sexual act that someone would be subject to a wrongful action?

2b: What specifically did you threaten to do?

2c: How did you communicate this threat?

2d: Did you intend for VICTIM to believe that you would carry out this wrongful action?

2e: Who was the subject of this wrongful action?

2f: Did you specifically threaten to initiate an adverse personnel action unless the alleged victim submits to your requested sexual act; or threaten to withhold favorable personnel action unless the alleged victim submits to your requested sexual act.

2g: Which one?

2h: Do you believe a reasonable person in the VICTIM’s position would believe that you would carry out these actions if VICTIM did not comply with your requests for sexual acts?

2i: Why is that?

2j: How do you know that VICTIM did not agree?

2k: Did you know at the time of the sexual act that VICTIM was only agreeing to the sexual act due to the threatened wrongful actions?

[Fraudulent Representation]

2a: Did you or someone else represent to VICTIM that the sexual acts would serve some professional purpose?

2b: Please explain what the professional purpose was?

2c: Did you know that this representation was untrue at the time?

2d: Did you intend to deceive victim by telling him/her that the sexual acts served a professional purpose?

2e: Do you believe that VICTIM was in fact deceived?

2f: Do you agree that VICTIM would not have allowed you to engage in the ssexual acts had he/she known the truth?

[Another Person]

2a: Did you engage in the sexual act by inducing in VICTIM a belief by artifice, pretense, or concealment that you were another person?

2b: How did you induce this belief?

2c: Did VICTIM in fact believe that you were another person?

2d: Who did VICTIM believe you were?

2e: How do you know that VICTIM believed you were another person?

2f: Did you know at the time that VICTIM believed you were someone else?

3a: What was your intent in causing this penetration of the (vulva), (anus), (mouth)?

3b: Did you specifically intend to abuse, humiliate, or degrade any person or arouse or gratify the sexual desire of any person?

3c: Which one?

4a: Did anyone or anything force or coerce you to commit this sexual act?

4b: Could you have avoided committing this act if you had wanted to?

4c: Do you believe you have any legal justification or excuse for committing this act?
