Article 107 – FALSE OFFICIAL STATEMENT

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you (signed a certain official document) (made to (state the name of the person to whom the statement was allegedly made) a certain official statement), that is: (describe the document or statement as alleged);

(2) That such (document) (statement) was (totally false) (false in that (state the allegedly false matters);

(3) That you knew it to be false at the time you (signed) (made) it; and

(4) That the false (document) (statement) was made with the intent to deceive.

Definitions:

A statement may be made orally, in writing, or both and include records, returns, regulations, orders, or other documents.

A statement is official when the maker is either acting in the line of duty or the statement bears a clear and direct relationship to the maker’s official military duties, or where the receiver is either a military member carrying out a military duty when the statement is made or a civilian necessarily performing a military function when the statement is made. The rank or status of the person intended to be deceived is immaterial. The Government may be the victim of this offense.

“Intent to deceive” means to purposely mislead, to cheat, to trick another, or to cause another to believe as true that which is false. It is not necessary that the false statement be material to the issue at inquiry. The expectation of material gain is not an element of this offense.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE, did you make an official statement or sign a certain official document? Which one?

1b: What did that statement / document pertain to?

1c: Was this done in your official capacity?

1d: Was this done in the line of duty; in other words, did you have a duty to make a statement?

1e: Who did you make this statement to?

1f: What were the circumstances in which you came to make this statement?

1g: Do you believe and admit that your statement was made to a person who, in receiving it, was discharging the functions of his particular office?

1h: Was this individual conducting an investigation into your alleged misconduct at the time?

1i: Were you advised of any rights under Article 31?

1j: Do you believe and admit that, even if you had no duty to answer or provide a statement, by choosing to do so, you had an official duty to do so truthfully?

2a: Was this statement which you made false?

2b: In what respect?

3a: Did you know at the time that this statement was false?

3b: How did you know it was false?

4a: Was this done to make another believe this statement was true when you, in fact, knew it to be false?

4b: Did you intend to deceive him/her?

4c: Why did you want to deceive this individual by making a false statement?

4d: Did you make this false statement voluntarily?

4e: Do you have any legal justification or excuse for making this false statement?

4f: Did anyone force or coerce you into making this statement?

4g: Could you have made a truthful statement if you had wanted to?
