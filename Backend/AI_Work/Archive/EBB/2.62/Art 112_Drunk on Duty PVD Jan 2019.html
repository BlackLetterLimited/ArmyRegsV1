Article 112 – DRUNK ON DUTY

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you were on duty as (state the nature of the military duty); and

(2) That (he) (she) was found drunk while on this duty.

Drunk” means the state intoxication by alcohol that is sufficient to impair the rational and full exercise of mental or physical faculties, or the state of meeting or exceeding a blood alcohol content limit with respect to alcohol concentration in a person's blood of 0.08 grams of alcohol per 100 milliliters of blood and with respect to alcohol concentration in a person's breath of 0.08 grams of alcohol per 210 liters of breath, as shown by chemical analysis.

“Duty” means military duty. Every duty which an officer or enlisted person may legally be required by superior authority to execute is necessarily a military duty.

“On duty” means duties or routine or detail, in garrison, at a station, or in the field. It does not relate to those periods when the person is considered (“off duty”) (or) (“on liberty”). (In a region of active hostilities, the circumstances are often such that all members of a command may properly be considered as being continuously on duty.)

(Commanders, while in the field or on board a ship, are constantly on duty when in the actual exercise of command.)

(An officer of the day and members of the guard, or of the watch, are on duty during their entire tour.)

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On ______ were you on duty?

1b: What was your assigned duty?

1c: Where were you?

1d: Had you been relieved from your assigned duties?

2a: While you were on duty, were you intoxicated?

2b: Was your intoxication a result of alcohol use? How much alcohol did you drink?

2c: Was there a breathalyzer conducted (or other blood alcohol test administered)? Do you know the results of that test?

2d: At the time you were on duty, was your state of intoxication sufficient to impair the rational and full exercise of your mental or physical faculties?

2e: In what way/manner was your impairment shown?

2e: Do you believe that you were drunk at the time? Why?

2f: How did your intoxication become known?

2g: Do believe that you had any legal excuse or justification to be drunk on duty?

2h: Do you believe that you could have refrained from being intoxicated/drunk while on duty?

2i: Did anything or anyone force you to be drunk on duty?
