Article 97 – UNLAWFUL DETENTION

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you (apprehended) (arrested) (confined) (state the name of the person allegedly detained);
(and)

(2) That you unlawfully exercised (his) (her) authority to do so;
[and]

NOTE 1: Belief in lawfulness of confinement in issue. Element (3) must be given if there is any evidence from which it may justifiably be inferred that the accused may have had a reasonable belief that the restraint was lawful. See also Instruction 5-11, Ignorance or Mistake of Fact or Law—General Discussion, for additional instructions which may be appropriate when such issue arises.

[(3)] That you had no reasonable belief that the (apprehension) (arrest) (confinement) was lawful.

Definitions:

“Apprehension” means taking a person into custody; that is, placing a restraint on a person’s freedom of movement. The restraint may be physical and forcible, or it may be imposed by clearly informing the person being apprehended that (he) (she) is being taken into custody. An apprehension is attempted, then, by clearly informing a person orally or in writing that (he) (she) is being taken into custody or by attempting to use a degree and kind of force which clearly indicates that (he) (she) is being taken into custody.

The apprehension, arrest, or confinement must be against the will of the person restrained, but force is not required.

It is a defense to this charge that you held a reasonable believe that the arrest, apprehension or confinement was lawful.

“Arrest” is the restraint of a person by oral or written order directing a person to remain within specified limits.

“Confinement” is physical restraint, which deprives a person of freedom, imposed as pre-trial confinement, as a result of nonjudicial punishment, or as post-trial confinement.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE, at PLACE, did you arrest/apprehend/confine a certain person?

1b: Who was PERSON?

1c: Why did you apprehend/arrest/confine PERSON?

2a: Did you arrest/apprehend/confine PERSON unlawfully?

2b: Were you a member of a police force or investigative agency?

2c: Did you present identification when trying to apprehend/arrest/confine PERSON?

2d: Did you verbally inform PERSON of your credentials?

2e: Were you acting under color of authority when you arrested/apprehended/confined PERSON?

2f: Do you now believe you had any proper authority to apprehend/arrest/confine PERSON? Why?

2g: Do you believe your conduct was wrongful?

2h: Could you have avoided making the apprehension/arrest/confinement of PERSON if you had wanted to?

2i: Did anyone force or coerce you into this conduct?

2j: Do you believe you had any legal justification or excuse for making the apprehension/arrest/confinement of PERSON?

If belief the arrest/apprehension/confinement was lawful is raised, as question 3 below.

3a: At the time, did you believe you had the authority to lawfully apprehend/arrest/confine PERSON?

3b: Why did you believe/not believe you had such authority?

3c: Do you now think your belief was reasonable? Why/why not?

3d: Do you believe and admit that, at the time you arrested/apprehended/confined PERSON, you had no reasonable belief that the arrest/apprehension/confinement was lawful.
