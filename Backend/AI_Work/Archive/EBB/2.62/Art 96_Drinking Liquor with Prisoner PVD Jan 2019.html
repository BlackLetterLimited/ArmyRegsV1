Article 96 – DRINKING LIQUOR WITH PRISONER

Elements:

(1) That (state the name of the prisoner) was a prisoner; and

(2) That (state the time and place alleged) you unlawfully drank (an) alcoholic beverage(s) with (state the name of the prisoner).

Definitions:

The term prisoner is a person who is in confinement or custody imposed under R.C.M. 302, 304, or 305, or under sentence of a court-martial who has not been set free by proper authority.

The term liquor includes any alcoholic beverage.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE at PLACE was PRISONER a prisoner, as I’ve defined that term for you?

1b: Did you know that at the time?

1c: How did you know PRISONER was a prisoner?

1d: What was your relationship to PRISONER at the time?

1e: What was your position?

1f: What were your duties?

2a: On DATE at PLACE did you drink alcoholic beverages with PRISONER?

2b: Did you do so unlawfully?

2c: Did you provide the liquor?

2d: Where did you get it?

2e: What kind of liquor was it?

2f: Explain how you came to be drinking liquor with PRISONER.

2g: How much did you both drink?

2h: Where were you drinking the liquor?

2i: Did you have any reason to believe it was lawful to drink liquor PRISONER?

2j: Did anyone give you authority to do this?
