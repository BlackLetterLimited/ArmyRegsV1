Article 129 – UNLAWFUL ENTRY

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you entered (real property) (personal property amounting to a structure usually used for habitation or storage) (__________) of another, to wit: (state the property as alleged in the specification)); and

(2) That such entry was unlawful.

Definitions:

An entry is “unlawful” if it is made without the consent of any person authorized to consent to entry or without other lawful authority. An entry of any part of the body or the insertion of any tool or other device is sufficient. It is not necessary that the property be in use at the time of the entry.

“Structure” refers to only those structures which are in the nature of a building or dwelling. Examples include: A stateroom, hold, or other compartment of a vessel; an inhabitable trailer; an enclosed truck or freight car; a tent; a houseboat.

It is not necessary that the building or structure be in use at the time of entry.
