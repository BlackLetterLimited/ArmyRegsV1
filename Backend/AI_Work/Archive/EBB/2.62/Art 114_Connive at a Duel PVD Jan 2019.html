Article 114 – CONNIVE AT FIGHTING A DUEL

Elements:

(1) That (state the names of the alleged duelers) intended to and were about to engage in a duel at or near (state the place alleged);

(2) That you had knowledge of the planned duel; and

(3) That (state the time and place alleged), you connived at the fighting of the duel by (state the manner alleged).

Definitions:

"Duel" means combat between two persons for private reasons fought with deadly weapons by prior agreement.

A "deadly weapon" is one which is used in a manner likely to produce death or grievous bodily harm.

A weapon is "likely" to produce death or grievous bodily harm when the probable results of its use would be death or serious bodily injury (although this may not be the use to which the instrument is ordinarily put).

It is not necessary that death or serious bodily injury actually occur.

To “connive” at the fighting of the duel means to fail to notify appropriate authorities or to take other reasonable action to prevent the duel.

Anyone who knows that steps are being or have been taken toward arranging or fighting a duel and who fails to notify appropriate authorities and to take other reasonable preventive action has committed this offense.
