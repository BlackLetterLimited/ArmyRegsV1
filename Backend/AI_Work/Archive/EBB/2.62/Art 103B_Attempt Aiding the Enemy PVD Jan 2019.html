Article 103B – ATTEMPTING TO AID THE ENEMY

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you did a certain act, namely: (state the manner in which the giving of aid was allegedly attempted);

(2) That (state the name or description of the enemy who purportedly was to receive the aid) was an enemy;

(3) That the act was done with the intent to aid the enemy with certain arms, ammunition, supplies, money or other things;

(4) That the act amounted to more than mere preparation; that is, it was a direct movement toward the offense of aiding the enemy; and

(5) That the act apparently tended to bring about the offense of aiding the enemy with certain arms, ammunition, supplies, money or other things; that is, the act apparently would have resulted in the actual commission of the offense of aiding the enemy except for (a circumstance unknown to you) (an unexpected intervening circumstance) (__________) which prevented the completion of the offense).

Definitions:

Proof that the offense of aiding the enemy actually occurred or was completed is not required.

To "aid the enemy" means to furnish it with (arms) (ammunition) (supplies) (money) (__________), (whether or not the articles furnished were needed by the enemy) (and) (whether or not the transaction was a sale or a donation).

"Enemy" includes (not only) organized opposing forces in time of war, (but also any other hostile body that our forces may be opposing) (such as a rebellious mob or a band of renegades) (and includes civilians as well as members of military organizations).

"Enemy" is not restricted to the enemy government or its armed forces. All the citizens of one belligerent are enemies of the government and the citizens of the other.

Preparation consists of devising or arranging the means necessary for the commission of the attempted offense. The acts which you did must amount to a substantial step and a direct movement toward the commission of the intended offense of Aiding the Enemy.

A substantial step is one that is strongly corroborative of your criminal intent and is indicative of your resolve to commit the offense. Whether or not the Aiding the Enemy actually occurred or was completed, you must have intended every element of Aiding the Enemy.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE at PLACE, did you do ACT?

1b. How did you do ACT?

2a: Who was ENEMY?

2b: What made ENEMY an enemy?

2c: Did you know when you did ACT that ENEMY was an enemy?

2d: How did you know ENEMY was an enemy?

3a: When you did ACT, did you specifically intend to aid ENEMY?

3b: Describe the (arms) (ammunition) (supplies) (money) (__________) you intended to supply to ENEMY.

3c: When you did ACT, had you already obtained the (arms) (ammunition) (supplies) (money) (__________) you intended to supply to ENEMY?

3d: If so, how did you obtain the (arms) (ammunition) (supplies) (money) (__________) you intended to supply to ENEMY?

3e: If you had not already obtained them, how did you intend to obtain the (arms) (ammunition) (supplies) (money) (__________) you intended to supply to ENEMY?

3f: Did you supply the (arms) (ammunition) (supplies) (money) (__________) to ENEMY?

3g: If so, how did you supply the (arms) (ammunition) (supplies) (money) (__________) to ENEMY?

3g: If so, how did you supply the (arms) (ammunition) (supplies) (money) (__________) to ENEMY?

3h: If you did not supply the (arms) (ammunition) (supplies) (money) (__________) to ENEMY, how did you intend to do so?

3i: Did anyone give you the authority to do so?

3j: Did you have any legal justification or excuse for doing so?

3k: Was it a freely made decision on your part to attempt to aid the enemy in this manner?

3l: Did anyone force or coerce you to do this?

3m: Could you have avoided attempting to aid the enemy if you had wanted?

4a: Do you believe and admit that your actions were more than merely preparatory steps, that is, were clearly substantial steps made directly towards the commission of Aiding the Enemy?

4b: How so?

5a: Do you believe and admit that your attempt would have been successful but for the fact that (a circumstance unknown to you) (an unexpected intervening circumstance) prevented completion of that offense?

5b: Would ACT have brought about the offense of aiding the enemy with certain arms, ammunition, supplies, money or other things had there not been for an unknown or unexpected circumstance?

5c: What was that circumstance?

5d: How did that circumstance prevent the commission of the offense of Aiding the Enemy?
