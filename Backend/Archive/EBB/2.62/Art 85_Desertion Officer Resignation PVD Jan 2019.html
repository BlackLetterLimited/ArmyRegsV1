Article 85 – DESERTION BEFORE NOTICE OF ACCEPTANCE OF RESIGNATION

Elements:

(1) That the accused was a commissioned officer of the United States (Navy/Marine Corps) (__________) and had tendered (his) (her) resignation;

(2) That (state the time and place alleged) and before (he) (she) received notice of the acceptance of the resignation, the accused quit (his) (her) (post) (proper duties), that is, (state the post or proper duties alleged), without leave;

(3) That the accused did so with the intent to remain away from (his) (her) (post) (proper duties) permanently, (and)

(4) That the accused remained so absent until (state the date alleged); [and]

NOTE 1: Aggravating factors alleged. In the event one or more of the aggravating factors are alleged, the military judge must advise the court members of the aggravating factors as elements.

((5)) That the accused’s absence was in time of war; [and]

((6)) That the accused’s absence was terminated by apprehension.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Were you a commissioned officer on DATE?

1b: When did you receive your commission?

1c: Had you formally tendered your resignation before DATE?

1d: When and to whom had you tendered your resignation?

2a: On DATE, had you received notice that your resignation had been formally accepted?

2b: What was your unit (organization)(place of duty) on DATE?

2c: Where was it located?

2d: Were you with your unit on DATE?

2e: How did your absence from UNIT begin?

2f: Where were you?

2g: Why weren’t you with your UNIT on DATE?

2h: Was it a freely made decision on your part to leave or not return to your UNIT?

2i: Could you have been with your UNIT on DATE if you had wanted to?

2j: Did you have permission or authority from anyone in military authority to be absent from UNIT?

2k: Did you think you had permission from anyone to be absent?

3a: On the date that you left UNIT or anytime thereafter, did you form the intent to stay away permanently?

3b: Describe when and how you formulated your intent.

3c: Describe what steps you took, if any, to put your military career behind you.

3d: Did you ever decide while gone that you wished to return?

3e: Why did you intend on never returning to the military?

4a: When did you return to UNIT?

4b: Did you have contact with any military or civilian authorities while you were absent from your UNIT? If so, explain who, when how and what was discussed.

4c: Did anything or anyone prevent you from returning to your UNIT?

4d: Did you have any physical or mental infirmity that prevented you from returning to your UNIT?

4e: Could you have returned to your UNIT earlier if you had wanted to?

5a: Do you agree that your absence was in a time of war?

6a: Were you later apprehended? When? Where?

6b: Who apprehended you?

6c: Why were you apprehended?

6d: Did the authorities learn of your unauthorized absence status from someone other than you?

6e: How did they learn of it?

6f: Were you in the process of turning yourself into military or civilian authorities at the time?

6g: Did you say or do anything at the time that you intended to turn yourself in?

6h: Did you contact military or civilian authorities to tell them where they could locate you and that you wanted to turn yourself in?
