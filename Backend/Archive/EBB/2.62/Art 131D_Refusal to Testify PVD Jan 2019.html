Article 131D – WRONGFUL REFUSAL TO TESTIFY

Elements:

(1) That you were in the presence of (a) (an) ((general) (special) (summary) court-martial) (board of officers) (military commission) (court of inquiry) (officer conducting a preliminary hearing under Article 32, Uniform Code of Military Justice) (officer taking a deposition), or (_________) (of) (for) the United States, of which (state the name and rank of the presiding official) was the (military judge) (president) (chairman) (__________);

(2) That (state the name and rank of the presiding official)

(a) directed you to qualify as a witness, or

(b) directed you , after (he) (she) had qualified as a witness, to answer the following question(s) as a witness, namely: (set forth the question(s) alleged);

(3) That (state the time and place alleged), you refused to (qualify as a witness) (answer such questions); and

(4) That the refusal was wrongful.

Definitions:

To “qualify as a witness” means for the witness to declare that he/she will testify truthfully.

A witness may not be forced against (his) (her) will to answer any question if the answer would tend to incriminate (him) (her).

“Incriminate” means to put one in danger of a criminal prosecution or operate against one’s legal rights.

An accused as a witness before a military tribunal has the right to refuse to answer any question that is not material to the issues being determined by that tribunal and which would tend to degrade (him) (her).

“Material” means important to the issue or matter of inquiry.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: At the time and place alleged, were you in the presence of (a) (an) ((general) (special) court-martial) (duly appointed board of officers) (officer conducting a preliminary hearing pursuant to Article 32, Uniform Code of Military Justice) (officer taking a deposition), or (_________) (of) (for) the United States, of which (state the name and rank of the presiding official) was the (military judge) (president) (chairman) __________)?

1b: What was the nature of this proceeding?

1c: Why was the proceeding being conducted?

2a: Who was PRESIDING OFFICIAL?

2b: Did you know PRESIDING OFFICIAL was acting in an official capacity?

2c: Did PRESIDING OFFICIAL direct you to qualify as a witness?

2d: Or did he/she direct you, after you had qualified as a witness, to answer (a) (certain) question(s)?

2e: What (was) (were) the question(s) PRESIDING OFFICIAL directed you to answer?

3a: On DATE at PLACE, did you refuse to (qualify as a witness) (answer such question(s))?

3b: Why?

4a: Did you understand when you refused that your refusal was wrongful?

4b: Could you have qualified or answered the questions if you had wanted to?

4c: Did anyone force or coerce you into refusing?

4d: Do you believe you had any legal justification or authorization for your refusal?

4e: Do you agree that your conduct was wrongful?
