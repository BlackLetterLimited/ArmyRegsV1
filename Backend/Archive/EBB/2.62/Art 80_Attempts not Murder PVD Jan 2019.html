Article 80 – ATTEMPTS OTHER THAN MURDER/MANSLAUGHTER

Elements:

(1) That, (state the time and place alleged), you did (a) certain overt act(s), that is: (state the act(s) alleged or raised by the evidence);

(2) That the act(s) (was) (were) done with specific intent to commit the offense of (state the alleged attempted offense);

(3) That the act(s) amounted to more than mere preparation, that is, (it was) (they were) a substantial step and a direct movement toward the commission of the intended offense; and

(4) That such act(s) apparently tended to bring about the commission of the offense of (state the alleged attempted offense), (that is, the act(s) apparently would have resulted in the actual commission of the offense of (state the alleged attempted offense) except for (a circumstance unknown to you) (an unexpected intervening circumstance) (__________) which prevented completion of that offense.

Definitions:

Preparation consists of devising or arranging the means necessary for the commission of the attempted offense. The acts which you did must amount to a substantial step and a direct movement toward the commission of the intended offense of OFFENSE. A substantial step is one that is strongly corroborative of your criminal intent and is indicative of your resolve to commit the offense. Whether or not the OFFENSE actually occurred or was completed, you must have intended every element of OFFENSE.
