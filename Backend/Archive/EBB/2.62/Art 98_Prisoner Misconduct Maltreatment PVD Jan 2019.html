Article 98 – PRISONER MISCONDUCT – MALTREATMENT OF PRISONER

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you maltreated a prisoner held by the enemy by (state the manner of maltreatment alleged);

(2) That the act occurred while you were in the hands of the enemy in time of war;

(3) That you held a position of authority over the person maltreated; and

(4) That the act was without justifiable cause.

Definitions:

“Enemy” includes organized forces of the enemy in time of war, any hostile body that our forces may be opposing, such as a rebellious mob or a band of renegades, and includes civilians as well as members of military organizations. “Enemy” is not restricted to the enemy government or its armed forces. All the citizens of one belligerent are enemies of the government and the citizens of the other.

“Maltreated” means the infliction of real abuse, although not necessarily physical abuse. It must be without justifiable cause. (To assault) (To strike) (To subject to improper punishment) (or) (To deprive of benefits) could constitute maltreatment. (Abuse of an inferior by derogatory words may cause mental anguish and amount to maltreatment.)

If you occupy a position of authority over the prisoner, the source of that authority is not material. The authority may arise from the military rank of the accused or -- despite service regulations or customs to the contrary -- designation by the captor authorities or voluntary selection or election of the accused by other prisoners for their own self-government.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On (date) at (place) did you maltreat a prisoner held by the enemy?

1b: Describe the maltreatment.

2a: Did this act occur while you were in the hands of the enemy during a time of war?

2d: Was the other prisoner also in the hands of the enemy?

2e: Describe how you know that the act(s) was committed during a time of war?

3a: Did you hold a position of authority over the prisoner?

3b: Describe your position of authority over the prisoner.

4a: Did you have any justifiable cause for the maltreatment?

4b: Were you forced or coerced into the maltreatment?

4c: Could you have avoided maltreating the prisoner if you had wanted to?
