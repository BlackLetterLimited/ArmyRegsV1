Article 114 – FIREARM-WILLFUL DISCHARGE UNDER CIRCUMSTANCES TO ENDANGER HUMAN LIFE

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you discharged a firearm, to wit: (a service rifle) (__________);

(2) That such discharge was willful and wrongful; and

(3) That this discharge was under circumstances such as to endanger human life.

Definitions:

An act is done “willfully” if it is done intentionally or on purpose.

“Under circumstances such as to endanger human life” means that there must be a reasonable possibility of harm to human beings. Proof that human life was actually endangered is not required.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE at PLACE, did you discharge a firearm, specifically, (a service rifle) (__________)?

1b: Whose weapon was this?

1c: How did you come to possess it?

1d: Was the weapon loaded?

1e: Did you know it was loaded?

1f: Why did you discharge the firearm?

2a: Did you intentionally discharge the firearm?

2b: Explain what you did.

2c: How did you actually discharge the firearm?

2d: Did you believe you had a justification or excuse?

2e: Were you ordered by a superior to behave (or respond) this way?

2f: Did some circumstance outside your control force you to do this?

2g: Did anyone force or coerce you into this?

2h: Was it a freely made decision on your part?

2i: Could you have avoided discharging the firearm if you had wanted to?

2j: Did you understand your conduct was wrongful?

2k: Do you believe you had any legal authorization or justification for discharging the firearm in this manner?

3a: Do you believe that discharging the firearm in this manner endangered human life?

3b: Do you believe that there was a reasonable possibility of resulting harm to human beings?

3c: Explain why you believe that.
