2-7-29. RANDOMIZATION OF the COURT-MARTIAL PANEL ON OR AFTER 28 DECEMBER 2023

NOTE: For courts-martial arraigned on or after 28 December 2023, the judge shall randomize the members under revised RCM 911 (2024 MCM) and Annex 2 of EO 14103. Randomization may be appropriate at other times based on the interpretation of (1) the savings clause of EO 14103 (“Nothing in Annex 2 shall be construed to invalidate any nonjudicial punishment proceeding, restraint, preliminary hearing, referral of charges, trial in which arraignment occurred, or other action begun prior to the effective date established by section 539C of the NDAA FY 2022, and any such nonjudicial punishment proceeding, restraint, preliminary hearing, referral of charges, trial in which arraignment occurred, or other action may proceed in the same manner and with the same effect as if the Annex 2 amendments had not been prescribed.”); and (2) the law applicable to savings clauses (e.g., US v. Nichols, 6 CMR 27 (CMA 1952)).

When randomization is appropriate, the “military judge, or a designee thereof, shall randomly assign numbers to the members detailed by the convening authority.” See RCM 911 (2024 MCM) and Annex 2 of EO 14103. RCM 911 also provides that the judge must accomplish this randomization “prior to assembly” and “at an open session of the court-martial.” Therefore, a judge may choose to conduct this randomization during the initial session through arraignment. If the judge conducts the randomization during the initial session through arraignment, then the judge may use Paragraph 2-1-8 as a guide on how to accomplish the randomization during the initial session through arraignment. The judge may instead choose to wait until a later Article 39(a) session to accomplish this randomization. If the judge conducts the randomization during a later session, then the following script provides guidance for accomplishing the randomization required by RCM 911.

MJ: This Article 39(a) session is called to order.

TC: (Account for parties) (All parties present as before are again present(, with the following exception(s): __________)).

MJ: We will now randomize the members as required by RCM 911.

NOTE: Some courtrooms have the technological capability to conduct the entire randomization, including printing and marking the results, without recessing. Some courtrooms do not have that technological capability. Therefore, one of the two paragraphs below may be used, as appropriate.

MJ: [If the randomization will occur entirely on-the-record, without recessing:] I direct the Court Reporter, as my designee, to randomize the members using the randomization generator on the Army JAG Corps’ website. Counsel may approach and observe the Court Reporter perform this task, if you wish. Court Reporter, please print a copy of the results and mark them as the next Appellate Exhibit. [Court reporter does as directed.] The Court Reporter did as I directed. I have Appellate Exhibit ___ which reflects the result of the random assignment of numbers to the members. Does any party have an objection to the manner in which numbers were assigned to the members?

TC/DC: (Respond.)

MJ: [If the randomization will occur during a recess:] In a moment, we will recess to allow the Court Reporter to randomly assign numbers to the members as my designee. The Court Reporter will use the randomization generator on the Army JAG Corps’ website. Each party may be present and observe the Court Reporter perform this task. Court Reporter, please print a copy of the results and mark them as the next Appellate Exhibit. Please also allow the trial counsel and defense counsel to observe you when you randomly assign the numbers, if they desire. [Recess. Court reporter does as directed during the recess.] This Article 39(a) session is called to order. All parties are present, except the members. During the last recess, the Court Reporter did as I directed. Appellate Exhibit __ reflects the result of the random assignment of numbers to the members. Does any party have an objection to the manner in which numbers were assigned to the members?

TC/DC: (Respond.)

NOTE: After randomly assigning numbers to the members, RCM 911 (2024 MCM) also requires the judge to “determine, after accounting for any excusals by the convening authority or designee, how many members detailed by the convening authority must be present at the initial session for which members are required.” See RCM 911(b). What follows is guidance on how to direct how many members must be present at the initial session requiring the members’ presence. The numbers included below are merely examples. Unlike the randomization process above, RCM 911(b) does not require this determination to be accomplished in an open session. The judge may elect to make this determination closer to trial.

[MJ: In accordance with RCM 911(b), the Court directs that, excluding any excusals of members granted by the convening authority or the convening authority’s designee, the first (20) (18) (16) (14) (___) members on Appellate Exhibit ___ are required to be present at the initial session for which members are required. Does any party have an objection to the Court’s direction regarding the number of members that must be present at the initial session requiring the members’ presence?

TC/DC: (Respond.)]

NOTE: In all cases, continue below.

MJ: Are there other matters to take up before this court recesses?

TC/DC: (Respond.)

MJ: This court is in recess.
