Article 103A – ATTEMPTED ESPIONAGE

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you committed a certain overt act, to wit: ________________________________;

(2) That the act was done with intent to commit espionage against the United States;

(3) That the act amounted to more than mere preparation; and

(4) That the act apparently tended to bring about the offense of espionage.

Definitions:

“Intent or reason to believe” that the information “is to be used to the injury of the United States or to the advantage of a foreign nation” means that you acted in bad faith and without lawful authority with respect to information that is not lawfully accessible to the public.

“Instrument, appliance, or information relating to the national defense” includes the full range of modern technology (and matter that may be developed in the future) (including chemical or biological agents) (computer technology), and other matter related to the national defense.

“Foreign country” includes those countries that have and have not been recognized by the United States.

Preparation consists of devising or arranging the means necessary for the commission of the attempted offense. The acts which you did must amount to a substantial step and a direct movement toward the commission of the intended offense of Espionage. A substantial step is one that is strongly corroborative of your criminal intent and is indicative of your resolve to commit the offense. Whether or not the Espionage actually occurred or was completed, you must have intended every element of Espionage.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE at PLACE, did you commit the act of (_____________) as alleged in the specification?

1b: Describe what you did.

2a: When you committed ACT, did you specifically intend to commit the offense of espionage?

2b: Did you intend to communicate/deliver/transmit a document/writing/code book/signal book/sketch/photograph/photographic negative/ blueprint/plan/map/model/note/instrument/appliance/disk/information relating to the national defense to someone?

2c: To whom did you intend to provide this information?

2d: Did you know the individual? How?

2e: Was the information that you intended to provide information relating to national defense?

2f: Did you intend to communicate/delivere/transmit the information to a foreign government or to any faction or party or military or naval force within a foreign country?

2g: Was the individual to whom you intended to provide the information a representative/officer/agent/employee/subject/citizen of a foreign government? Which one?

2h: How did you know this?

2i: How did you intend to provide the information to him/her/them?

2j: Did you provide intend or believe that such matter would be used to the injury of the United States or to the advantage of a foreign nation? Why?

2k: Were you receiving or did you expect to receive money or favors in return for providing this information?

3a: Did ACT amount to more than mere preparation?

3b: Was ACT clearly a substantial step made directly towards the commission of Espionage?

3c: How so?

4a: Do you believe and admit that ACT tended to bring about the offense of Espionage?

4b: Would your attempt have been successful, that is, would Espionage have occurred, but for the fact that (a circumstance unknown to you) (an unexpected intervening circumstance) prevented completion of the offense?

4c: Describe what that circumstance was.

4d: Did anyone force or coerce you into attempting to commit Espionage?

4e: Could you have avoided attempting to commit espionage if you had wanted to?

4f: Was the choice to attempt to commit espionage a freely made decision on your part?

4g: Did you have any legal justification or excuse for your conduct?
