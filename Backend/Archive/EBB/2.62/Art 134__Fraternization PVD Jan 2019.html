Article 134 – FRATERNIZATION

Elements:

(1) That, on (state the date alleged), you were a (commissioned) (warrant) officer;

(2) That (state the time and place alleged), you fraternized on terms of military equality with (state the name(s) of the enlisted member(s) alleged) by (state the manner in which the fraternization is alleged to have occurred);

(3) That you then knew (state the name(s) of the enlisted member(s) alleged) to be (an) enlisted member(s);

(4) That such fraternization violated the custom of the (Naval Service of the United States) (United States Army) (United States Air Force) (United States Coast Guard) that officers shall not fraternize with enlisted members on terms of military equality; and

(5) That, under the circumstances, the conduct of you were (to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces) (and) (of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces).

Definitions:

Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline is conduct that causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.

Service discrediting conduct is conduct that tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.

Not all contact or association between (commissioned) (warrant) officers and enlisted persons is an offense. Whether the contact or association in question is an offense depends on the surrounding circumstances. Factors considered include whether the conduct has compromised the chain of command, resulted in the appearance of partiality, or otherwise undermined good order, discipline, authority, or morale. The facts and circumstances must be such as to lead a reasonable person experienced in the problems of military leadership to conclude that good order and discipline in the armed forces have been prejudiced by the tendency of your conduct to compromise the respect of enlisted persons for the professionalism, integrity, and obligations of a (commissioned) (warrant) officer.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE were you a (commissioned) (warrant) officer?

1b: What was your grade?

1c: How long had you held that grade?

2a: On DATE at PLACE, did you fraternize with ENLISTED MEMBER(S)?

2b: Describe what you did that you believe constitutes fraternization.

2c: Did your behavior with ENLISTED MEMBER(S) occur on terms or military equality?

2d: How so?

2e: Do you believe that your conduct has compromised the chain of command, resulted in the appearance of partiality, or otherwise undermined good order, discipline, authority, or morale?

2f: How so?

2g: Were you aware of what you were doing at the time?

3a: Who was/were ENLISTED MEMBER(S)?

3b: On or about DATE did you know/were you acquainted with ENLISTED MEMBER(S)?

3c: How so?

3d: Was/were ENLISTED MEMBER(S) enlisted members of the armed forces at that time?

3e: How did you know (been told, seen in uniform, supervised, etc.)?

3f: What was your professional/military relationship with ENLISTED MEMBER(S)?

3g: Were you in his/her/their chain of command?

3h: Were you superior in grade to him/her/them?

3i: Were you in a supervisory relationship to him/her/them?

3j: Did you write or provide input for his/her/their performance evaluations?

3k: Were you in a supervisory position/ relationship to any of his/her/their peers?

3l: Were you in a supervisory position/relationship to any of his/her/their military/civilian supervisors?

4a: Were you aware on DATE that such fraternization violated the custom of the service?

4b: Did you know on DATE that (warrant) (commissioned) officers shall not fraternize with enlisted members on terms of military equality;

4c: How did you know this?

4d: Had you reviewed Navy/Marine Corps regulations relating to fraternization?

4e: Did you know your conduct was wrongful?

4f: Did anyone or anything force you to engage in this course of action/conduct?

4g: Could you have avoided this conduct if you had wanted to?

4h: Was this a freely made decision on your part to engage in this conduct?

5a: Do you believe that your conduct was prejudicial to good order and discipline in the armed forces or was of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces?

5b: Which one or both?

5c: Explain why you believe this.
