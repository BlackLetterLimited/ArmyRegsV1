Article 118 – FELONY MURDER

Elements:

(1) That (state the name or description of the alleged victim) is dead;

(2) That his/her death resulted from the (act) (omission) of you in (state the act or failure to act alleged) at (state the time and place alleged);

(3) That the killing of (state the name or description of the alleged victim) by you were unlawful; and

(4) That, at the time of the killing, you were participating in the (attempted) perpetration of (burglary) (rape) (rape of a child) (sexual assault) (sexual assault of a child) (aggravated sexual contact) (sexual abuse of a child) (robbery) (aggravated arson).

Definitions:

The killing of a human being is unlawful when done without legal justification or excuse.

In order to be guilty of the offense of felony murder, you must have been engaged in the perpetration or attempted perpetration of a specific felony offense. Additionally, there must be a causal connection between the commission of the (burglary) (rape) (rape of a child) (sexual assault) (sexual assault of a child) (aggravated sexual contact) (sexual abuse of a child) (robbery) (aggravated arson) and the act which caused the victim’s death.
