ELEMENTS

In specification __ of Charge __, you are charged with the offense of absence from your unit, organization, or place of duty, or more commonly, absent without leave or AWOL in violation of Article 86, UCMJ. By pleading guilty to this offense, you are admitting that the following elements are true and accurately describe what you did:

That (state the time and place alleged), you (went from or remained absent) from your (unit) (organization) (place of duty at which you were required to be), that is, (state name of unit, organization, or place of duty);

That the absence was without proper authority from someone who could give you leave; and

That you remained absent until (state the date of alleged termination of absence); and

If termination by apprehension is alleged: That your absence was terminated by apprehension.

Definitions

In considering those elements, you should use and understand the following definition:

If termination by apprehension is alleged, give the following instructions:

"Apprehension" means your return to military control was involuntary. It must be shown that neither you nor persons acting at your request initiated your return.

That you were apprehended by civilian authorities, for a civilian violation, and were thereafter turned over to military control by the civilian authorities, does not necessarily indicate that your return was involuntary. Such return may be deemed involuntary if, after you were apprehended, such civilian authorities learned of your military status from someone other than you or persons acting at your request.

In addition, your return may be involuntary if, after being apprehended by civilian authorities, you disclosed your identity as a result of a desire to avoid trial, prosecution, punishment, or other criminal action at the hands of such civilian authorities. However, if you disclosed your identity to the civilian authorities because of your desire to return to military control, your return should not be deemed involuntary or by apprehension.

Arrest by civilian authorities does not, in the absence of special circumstances, terminate your unauthorized absence by apprehension where the record does not show such apprehension to have been connected with or done on behalf of the military authorities. Thus, in the absence of special circumstances, mere apprehension by civilian authorities does not sustain the government's burden of showing that your return to military control was involuntary.

Why?

Control by civilian authorities ordinarily is not a defense, but see MCM Part V ¶ 10c(5).

Authorized leave can be in-part be a defense. “When, [], a person on authorized leave, without fault, is unable to return at the expiration thereof, that person has not committed the offense of absence without leave. MCM Part V ¶ 10c(6)

Generally, surrender or apprehension to military or civilian authorities terminates the period of absence. If the member is apprehended by civilian authorities for “other reasons” the period of absence does not end until the member is delivered to military authorities. MCM Part V ¶ 10c(10)(a - e).
