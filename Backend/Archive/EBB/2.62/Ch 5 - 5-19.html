5–19. SELF-DEFENSE–GENERALLY AND USING THESE INSTRUCTIONS

The judge must instruct on self-defense, sua sponte, when the issue has been raised by some evidence. The first five instructions (Instructions 5-19-1 through 5-19-5) contain basic self-defense instructions that apply in five distinct situations:

a. Homicide is charged or the assault in issue involves the use of force likely to produce death or grievous bodily harm (Instruction 5-19-1).

b. Ordinary assault or battery not involving deadly force is in issue (Instruction 5-19-2).

c. Assault or assault consummated by a battery is in issue as a lesser included offense to an offense involving the use of force or likely to produce death or grievous bodily harm (Instruction 5-19-3).

d. Homicide is charged and there is evidence that the death was an unintended result of the application of less than deadly force (Instruction 5-19-4).

e. The use of force to deter (Instruction 5-19-5).

Instruction 5-19-6 contains instructions on issues that occasionally arise in connection with self-defense (e.g., opportunity to withdraw; mutual combat).
