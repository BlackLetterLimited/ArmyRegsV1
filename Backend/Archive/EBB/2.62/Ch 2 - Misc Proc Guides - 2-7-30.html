2-7-30. INITIAL SESSION THROUGH ARRAIGNMENT FOR A SPECIAL COURT-MARTIAL CONSISTING OF MILITARY JUDGE ALONE PURSUANT TO ARTICLE 16(C)(2)(A), UCMJ

NOTE: The following script is to only be used during the initial session through arraignment for Special Courts-Martial Consisting of Military Judge Alone pursuant to Article 16(c)(2)(A), UCMJ. Do NOT use this script for a General or Special Court-Martial. For the script for the initial session through arraignment for a General or Special Court-Martial, see paragraphs 2-1 through 2-1-7.

MJ: Please be seated. This Article 39(a) session is called to order.

TC: This court-martial was convened on ___________, pursuant to Article 16(c)(2)(A), UCMJ.

NOTE: A record of every pre-referral proceeding conducted pursuant to Article 30a, UCMJ shall be prepared and such record shall be included in the record of trial. See RCM 309(e). Trial counsel should account for any such proceedings, or lack thereof, as follows:

TC: [No Article 30a proceedings were held in connection with this case]. [Judge ( name ) ((ruled on an Article 30a matter) (presided over an Article 30a proceeding)) in connection with this case on ( date(s) ); specifically: ((an application for a pre-referral (investigative subpoena) (warrant) (order)) (a request for relief from subpoena or other process) (a matter referred by an appellate court) (an application to designate a person to assume the rights of an alleged victim under Article 6b) (an application for review of pretrial confinement) (an RCM 706 inquiry) (an RCM 909 capacity hearing) (A request for individual military counsel) (a victim’s petition for relief) (___________)) and the record of that proceeding will be added to the record of this court-martial].

NOTE: In peacetime, if less than three days (for a Special Court-Martial) have elapsed from the date of service of the referred charge(s) on the accused, the judge must inquire. If the accused objects, the judge must grant a continuance. When computing the days, do not count the day of service or the day of the initial session. If a waiver must be obtained, see paragraph 2-7-1, WAIVER OF STATUTORY WAITING PERIOD.

TC: The charge(s) (has) (have) been properly referred to this court for trial and (was) (were) served on the accused on __________. The three day statutory waiting period has (not) expired.

TC: The prosecution is ready to proceed (with the arraignment) in the case of United States versus (Private) (___) __________. The accused and the following persons detailed to this court are present: __________, Military Judge (Military Magistrate); __________, Trial Counsel; and __________, Defense Counsel. (The following persons detailed to this court are absent: __________.)

TC: __________ has been detailed as the court reporter and (has been previously sworn) (will now be sworn).

TC: (I) (All members of the prosecution) have been detailed to this court-martial by __________. (I am) (All members of the prosecution are) qualified and certified under Article 27(b) and sworn under Article 42(a), Uniform Code of Military Justice. (I have not) (No member of the prosecution has) acted in any manner that might tend to disqualify (me) (us) in this court-martial. (Further, (I am) (__________ (is) (are)) qualified and certified as a special trial counsel under Article 24a(b)(1), UCMJ.)

NOTE: If any trial counsel needs to be sworn, the judge will provide the following oath: “Do you (swear) (affirm) that you will faithfully perform all the duties of (assistant) trial counsel in the case now in hearing (so help you God)?” TC: (Responds).

RIGHTS TO COUNSEL

MJ: __________, you have the right to be represented by your detailed military defense counsel, (insert counsel name(s)). Your detailed military defense counsel (is a) (are) lawyer(s), certified by The Judge Advocate General as qualified to act as your defense counsel (and (is a) (are) member(s) of the (Army’s Trial Defense Service) (Navy-Marine Corps’ Defense Service Organization) (Air Force’s Trial Defense Division) (Coast Guard’s Defense Services Division). Your detailed military defense counsel’s services are provided at no expense to you.

You also have the right to be represented by a military counsel of your own selection, provided that the counsel you request is reasonably available. If you were represented by military counsel of your own selection, then your detailed defense counsel would normally be excused. However, you could request that your detailed counsel continue to represent you, but your request would not have to be granted. Do you understand that?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: In addition to your military defense counsel, you have the right to be represented by a civilian counsel at no expense to the government. Civilian counsel may represent you along with your military defense counsel or you could excuse your military counsel and be represented only by your civilian counsel. Do you understand that?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: Do you have any questions about your rights to counsel?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: By whom do you wish to be represented?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: And by only (that) (those) counsel?

ACC: (Responds.)

NOTE: If the accused elects pro se representation, see paragraph 2-7-2, PRO SE REPRESENTATION. The judge must be aware of any possible conflict of interest by counsel, and if a conflict exists, the judge must obtain a waiver from the accused or order new counsel appointed for the accused. See paragraph 2-7-3, WAIVER OF CONFLICT-FREE COUNSEL.

MJ: Defense Counsel, please announce your detailing and qualifications.

DC: (I) (All detailed members of the defense) have been detailed to this court-martial by __________. (I am) (All detailed members of the defense are) qualified and certified under Article 27(b) and sworn under Article 42(a), Uniform Code of Military Justice. (I have not) (No member of the defense has) acted in any manner that might tend to disqualify (me) (us) in this court-martial.

NOTE: If any defense counsel needs to be sworn, the judge will provide the following oath: “Do you swear or affirm that you will faithfully perform all the duties of defense counsel in the case now in hearing (so help you God)?” DC: (Responds).

Civilian DC: I am an attorney and licensed to practice law in the state(s) of __________. I am a member in good standing of the (__________) bar(s). I have not acted in any manner which might tend to disqualify me in this court-martial.

NOTE: In all cases with a civilian defense counsel, the judge will provide the following oath: “Do you, __________, (swear) (affirm) that you will faithfully perform the duties of individual defense counsel in the case now in hearing (so help you God)?” CDC: (Responds).

FORUM RIGHTS

NOTE: If the charges contain any offenses alleged to have been committed prior to 1 January 2019, the judge must conduct the below colloquy with the accused to determine if the accused elects to be sentenced under the sentencing rules that became effective on 1 January 2019. To proceed with a special court-martial consisting of a military judge alone, the accused must agree to be sentenced under the sentencing rules that became effective on 1 January 2019. See Executive Order 13825, Section 10, and RCM 902A (2019 MCM). If all charges allege offenses committed on or after 1 January 2019, skip to the next NOTE.

MJ: Because you are charged with offenses allegedly occurring both before 1 January 2019 and on or after 1 January 2019, you may only be tried at a special court-martial consisting of a military judge alone pursuant to Article 16(c)(2)(A) if you elect to be sentenced under the sentencing rules that went into effect on 1 January 2019. If you make this election, and are later convicted of any offense, no matter when the offense occurred, you will be sentenced under the sentencing rules in effect on 1 January 2019. You must make this election prior to arraignment. Your election regarding the applicable sentencing rules is permanent. If you do not elect to be sentenced under the sentencing rules that went into effect on 1 January 2019, we will be unable to proceed with this trial at this forum. Do you understand?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: Have you discussed this with your defense counsel?

ACC: (Responds)

MJ: Did your defense counsel fully explain the choices you have?

ACC: (Responds)

MJ: Do you have any questions about your right to elect the applicable sentencing rules?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: Do you agree to be sentenced under the sentencing rules that became effective on 1 January 2019?

ACC: (Responds)

NOTE: In all cases, continue below.

MJ: ___________, because this case has been referred to a special court-martial consisting of a military judge alone pursuant to Article 16(c)(2)(A) UCMJ, the military judge will decide whether you are guilty, and if found guilty, the military judge will determine your sentence. Do you understand that?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: You have the right to object to the trial of any specification by a special court-martial consisting of a military judge alone if: (1) the maximum authorized confinement for the offense alleged in the specification would be greater than two years if the offense were tried by a general court-martial, with the exception of a specification alleging wrongful use or possession of a controlled substance in violation of Article 112a, UCMJ or an attempt thereof under Article 80, UCMJ; or (2) the specification alleges an offense for which sex offender notification would be required under regulations issued by the Secretary of Defense.

MJ: If you object to trial by special court-martial consisting of a military judge alone for (the) (any) specification, the convening authority will have the option of referring (that) (those) specification(s), (along with the other specifications on the charge sheet), to a general or special court-martial, at which the maximum punishment authorized would be increased.

TC/DC/ACC: (Respond.)

((IF NECESSARY:) MJ: ___________, do you object to trial by special court-martial consisting of a military judge alone for (the) (any) specification?

ACC: (Responds.))

NOTE: If the accused objects to the trial of any specification by special court-martial consisting of a military judge alone, recess the proceedings and allow trial counsel to refer the matter to the convening authority. If the accused does not object, announce that the court is assembled, as stated below, and then proceed to paragraph 2-1-6, ARRAIGNMENT. However, before announcing assembly of the court, the judge should consider the impact of doing so on the ability to later change the judge pursuant to RCM 505(e).

MJ: The court is assembled.

MJ: __________, in a few moments, I will ask your defense counsel to enter your plea. Certain motions must be made before your plea is entered or else you waive the right to make them. Therefore, motions to dismiss or to grant other appropriate relief should be made at this time.

MJ: Defense, do you have any motions to file before entry of plea?

DC: The defense (has (no) (the following) motions) (requests to defer motions).

NOTE: If the defense files motions, the judge should address the motions before receiving the plea OR indicate that motions will be addressed after the arraignment or at a later session. If the judge grants a request to defer the filing of motions, the judge should indicate a deadline for the filing of motions. The judge could state that the request to defer is granted in accordance with a published or to-be published pretrial scheduling order.

MJ: __________, what just happened is called an arraignment. An arraignment has certain legal consequences, one of which I’d like to explain to you now. Under ordinary circumstances, you have the right to be present at every session and stage of your trial. However, if you are voluntarily absent at any point in this trial going forward, you may forfeit the right to be present. Future sessions, the trial, and sentencing, if necessary, could go forward even if you were not present. Do you understand that?
