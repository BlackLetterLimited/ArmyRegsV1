ELEMENTS

In specification __ of Charge __, you are charged with the offense of Communicating a Threat, in violation of Article 134, UCMJ. By pleading guilty to this offense, you are admitting that the following elements are true and accurately describe what you did:

That (time and place alleged), you communicated certain language, to wit: state the language alleged, or words to that effect, expressing a present determination or intent to wrongfully injure the person, property, or reputation of another person, presently or in the future;

That the communication was made known to PERSON (or a third person);

That the communication was wrongful; and

That, under the circumstances, your conduct was to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces (and) was of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces.

Definitions

In considering those elements, you should use and understand the following definitions:

The language used must, under the circumstances, be such that a reasonable person would understand it as expressing a present determination or intention to wrongfully injure another immediately or in the future. However, it is not necessary that you actually intended to do the injury threatened.

A communication is "wrongful" if you transmitted it for the purpose of issuing a threat, transmitted it with the knowledge that it would be viewed as a threat, or acted recklessly with regard to whether it would be viewed as a threat. "Reckless" means conduct that exhibits a culpable disregard of foreseeable consequences to others from the act or omission involved.

A communication is not "wrongful" if it is made in jest or as idle banter or for an innocent or legitimate purpose.

("Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline" is conduct which causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.)

("Service discrediting conduct" is conduct which tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.)

What were the circumstances?

Do you admit that in hearing the statement, a reasonable person would understand your statement as expressing a present determination or intent to wrongfully injure PERSON immediately or in the future?

Why do you believe that to be the case?

Did you say (write) it for the purpose of issuing a threat?

If not: Did you act recklessly with regard to whether it would be viewed as a threat? Explain why.

Did you intend the language in question as a joke? Were you serious?

Did you have any kind of innocent or legitimate purpose for making the threat?

Do you believe and admit that the communication was wrongful? Why?

Were your actions prejudicial to good order and discipline/service discrediting? Why?

Final Questions

POTENTIAL ISSUES raised?

Be wary of an ACC minimizing conduct or his intent … if it happens, re-read the pertinent portion of the instruction and then take a recess. If, after the recess, there continues to be doubt regarding whether the accused believes he is guilty, don’t accept the plea.
