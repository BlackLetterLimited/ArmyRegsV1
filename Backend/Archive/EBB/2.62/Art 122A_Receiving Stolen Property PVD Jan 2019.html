Article 122A – RECEIVING STOLEN PROPERTY

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you wrongfully (received) (bought) (concealed) (describe the property alleged);

(2) That (describe the property alleged) was of a value of about $_________ (or of some lesser value, in which case the finding should be in the lesser amount);

(3) That the property belonged to (state the name of the owner or other person alleged);

(4) That the property had been stolen by some person other than you ; and

(5) That, at the time you (received) (bought) (concealed) the property, (he) (she) knew it was stolen.

Definitions:

In order to be guilty of this offense, ITEM must have been stolen before you (received) (bought) (concealed) it.

“Stolen” means obtained through larceny. The elements of the offense of larceny are:

(a) That on or about some date, at a place, another person, not you, wrongfully took, withheld, or obtained the property from the possession of its rightful owner or someone with greater legal claim to it;

(b) That the property belonged to someone other than the person who wrongfully took, withheld, or obtained it;

(c) That the property was of some value;

(d) That the taking, withholding, or obtaining was with the intent: [permanently to (deprive) (defraud) of the use and benefit of the property] (or) [permanently to appropriate the property to his own use or the use of someone other than the owner.]

"Possession" means care, custody, management, or control.

"Owner" refers to any person (or entity) who, at the time of the (taking) (obtaining) (withholding) had a greater right to possession than you did, in the light of all conflicting interests. Property "belongs" to a person or (entity) having (title to the property) (a greater right to possession of the property than you) (or) (possession of the property).

"Took" means any actual or constructive moving, carrying, leading, riding, or driving away of another's personal property.

A (taking) (or) (withholding) is wrongful only if done without the consent of the owner and with a criminal state of mind.

An obtaining is wrongful only when it is accomplished by false pretenses with a criminal state of mind.

A criminal "false pretense" is any misrepresentation of fact by a person who knows it to be untrue, which is intended to deceive, which does in fact deceive, and which is the means by which value is obtained from another without compensation. The misrepresentation must be an important factor in causing the owner to part with the property. The misrepresentation does not, however, have to be the only cause of the obtaining.
