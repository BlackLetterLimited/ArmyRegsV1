Article 134 – PANDERING BY INDUCING, ENTICING, OR PROCURING ACT OF PROSTITUTION

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you induced, enticed, or procured (state the name of the person alleged) to engage in (a sexual act) (sexual acts) for hire and reward with persons to be directed to him/her by you ;

(2) That this inducing, enticing, or procuring by you were wrongful; and

(3) That, under the circumstances, the conduct of you were (to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces) (and) (of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces).

Definitions:

Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline is conduct, which causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.

Service discrediting conduct is conduct, which tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.

The word “induce” means to lead on, to influence, to prevail upon, to persuade, to bring about or to cause.

The word “entice” means to solicit, to persuade, to procure, to allure, to attract, to coax, or to seduce.

The word “procure” means to cause, to obtain, or to bring about.

The phrase “for hire and reward” means for the purpose of receiving money or other compensation.

“Wrongful” means without legal justification or excuse.

“Sexual act” means any penetration, however slight, of the vulva, anus, or mouth by the penis, another body part, or an object.
