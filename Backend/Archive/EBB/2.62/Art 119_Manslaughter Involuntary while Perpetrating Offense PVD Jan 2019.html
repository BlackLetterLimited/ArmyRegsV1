Article 119 – INVOLUNTARY MANSLAUGHTER-PERPETRATING CERTAIN OFFENSES

Elements:

(1) That (state the name or description of the alleged victim) is dead;

(2) That his/her death resulted from your act or omission in (state the act or omission alleged) at (state the time and place alleged);

(3) That the killing of (state the name or description of the alleged victim) by you was unlawful; and

(4) That, at the time of the killing, you were participating in the (attempted) commission of the offense of (assault) (battery) (false imprisonment) (maiming) (__________) directly affecting the person of (state the name or description of the alleged victim); [and].

[(5)] That (state the name or description of the alleged victim) was a child under the age of 16 years.

Definitions:

The killing of a human being is unlawful when done without legal justification or excuse.

To find that you were participating in the (attempted) commission of the offense of (assault) (battery) (false imprisonment) (maiming) (__________), you must be satisfied by legal and competent evidence beyond a reasonable doubt:

(LIST ELEMENTS)

In order for the killing, to be committed while you were (participating in) (attempting) (state the offense directly affecting the victim), your act must have caused the victim’s death and the (state the offense alleged to have been perpetrated or attempted) occurred at substantially the same time and place. Additionally, there must be a causal connection between the commission of the (attempted) offense of (state the offense alleged to have been perpetrated or attempted) and the act which caused the victim’s death.

(IF APPLICABLE) Knowledge that (state the name or description of the alleged victim) was under the age of 16 years is not an element of the offense.

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.
