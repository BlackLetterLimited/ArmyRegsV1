Article 119A – INJURING AN UNBORN CHILD

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you were engaged in the [(murder) (voluntary manslaughter) (involuntary manslaughter) (rape) (robbery) (maiming) (assault), of (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman)] [burning or setting afire , as arson, of (a dwelling inhabited by) (a structure or property (known to be occupied by) (belong to) (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman)];

(2) That (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman) was then a pregnant woman; and

(3) That you thereby caused bodily injury to the unborn child of (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman).

Definitions:

A “pregnant woman” is a female of any age who is carrying within her body an unborn child.

The term ‘unborn child’ means a child in utero (or a member of the species Homo Sapiens who is carried in the womb), at any stage of development, from conception to birth.

For the purpose of this offense, the term “bodily injury” to the unborn child is a cut, abrasion, bruise, burn, or disfigurement; physical pain; illness; impairment of the function of a bodily member, organ, or mental faculty; or any other injury to the body, no matter how temporary.

There is not requirement that you had an intent to injure the unborn child, or even had actual knowledge that (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman) was, at the time pregnant when the offense was committed.

For you to be guilty of this offense, your actions while engaging in the offense of (list offense) must have been the proximate cause of the injury to the unborn child.

(You may not be convicted of this offense for your conduct relating to an abortion for which the consent of (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman), or a person authorized by law to act on her behalf, had been obtained or for which the law implies such consent.) ((Likewise,) You may not be convicted of this offense for your conduct relating to any medical treatment of (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman) or her unborn child.)
