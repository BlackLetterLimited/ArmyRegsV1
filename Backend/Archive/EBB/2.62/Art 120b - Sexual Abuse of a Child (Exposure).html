POTENTIAL ISSUES

MISTAKE OF FACT AS TO AGE

“Mistake of fact as to age” means that you held, as a result of ignorance or mistake, an incorrect belief that (victim) was at least 16 years old. The ignorance or mistake must have existed in your mind and must have been reasonable under all the circumstances. To be reasonable the ignorance or mistake must have been based on information, or lack of it, which would indicate to a reasonable person that (victim) was at least 16 years old. Additionally, the ignorance or mistake cannot be based on the negligent failure to discover the true facts. Negligence is the absence of due care. Due care is what a reasonably careful person would do under the same or similar circumstances.

Do you understand the defense of mistake of fact as to age as I’ve explained it to you?

Did you have an incorrect belief that (victim) was at least 16 years old? Why?

Was that belief reasonable under all the circumstances? Why not?

Did you negligently fail to discover the true facts about (victim’s) true age? Explain why you believe this to be the case.

Do you believe the defense of mistake of fact as to age applies to your situation? Why not?

Do you agree and admit that your mistake as to (victim’s) age was unreasonable under all the circumstances.

Do you agree and admit that the defense of mistake of fact as to the age of (victim) does not apply in your case?

VOLUNTARY INTOXICATION RAISED

NOTE 5: Voluntary intoxication and mistake of fact as to age. If there is evidence of the accused’s voluntary intoxication, the following instruction is appropriate:

Your state of voluntary intoxication, at the time of the offense is not relevant to mistake of fact. A mistaken belief that (victim) was at least 16 years of age must be that which a reasonably careful, ordinary, prudent, sober adult would have had under the circumstances at the time of the offense. Voluntary intoxication does not permit what would be an unreasonable belief in the mind of a sober person to be considered reasonable because the person is intoxicated.

Do you understand this instruction?
