Article 92 – VIOLATING GENERAL ORDER OR REGULATION

Elements:

(1) That there was in existence a certain lawful general (order) (regulation) in the following terms: (state the date and specific source of the alleged general order or regulation and quote the order or regulation or the specific portion thereof);

(2) That you had a duty to obey such (order) (regulation); and

(3) That (state the time and place alleged), you (violated) (failed to obey) this lawful general (order) (regulation) by (here the military judge should enumerate the specific acts and any state of mind or intent alleged which must be established by the prosecution in order to constitute the violation of the order or regulation).

Definitions:

The term “General Order or Regulation”, to be lawful, must relate to specific military duty and be one which is authorized under the circumstances. A general order or regulation is lawful if it is reasonably necessary to safeguard and protect the morale, discipline, and usefulness of the members of a command and is directly connected with the maintenance of good order in the services. It is illegal if it is unrelated to military duty; its sole purpose is to accomplish some private end; it is arbitrary and unreasonable; or it is given for the sole purpose of increasing the penalty for an offense which it is expected you may commit.

General orders or regulations are those orders or regulations which are generally applicable to an armed force and which are properly published by the President, the Secretary of Defense or a military department such as the Secretary of the Navy.

General orders also include those orders which are generally applicable throughout the command of the officer issuing them or throughout a particular subdivision thereof and which are issued by (an officer having general court-martial jurisdiction) (or) (a general or flag officer in command) (or) (a commander superior to one of these.)

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Directing your attention to AE __, has your defense counsel reviewed with you ORDER?

1b: Who issued this order or regulation?

1c: Do you believe that he was authorized to issue ORDER?

1d: When was it implemented?

1e: Do you believe that the order or regulation was properly published?

1f: Do you believe and admit that it is a lawful order or regulation, as I have defined it for you?

1g: Do you believe and admit that it was a lawful order or regulation in effect on DATE?

1h: Do you believe that the order is reasonably necessary to safeguard and protect the morale, discipline, and usefulness of his command?

1i: Do you believe and admit that it applied to you on DATE?

1j: Do you understand the terms in RULE?

2a: Do you believe and admit that you had a duty to obey this order or regulation?

2b: Why?

3a: Do you believe and admit that you violated your duty under this order or regulation on DATE at PLACE?

3b: How did you violate this order or regulation on DATE?

3c: Do you believe and admit that failure to do so was wrongful?

3d: Why?

3e: Could you have followed this order if you had wanted to?

3f: Did anyone force or coerce you into violating this order?
