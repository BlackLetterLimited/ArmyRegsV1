Article 134 – DISLOYAL STATEMENTS

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you made a certain statement in the following terms: “(quote the statement alleged)”, or words to that effect;

(2) That the statement was made to another person;

(3) That the statement was disloyal to the United States;

(4) That the statement was made with the intent to:

(a) Promote (disloyalty) (disaffection) (disloyalty and disaffection) toward the United States by any member of the armed forces, or

(b) (Interfere with) (Impair) the (loyalty to the United States) (good order and discipline of any member of the armed forces of the United States);

(5) That, under the circumstances, the conduct of you were (to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces) (and) (of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces).

Definitions:

"Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline" is conduct which causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.

"Service discrediting conduct" is conduct which tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.

A statement is "made" by a person if it is spoken, uttered, written, published, printed, issued, put forth, or circulated by that person. A statement is made "in public" if it is made openly or known to many.

"Disloyalty" means not being true or faithful to the United States. Being unfaithful or untrue to a department of government or to any particular person is not necessarily disloyalty toward the United States.

"Disaffection" means disgust, discontent with, ill will or hostility toward the United States. Disgust or discontent with, ill will or hostility toward a department of government or to any particular person is not necessarily disaffection toward the United States. (Therefore, willful disobedience by the accused of (an) order(s) or urging by the accused that other members of the military willfully disobey (an) order(s) is not the equivalent of (disloyalty) (disaffection) (disloyalty and disaffection) toward the United States.)

Additionally, the mere disagreement with or objection to a policy of the government is not necessarily indicative of (disloyalty) (disaffection) (disloyalty and disaffection) to the United States.)

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Were you at PLACE on DATE?

1b: On DATE at place, did you make a disloyal statement?

1c: What was the statement?

2a: What kind of setting were you in when you made the statement?

2b: What were the circumstances under which the statement was made?

2c: Who was present?

2d: To whom did you make the statement?

2e: Did they hear it?

2f: How do you know that?

3a: Was the statement disloyal to the United States as I’ve defined the term?

3b: In what respects?

4a: Why did you make the disloyal statement?

4b: Did you intend to promote (disloyalty) (disaffection) (disloyalty and disaffection) toward the United States by any member of the armed forces?

4c: If so whom?

4d: How did you intend to promote (disloyalty) (disaffection) (disloyalty and disaffection) toward the United States by a member of the armed forces?

4e: Did you intend to interfere with or impair the loyalty to the United States or good order and discipline of any member of the armed forces of the United States?

4f: In what respects?

5a: Do you believe and admit that your conduct, under the circumstances, was prejudicial to good order and discipline in the armed forces or of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces?

5b: Which, or both?

5c: Explain why you believe that.

5d: Did you have any legal justification or excuse for your conduct?

5e: Did anyone force or coerce you to do this?

5f: Was it a freely made decision on your part to do this?

5g: Could you have avoided engaging in this misconduct if you had wanted?
