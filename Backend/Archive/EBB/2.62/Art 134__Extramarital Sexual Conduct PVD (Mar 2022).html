Article 134 – EXTRAMARITAL SEXUAL CONDUCT

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), ##the accused## wrongfully engaged in extramarital conduct, to wit: __________ with __________;

(2) That, at the time, the ##accused## knew that (he/she) (the other person) was married to someone else; and

(3) That, under the circumstances, the conduct of ##the accused## was (to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces) (and) (of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces).

Definitions:

“Extramarital conduct” includes any of the following acts engaged in by persons of the same or opposite sex:

Genital to genital sexual intercourse;

Oral to genital sexual intercourse;

Anal to genital sexual intercourse; and

Oral to anal sexual intercourse.

Not every act of extramarital sexual conduct constitutes an offense under the UCMJ. To constitute an offense under the UCMJ, the extramarital conduct must either be directly prejudicial to good order and discipline or service discrediting or both.

Extramarital conduct that is directly prejudicial to good order and discipline includes conduct that has an obvious, and measurably divisive effect on unit or organization discipline, morale, or cohesion or is clearly detrimental to the authority or stature of or respect toward a Servicemember, or both.

Extramarital conduct may be service discrediting even though the conduct is only indirectly or remotely prejudicial to good order and discipline.

“Discredit” means to injure the reputation of the armed forces and includes extramarital conduct that has a tendency, because of its open or notorious nature, to bring the Service into disrepute, make it subject to public ridicule, or lower it in public esteem.

Extramarital conduct that is private and discreet in nature may not be service discrediting by this standard. However, under the circumstances, it may be determined to be conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline.

When determining whether extramarital conduct is prejudicial to good order and discipline or is of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces, all relevant circumstances should be considered, including but not limited to the following factors:

-The accused’s marital status, military rank, grade, or position;

-The co-actor’s marital status, military rank, grade, and position, or relationship to the armed forces;

-The military status of the accused’s spouse or the spouse of the co-actor, or their relationship to the armed forces;

-The impact, if any, of the extramarital conduct on the ability of the accused, the co-actor, or the spouse of either to perform their duties in support of the armed forces;

-The misuse, if any, of Government time and resources to facilitate the commission of the conduct;

-Whether the conduct persisted despite counseling or orders to desist; the flagrancy of the conduct, such as whether any notoriety ensued; and whether the extramarital conduct was accompanied by other violations of the UCMJ;

-The negative impact of the conduct on the units or organizations of the accused, the co-actor, or the spouse of either of them, such as a detrimental effect on unit or organization morale, teamwork, and efficiency;

-Whether the accused’s or co-actor’s marriage was pending legal dissolution, which is defined as an action with a view towards divorce proceedings, such as the filing of a petition for divorce; and

-Whether the extramarital conduct involves an ongoing or recent relationship or is remote in time.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Did you, on or about DATE at PLACE, wrongfully engage in extramarital conduct with PERSON?

1b: Who was PERSON?

1c: How did you come to engage in extramarital conduct with PERSON?

1d: What was your relationship?

1e: Describe the extramarital contact you had with PERSON.

1f: Do you believe and admit that your conduct constitutes extramarital conduct as I’ve defined that term for you?

1g: Where did this take place?

1h: Was it in a public or private setting?

1i: Was anyone else present when this occurred?

1j: Did you have any legal justification or excuse?

1k: Was the decision to engage in extramarital conduct freely made on your part?

1l: Could you have avoided engaging in extramarital conduct if you had wanted?

1m: Do you believe and admit that you extramarital conduct was wrongful?

1n: Why?

2a: At the time, were you or PERSON married to another?

2b: Explain which or if both of you were married.

2c: What was the status of your (his)(her) marriage? Were either of you separated or pending legal dissolution?

2d: Were you aware that (she)(he) was married [if other party married] when you committed the act?

2e: How did you know (she)(he) was married?

2f: Did (she)(he) tell you?

2g: Was (she)(he) living with his/her spouse?

2h: If you were married, what date did you get married?

2i: Did you have a marriage certificate?

2j: Did you and SPOUSE hold yourselves out to be married?

3a: Under the circumstances, was your conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline in the armed forces or of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces?

3b: In what way?

3c: Did your extramarital conduct have an obvious, and measurably divisive effect on unit or organization discipline, morale, or cohesion? How so?

3d: Was your extramarital conduct clearly detrimental to the authority or stature of or respect toward a Servicemember? How so?

3e: Did your extramarital conduct injure the reputation of the armed forces? Meaning, did your extramarital conduct, because of its open or notorious nature, bring the Service into disrepute, make it subject to public ridicule or lower it in public esteem? How so?

3f: Did other people know of your conduct?

3g: Had anyone cautioned you about or directed you to stop your conduct with PERSON?

3h: Did your extramarital conduct impact the ability of you, your co-actor, or the spouse of either you or your co-actor to perform their duties in support of the armed forces? How so?

3i: Were Government time and/or resources misused to facilitate the commission of your extramarital conduct?

3j: What was the negative impact of your extramarital conduct on the units or organizations of you, your co-actor, or the spouse of either you or your co-actor? Did the units or organizations have a detrimental effect on morale, teamwork, and/or efficiency?
