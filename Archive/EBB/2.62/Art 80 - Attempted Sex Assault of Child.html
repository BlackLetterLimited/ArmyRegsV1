Elements

Please take a look at look at specification ___ of Charge __, which represents a violation of Article 80 of the Uniform Code of Military Justice. The elements of that offense, which is called attempted sexual assault of a child, are:

(1) That (stated the time and place alleged), you did certain acts, that is: (state the acts alleged or raised by the evidence);

NOTE: Attempt specs often don’t allege a specific overt act, but just allege that the accused attempted to sexually assault a child. The MJ can specify overt acts based on the facts of the case. See U.S. v. Allen, 2017 CCA Lexis 649 (AFCCA 2017). Consider using a stip of fact to identify overt acts.

(2) That the acts were done with the specific intent to commit the offense of sexual assault of a child;

(3) That the acts amounted to more than mere preparation, that is, they were a substantial step and a direct movement toward the commission of the intended offense.

(4) That such acts apparently tended to bring about the commission of the offense of sexual assault of a child, that is the acts apparently would have resulted in the actual commission of the offense of sexual assault of a child except for a circumstance unknown to you which prevented completion of that offense.

Definitions

In considering those elements, you should use and understand the following definitions:

“Preparation” consists of devising or arranging the means or measures necessary for the commission of the attempted offense. To be guilty of this offense, you must have gone beyond preparatory steps, and your act(s) must have amounted to a substantial step and a direct movement toward the commission of the intended offense. A substantial step is one that is strongly corroborative of your criminal intent and is indicative of your resolve to commit the offense.

Proof that the offense of sexual assault of a child actually occurred or was completed by you are not required. However, to be guilty of the offense of attempted sexual assault of a child, you must have intended every element of the offense of sexual assault of a child.

The elements of the attempted offense of sexual assault of a child are:

(1) That you committed a sexual act upon (state name of “victim”), by causing penetration, however slight, of (“victim’s”) (vulva) (anus) (mouth) by your (penis) (other body part) (object); (and)

(2) That at the time, (“victim”) had attained the age of 12 years, but had not attained the age of 16 years; (and)

NOTE 2: Penetration by object or body part other than penis. If the penetration occurs by something other than the Accused’s penis, add the following element.

(3) That you did so with an intent to (gratify your sexual desire) (humiliate/harass/or other intent alleged in the specification).

The “vulva” is the external genital organs of the female, including the entrance of the vagina and the labia majora and labia minora. “Labia” is the Latin and medically correct term for “lips.”

Under the circumstances it may have been impossible for you to have committed the offense of Sexual Abuse of a Child because (“victim”) was actually a law enforcement agent who was over the age of 16 years old. However, if the facts were as you believed them to be, and under those facts your conduct would constitute the offense of Sexual Abuse of a Child, you may plead guilty to attempted Sexual Abuse of a Child even though under the facts as they actually existed it was impossible for you to complete the offense of Sexual Abuse of a Child.

ENTRAPMENT

There is a potential defense of entrapment that I will explain to you so that you can determine whether you believe the defense applies in your case. If you believe this defense applies, you may not plead guilty to this offense and I am prohibited from accepting your plea.

Entrapment is a defense when government agents, or people cooperating with them, cause an innocent person to commit a crime which otherwise would not have occurred. You may not be convicted of the offense of attempted sexual assault of a child if you were entrapped.

An "innocent person" is one who is not predisposed or inclined to readily accept the opportunity furnished by someone else to commit the offense charged. It means that you must have committed the offense charged only because of inducements, enticements, or urging by representatives of the government. You should carefully note that if you have the predisposition, inclination, or intent to commit an offense or you are already involved in unlawful activity which the government is trying to uncover, the fact that an agent provides opportunities or facilities or assists in the commission does not amount to entrapment. You should be aware that law enforcement agents can engage in trickery and provide opportunities for criminals to commit an offense, but they cannot create criminal intent in otherwise innocent persons and thereby cause criminal conduct.

The defense of entrapment exists if the original suggestion and initiative to commit the offense originated with the government, not you, and that you were not predisposed or inclined to commit the offense of attempted sexual assault of a child. Thus, you must balance your resistance to temptation against the amount of government inducement. The focus is on your latent predisposition, if any, to commit the offense, which is triggered by the government inducement.
