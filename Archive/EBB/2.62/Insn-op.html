Prefatory Instructions on Findings

Mental Responsibility IS in Issue

Computer Benchbook Editor's Note:

This NOTE and all other NOTES in red will not be printed if you elect not to print them using the "Include Notes in Print-Outs" option in the Computer Benchbook.

These instructions are the AIR FORCE version based on the standard prefatory instructions on findings found in DA PAM 27-9. They presume the Military Judge will give ALL instructions less the procedural voting instructions followed by argument of counsel. Following counsels' argument, the procedural voting instructions will be given.

The entire script is available from the Computer Benchbook Main Menu.

MJ: The court is called to order. All parties are again present to include the court members.

(IF PROVIDING WRITTEN INSTRUCTIONS:)

MJ: Members of the court, I am about to give you instructions on the law you must follow when you close to deliberate and vote on the findings. I will provide you these instructions in writing, so I ask that you simply listen to them at this point and not attempt to copy them down verbatim, although you are certainly free to take notes as you see fit.

I will first instruct you on the law applicable to the charge(s) before you, then counsel will provide argument, and then finally I will provide you procedural instructions on conducting your deliberations and voting.

Shortly after you close for your deliberations, I will have the bailiff bring you the written instructions, after I have corrected any typographical errors I might find as I read them to you. Does any member have any questions before we begin?

MBRS: (Respond.)

NOTE: RCM 920(b) provides that instructions on findings shall be given before or after arguments by counsel or at both times. What follows is the giving of preliminary instructions prior to argument with procedural instructions given after argument.

BEGIN WRITTEN INSTRUCTIONS IF USED.

MJ: Members of the court, when you close to deliberate and vote on the findings, each of you must resolve the ultimate question of whether the accused is guilty or not guilty based upon the evidence presented here in court and upon the instructions which I will give you. My duty is to instruct you on the law. Your duty is to determine the facts, apply the law to the facts, and determine the guilt or innocence of the accused. The law presumes the accused to be innocent of the charge(s) against (him) (her).

During the trial some of you took notes. You may take your notes with you into the deliberation room. However, your notes may not be read or shown to other court members.

I will advise you of the elements of each offense alleged.

Computer Benchbook Editor's Note:

Here instruct on the elements of the charged offenses, and Lesser Included Offenses raised by the evidence. The language used to introduce charged offenses and LIOs, as well as that language to compare LIOs with charged offenses and other LIOs, has been omitted as using the Computer automatically incorporates that language.

NOTE: After instructions on the elements of an offense alleged, the members of the court must be advised of all lesser included offenses raised by the evidence and within the scope of the pleadings. The members should be advised, in order of diminishing severity, of the elements of each lesser included offense, and its differences from the principal offense and other lesser offenses, if any. The members will not be instructed on lesser offenses that are barred by the statute of limitations unless the accused waives the bar.

OTHER APPROPRIATE INSTRUCTIONS NOTE: For other instructions which may be appropriate in a particular case, see Chapter 4, Confessions and Admissions, Chapter 5, Special and Other Defenses, Chapter 6, Mental Responsibility, Chapter 7, Evidentiary Instructions. Generally, instructions on credibility of witnesses (see Instruction 7-7) and circumstantial evidence (see Instruction 7-3) are typical in most cases and should be given prior to proceeding to the following instructions.
