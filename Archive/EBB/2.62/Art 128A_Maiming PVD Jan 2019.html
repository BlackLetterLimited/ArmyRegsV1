Article 128A – MAIMING

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you, inflicted upon (state the name of the alleged victim) a certain injury, namely: (state the injury alleged);

(2) That this injury (seriously disfigured the body of (state the name of the alleged victim)) (destroyed or disabled an organ or member of (state the name of the alleged victim)) (seriously diminished the physical vigor of (state the name of the alleged victim)) by injuring an organ or other part of his/her body; and

(3) That you inflicted this injury with an intent to cause some injury to the person of (state the name of the alleged victim).

Definitions:

Maiming requires a specific intent to injure generally but not a specific intent to maim. Thus, one commits the offense who intends only a slight injury, if in fact there is infliction of an injury of the type specified in this article.

The disfigurement, diminished physical vigor, or destruction or disablement of the body part must be a serious injury of a substantially permanent nature. Once the injury is inflicted, it does not matter that the victim may eventually recover the use of the body part, or that the disfigurement may be corrected medically. While a disfigurement does not have to mutilate an entire body part, it must cause visible bodily damage and significantly detract from the victim’s physical appearance.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Where were you on/at [STATE THE TIME ALLEGED]?

1b: At or near that time, did you inflict an injury upon (state the name of the alleged victim)?

1c: What injury did you inflict upon (state the name of the alleged victim)?

1d: How did you inflict that injury upon (state the name of the alleged victim)?

2a: What effect did that injury have on (state the name of the alleged victim)?

2b: Do you believe that this injury (select as appropriate based on the specification: (seriously disfigured the body of (state the name of the alleged victim)) (destroyed or disabled an organ or member of (state the name of the alleged victim)) (seriously diminished the physical vigor of (state the name of the alleged victim)))?

2c: How so?

2d: How do you know that?

2e: Have you seen any medical reports about the injuries inflicted upon (state the name of the alleged victim)?

2f: Do those reports confirm what you have just told me about the nature of those injuries?

3a: What was your intent when you inflicted this injury upon (state the name of the alleged victim)?

3b: Did you intend to cause some injury to the person of (state the name of the alleged victim)?

3c: What injury did you intend to cause?

4a: Do you admit that your actions were wrongful?

4b: Why were they wrongful?

4c: Were you acting in self-defense or in defense of someone else or some property?

4d: Did you have any legal justification or excuse for your actions?

4e: Could you have avoided inflicting injuries upon (state the name of the alleged victim) if you had wanted to?

4f: Was it a freely made decision on your part to inflict these injuries upon (state the name of the alleged victim)?

4g: Once again, do you agree then that, taken together, your actions satisfy the elements and definitions of this offense as I stated them for you earlier and that you are in fact guilty of the offense of maiming?
