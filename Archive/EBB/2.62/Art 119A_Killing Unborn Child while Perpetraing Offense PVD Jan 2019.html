Article 119A – KILLING AN UNBORN CHILD

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you were engaged in the [(murder) (voluntary manslaughter) (involuntary manslaughter) (rape) (robbery) (maiming) (assault), of (state name of the alleged pregnant woman)] [arson of (a dwelling inhabited by) (a structure or property (known to be occupied by) (belong to) (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman)];

(2) That (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman) was then a pregnant woman; and

(3) That your thereby caused the death of the unborn child of (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman).

Definitions:

A “pregnant woman” is a female of any age who is carrying within her body an unborn child.

The term “unborn child” means a child in utero (or a member of the species Homo Sapiens who is carried in the womb), at any stage of development, from conception to birth.

Proof that the you had an intent to injure or kill the unborn child, or even had actual knowledge that (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman) was pregnant at the time the offense was committed, is not required.

Your actions while engaging in that offense must be the proximate cause of the death of the unborn child.

You may not be convicted of this offense for your conduct relating to an abortion for which the consent of (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman), or a person authorized by law to act on her behalf, had been obtained or for which the law implies such consent. Likewise you may not be convicted of this offense for your conduct relating to any medical treatment of (state the name of the alleged pregnant woman) or her unborn child.)
