APPENDIX I: PROCEDURAL GUIDE FOR ARTICLE 30A SESSION

NOTE: Article 30a and its implementation in R.C.M. 309 refer to pre-referral “proceedings.” Not every Article 30a proceeding requires a hearing. If the military judge detailed to preside determines that a hearing is either necessary or appropriate, the following guide may be used.

MJ: Please be seated. This Article 30a session is called to order.

MJ: This pre-referral proceeding is authorized by Article 30a, UCMJ, Rule for Courts-Martial 309(b) [add the subparagraph that represents the subject matter of the hearing, for example: (6) (for requests to review the propriety of pretrial confinement) or (7) (for requests to order an inquiry into the mental capacity or mental responsibility of the accused)], and [insert Service regulation, e.g., Army Regulation 27-10, paragraph 5-18(a)], in the matter of [state the type of request and any case identifier, e.g., (a motion to review the propriety of pretrial confinement of SPC Billy Jones) (motion to order an inquiry into the mental capacity and/or mental responsibility of SPC Billy Jones)].

MJ: Government counsel, please announce your detailing and qualifications and the identity of the court reporter.

TC: (I) (All counsel for the Government) have been detailed to this proceeding by __________. (I am) (All counsel for the Government are) qualified and certified under Article 27(b) and sworn under Article 42(a), Uniform Code of Military Justice. (I have not) (No counsel has) acted in any manner that might tend to disqualify (me) (us) in this proceeding.

TC: __________ has been detailed reporter for this proceeding and (has been previously sworn) (will now be sworn).

(TC: “Do you (swear) (affirm) that you will faithfully perform the duties of reporter to this Article 30a, UCMJ, proceeding (, so help you God)?”)

RIGHTS TO COUNSEL, IF APPLICABLE

NOTE: This may be applicable in matters referred by an appellate court or during a pre-referral review of continued pretrial confinement.

MJ: __________, you have the right to be represented by (insert counsel name(s)), your detailed military defense counsel. Your detailed military counsel (is a) (are) lawyer(s), certified by The Judge Advocate General as qualified to act as your defense counsel (and (is a) (are) member(s) of the (Army’s Trial Defense Service) (Navy-Marine Corps’ Defense Service Organization) (Air Force’s Trial Defense Division) (Coast Guard’s Defense Services Division). Your detailed military defense counsel’s services are provided at no expense to you.

You also have the right to be represented by a military counsel of your own selection, provided that the counsel you request is reasonably available. If you were represented by military counsel of your own selection, then your detailed defense counsel would normally be excused. However, you could request that your detailed counsel continue to represent you, but your request would not have to be granted. Do you understand that?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: In addition to your military defense counsel, you have the right to be represented by a civilian counsel at no expense to the government. Civilian counsel may represent you along with your military defense counsel or you could excuse your military counsel and be represented only by your civilian counsel. Do you understand that?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: Do you have any questions about your rights to counsel?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: By whom do you wish to be represented?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: And by only (that) (those) counsel?

ACC: (Responds.)

NOTE: If the accused elects pro se representation, see paragraph 2-7-2, PRO SE REPRESENTATION. The judge must be aware of any possible conflict of interest by counsel, and if a conflict exists, the judge must obtain a waiver from the accused or order new counsel appointed for the accused. See paragraph 2-7-3, WAIVER OF CONFLICT-FREE COUNSEL.

MJ: Defense Counsel, please announce your detailing and qualifications.

DC: (I) (All detailed members of the defense) have been detailed to this court-martial by __________. (I am) (All detailed members of the defense are) qualified and certified under Article 27(b) and sworn under Article 42(a), Uniform Code of Military Justice. (I have not) (No member of the defense has) acted in any manner that might tend to disqualify (me) (us) in this court-martial.

NOTE: If any defense counsel needs to be sworn, the judge will provide the following oath: “Do you swear or affirm that you will faithfully perform all the duties of defense counsel in the case now in hearing (so help you God)?” DC: (Responds).

Civilian DC: I am an attorney and licensed to practice law in the state(s) of __________. I am a member in good standing of the (__________) bar(s). I have not acted in any manner which might tend to disqualify me in this court-martial.

NOTE: In all cases with a civilian defense counsel, the judge will provide the following oath:

MJ: Do you, __________, (swear) (affirm) that you will faithfully perform the duties of individual defense counsel in the case now in hearing (so help you God)?

CDC: (Responds).

MJ: I have been properly certified and sworn, and detailed (myself) (by __________) to this proceeding. I am not aware of any matter that might be a ground for challenge against me (__________). Does either side desire to question or challenge me?

TC/DC: (Respond.)

MJ: Counsel for both sides appear to have the requisite qualifications, and all personnel required to be sworn have been sworn.
