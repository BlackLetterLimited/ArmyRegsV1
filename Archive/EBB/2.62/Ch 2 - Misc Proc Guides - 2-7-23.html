2-7-23. ABSENT ACCUSED INSTRUCTION: PRELIMINARY FINDINGS

MJ: Under the law applicable to trials by court-martial, various circumstances may exist whereby a court-martial can proceed to findings and sentence, if appropriate, without the accused being present in the courtroom. I have determined that one or more of these circumstances exist in this case. You are not permitted to speculate as to why the accused is not present in court today and that you must not draw any inference adverse to the accused because the accused is not appearing personally before you. You may neither impute to the accused any wrongdoing generally, nor impute to the accused any inference of guilt with respect to the accused’s nonappearance here today. Further, should the accused be found guilty of any offense presently before this court, you must not consider the accused’s nonappearance before this court in any manner when you close to deliberate upon the sentence to be adjudged.

Will each member follow this instruction?

REFERENCES : See US v. Minter, 8 MJ 867 (NMCMR 1980); See also US v. Denney, 28 MJ 521 (ACMR 1989) (indicating that accused’s absence may be considered for rehabilitative potential); US v. Chapman, 20 MJ 717 (NMCMR 1985), aff’d, 23 MJ 226 (CMA 1986) (summary affirmance).
