Article 131B – OBSTRUCTING JUSTICE

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and date alleged), you wrongfully did (a) certain act(s), that is, (state the act(s) alleged);

(2) That you did so in the case of (himself) (herself) (__________) against whom you had reason to believe there were or would be criminal or disciplinary proceedings pending;

(3) That the act(s) (was) (were) done with the intent to influence, impede or otherwise obstruct the due administration of justice.

Definitions:

"Wrongfully" means without legal justification or excuse. You must have had the specific intent to (influence) (impede) the due administration of justice, but there need not have been an actual obstruction of justice.

The term "criminal proceedings" includes (lawful searches) (criminal investigations conducted by police or command authorities) (Article 15 non-judicial punishment proceedings) (Article 32 investigations) (courts-martial) (state and federal criminal trials) (____). It is not necessary that charges be pending or even that an investigation be underway. You need not have known that charges have been brought or proceedings begun; however, you must have had reason to believe there were or would be criminal proceedings against NAME or that some law enforcement official of the military would be investigating the case of NAME.

Asking that one not reveal or report that an offense occurred is not an obstruction of justice unless you knew, or had reason to believe, that there were or would be criminal proceedings pending and your acts were done with the intent to obstruct justice. If you advised a potential witness of his legal right to remain silent merely to inform the witness about possible self-incrimination, that would not amount to a specific intent to (impede) (influence) the due administration of justice. However, if this advice was given for a corrupt purpose, such as a desire to protect yourself or others from the prospective witness' possibly damaging statements, you had a corrupt motive and you had a specific intent to (impede) (influence) the due administration of justice.

[To constitute an obstruction of justice the acts alleged to be the obstruction must be separate and not part of the commission of another offense alleged to have been committed by you. When there is a (conspiracy) (plan) to commit an offense other than obstruction of justice itself, and the (conspiracy) (plan) contemplates that the parties will take affirmative actions to obstruct justice in relation to the offense(s) which (is) (are) the object of the conspiracy, obstruction of justice is not a separate offense. Committing an offense in such a way as to avoid detection does not amount to obstruction of justice.]

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: 1a. On DATE, at PLACE, did you do ACT?

1b: Did you have any legal justification or excuse for doing ACT?

1c: Were you authorized by anyone to do ACT?

1d: Did anyone force or coercing you into doing ACT?

1e: could you have avoided doing ACT if you had wanted to.

1f: Do you believe doing ACT on DATE at PLACE was wrongful?

2a: At the time, was the case of NAME the subject of a criminal investigation or proceeding?

2b: Who was conducting this investigation or proceeding?

2c: How did you know this?

2d: What did you think might happen as a result of this investigation?

3a: What was your purpose in doing ACT?

3b: Did you specifically intend to (influence) (impede) (or obstruct) the due administration of justice?
