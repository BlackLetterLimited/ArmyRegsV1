Article 94 – MUTINY BY CREATING VIOLENCE OR DISTURBANCE

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you created (violence) (a disturbance) by (state the manner alleged); and

(2) That you created this (violence) (disturbance) with intent to (usurp) (and) (override) lawful military authority.

Definitions:

(“Violence” means the exertion of physical force.) (“Disturbance” means the interruption of or interference with a state of peace or order.) (“Usurp” means to seize and to hold by force or without right.) (“Override” means to set aside or supersede.)

(This offense may be committed by (one person acting alone) (or) (more than one person).)

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On (date) at (place) did you create violence or a disturbance?

1b: Which one?

1c: How did you do so?

2a: Did you create this (violence) (disturbance) with the intent to usurp (and) (or) override lawful military authority?

2b: Did anyone or anything force or coerce you to create this violence or disturbance?

2c: Could you have avoided committing this offense if you had wanted to?
