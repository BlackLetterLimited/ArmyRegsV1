ELEMENTS

In specification __ of Charge __, you are charged with the offense of Obstruction of Justice, in violation of Article 134, UCMJ. By pleading guilty to this offense, you are admitting that the following elements are true and accurately describe what you did:

That on or about DATE, at or near PLACE, you wrongfully did (a) certain act(s), that is, (STATE ACTS ALLEGED)

That you did so in (your case) (the case of PERSON) against whom you had reason to believe there were or would be criminal proceedings pending;

That the act(s) (was) (were) done with the intent to influence impede the due administration of justice; and

That, under the circumstances, your conduct was to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces AND of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces.

IF ALLEGED: That you had reason to believe that (PERSON) would be called upon to provide evidence as a witness.

Definitions

In considering those elements, you should use and understand the following definitions:

"Criminal proceedings" includes lawful searches, criminal investigations conducted by police or command authorities, Article 15 non-judicial punishment proceedings, Article 32 preliminary hearings, courts-martial, and state and federal criminal trials.

Criminal proceedings do not include administrative proceedings or inspections, such as flying status hearings, health and welfare inspections, routine and random urinalysis tests, and inspections to determine and ensure security, military fitness, or good order and discipline.

One can obstruct justice in relation to a criminal proceeding involving himself/herself.

While the prosecution is required to prove beyond a reasonable doubt you had the specific intent to influence impede the due administration of justice, there need not be an actual obstruction of justice. That you failed in your efforts is not a defense.

It is not necessary that charges be pending or even that an investigation be underway. You also do not have to know that charges have been brought or proceedings begun. The government must, however, prove beyond a reasonable doubt that you had reason to believe there were or would be criminal proceedings against (you) (PERSON) or that some law enforcement official of the military would be investigating your actions.

"Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline" is conduct which causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.

"Service discrediting conduct" is conduct which tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.

Communicating with victims or witnesses: Asking that one not reveal or report that an offense occurred is not an obstruction of justice unless you knew or had reason to believe that there were or would be criminal proceedings pending and your acts were done with the intent to obstruct justice.

Advising witness to remain silent: If you advised a potential witness of his/her legal right to remain silent merely to inform the witness about possible self-incrimination, that would not amount to a specific intent to impede influence the due administration of justice. However, if this advice was given for a corrupt purpose, such as a desire to protect yourself or others from the prospective witness' possibly damaging statements, then this may be obstruction.

To constitute an obstruction of justice the acts alleged to be the obstruction must be separate and not part of the commission of another offense alleged to have been committed by you.

Conspiracy: When there is a (conspiracy) (plan) to commit an offense other than obstruction of justice itself, and the plan contemplates that the parties will take affirmative actions to obstruct justice in relation to the offenses which are the object of the conspiracy, obstruction of justice is not a separate offense. Consequently, unless the alleged obstruction of justice was not part of the plan to commit the offense of OFFENSE, you may not be convicted of obstruction of justice. Committing an offense in such a way as to avoid detection does not amount to obstruction
