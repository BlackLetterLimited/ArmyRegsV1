Article 91 – ASSAULT ON WARRANT, CHIEF, OR PETTY OFFICER

Elements:

(1) That (state the time alleged), you were (an enlisted service member) (a warrant officer);

(2) That (state the time and place alleged) you :

(a) (attempted to do) (offered to do) (did) bodily harm to (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged), or

(b) (struck) (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged);

(3) That you did so by (state the alleged manner of the striking or assault);

(4) That, at the time, (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged) was in the execution of his/her office; (and)

(5) That you knew, at the time, that (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged) was a (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer; [and]

NOTE 1: Victim the superior noncommissioned/petty officer of the accused. If the victim was the accused’s superior warrant, noncommissioned, or petty officer, the following two elements apply:

[(6)] That (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged) was the superior (noncommissioned) (petty) (warrant) officer of you ; and

[(7)] That you then knew that (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged) was you ’s superior (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer.

Definitions:

A (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer is “in the execution of (his) (her) office” when that officer is doing any act or service required or authorized to be done by statute, regulation, the order of a superior, custom of the service, or military usage.

(READ IF ASSAULT BY ATTEMPT)

An assault is an attempt with unlawful force or violence to do bodily harm to another. An “attempt to do bodily harm” is an overt act which amounts to more than mere preparation and is done with apparent present ability to do bodily harm to another. Physical injury or offensive touching is not required.

An act of force or violence is unlawful if done without legal justification or excuse and without the lawful consent of the victim.

The use of threatening words alone does not constitute an assault. However, if the threatening words are accompanied by a menacing act or gesture, there may be an assault since the combination constitutes a demonstration of violence.

(READ IF ASSAULT BY OFFER)

An assault is an offer with unlawful force or violence to do bodily harm to another. An “offer to do bodily harm” is an intentional or culpably negligent act or failure to act which foreseeably causes another to reasonably believe that force will immediately be applied to (his) (her) person. There must be an apparent present ability to bring about bodily harm. Physical injury or offensive touching is not required and specific intent to do bodily harm is not required.

An act of force or violence is unlawful if done without legal justification or excuse and without the lawful consent of the victim.

The use of threatening words alone does not constitute an assault. However, if the threatening words are accompanied by a menacing act or gesture, there may be an assault since the combination constitutes a demonstration of violence.

(READ IF ASSAULT CONSUMMATED BY A BATTERY)

An assault is an attempt or offer with unlawful force or violence to do bodily harm to another. An assault in which bodily harm is inflicted is called a battery. A “battery” is an unlawful and intentional or culpably negligent application of force or violence to another. The act must be done without legal justification or excuse and without lawful consent of the victim.

“Bodily harm” means any physical injury to or offensive touching of another person, however slight.

An act of force or violence is unlawful if done without legal justification or excuse and without the lawful consent of the victim.

Culpable negligence is a degree of carelessness greater than simple negligence. Simple negligence is the absence of due care. The law requires everyone at all times to demonstrate the care for the safety of others that a reasonably careful person would demonstrate under the same or similar circumstances; that is what “due care” means. Culpable negligence is a negligent act or failure to act with a gross, reckless, wanton or deliberate disregard for the foreseeable results to others, instead of merely a failure to use due care.

“Superior (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer” includes any (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer who is superior in rank to you but does not include an acting noncommissioned or petty officer.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On or about DATE, were you an (enlisted service member/warrant officer)?

1b: What rank were you?

2a: On this date, did you: (a) (attempt to do) (offer to do) (do) bodily harm to (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged), or (b) (strike) (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged)?

2b: Which one?

2c: Describe what you did.

2d: Explain the circumstances under which this happened.

2e: Why did you (attempt to do) (offer to do) (do) bodily harm to VICTIM?

2f: Did VICTIM strike you first or threaten you?

2g: Were you acting in self-defense?

2h: Were you acting in defense of another?

3a: Describe the manner in which you committed the (attempt)(offer)(actual) bodily harm to VICTIM.

4a: When you struck VICTIM, was he in the execution of his office?

4b: How do you know?

4c: Was he in uniform?

4d: Was he at his command/office/on post?

4e: Why do you believe he was in the execution of his office?

4f: Did he/she do anything that would cause him / her to lose the protections of his / her office?

5a: Did you know on DATE at PLACE that PERSON was a (warrant/NCO/petty officer)?

5b: How?

5c: Did anyone force or coerce you into (attempting) (offering)(doing) bodily harm to VICTIM?

5d: Was it a freely made decision of yours to do this?

5e: Did you have permission from anyone of authority to do this?

5f: Do you believe you had any legal justification or excuse for your misconduct?

5g: Could you have avoided ACT if you had wanted to?

IF SUPERIOR ALLEGED, ASK QUESTIONS 6 AND &

6a: Do you believe that VICTIM was superior in rank to you?

6b: Was VICTIM your superior (noncommissioned officer) (warrant officer) (petty officer)?

7a: Did you know at the time that VICTIM your superior (noncommissioned officer) (warrant officer) (petty officer)?

7b: How did you know?
