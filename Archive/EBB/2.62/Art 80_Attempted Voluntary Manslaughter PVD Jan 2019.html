Article 80 – ATTEMPTED VOLUNTARY MANSLAUGHTER

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you did (a) certain act(s), that is, (state the act(s) alleged or raised by the evidence);

(2) That such act(s) (was) (were) done with the specific intent to unlawfully kill (state the name of the alleged victim); that is, to kill without justification or excuse;

(3) That such act(s) amounted to more than mere preparation; that is, (it was) (they were) a substantial step and a direct movement toward the unlawful killing of (state the name of the alleged victim); and

(4) That such act(s) apparently tended to bring about the commission of the offense of voluntary manslaughter, that is, the act(s) apparently would have resulted in the actual commission of the offense of voluntary manslaughter except for (a circumstance unknown to you) (an unexpected intervening circumstance) (__________) which prevented completion of that offense.

Definitions:

Preparation consists of devising or arranging the means necessary for the commission of the attempted offense. The acts which you did must amount to a substantial step and a direct movement toward the commission of the intended offense of involuntary manslaughter. A substantial step is one that is strongly corroborative of your criminal intent and is indicative of your resolve to commit the offense. Whether or not the involuntary manslaughter actually occurred or was completed, you must have intended every element of involuntary manslaughter.

LIST ELEMENTS OF VOLUNTARY MANSLAUGHTER

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE at PLACE did you commit OVERT ACT ALLEGED?

1b: Describe what you did.

2a: When you committed OVERT ACT, did you specifically intend to unlawfully kill ALLEGED VICTIM?

2b: Did you believe you had any legal justification or excuse to kill ALLEGED VICTIM?

2c: Did you know at the time of OVERT ACT that your conduct was wrongful?

2d: Did anyone or anything force you to attempt to kill ALLEGED VICTIM?

2e: Could you have avoided attempting to kill ALLEGED VICTIM if you had wanted to?

3a: Do you believe and admit that OVERT ACT amounted to more than mere preparation?

3b: Do you believe and admit that OVERT ACT was a substantial step and a direct movement toward the unlawful killing of ALLEGED VICTIM?

4a: Was OVERT ACT an act that apparently tended to bring about the commission of the offense of voluntary manslaughter?

4b: Would OVERT ACT have resulted in the actual commission of the offense of voluntary manslaughter except for a circumstance unknown to you or an unexpected intervening circumstance?

4c: Did CIRCUMSTANCE prevent completion of the offense?

4d: Do you believe and admit that it was CIRCUMSTANCE, and not any action or decision on your part, that prevented the completion of the offense of VOLUNTARY MANSLAUGHTER?
