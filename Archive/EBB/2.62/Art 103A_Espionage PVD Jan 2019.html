Article 103A – ESPIONAGE

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you communicated, delivered, or transmitted any document, writing, code book, signal book, sketch, photograph, photographic negative, blueprint, plan, map, model, note, instrument, appliance, or information relating to the national defense;

(2) That this matter was communicated, delivered, or transmitted to (state the party allegedly communicated with), any foreign government, or to any faction or party or military or naval force within a foreign country, whether recognized by the United States, or to any representative, officer, agent, employee, subject, or citizen thereof, either directly or indirectly; and

(3) That you did so with intent or reason to believe that such matter would be used to the injury of the United States or to the advantage of a foreign nation.

Definitions:

“Intent or reason to believe” that the information “is to be used to the injury of the United States or to the advantage of a foreign nation” means that you acted in bad faith and without lawful authority with respect to information that is not lawfully accessible to the public.

“Instrument, appliance, or information relating to the national defense” includes the full range of modern technology (and matter that may be developed in the future) (including chemical or biological agents) (computer technology), and other matter related to the national defense.

“Foreign country” includes those countries that have and have not been recognized by the United States.

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE at PLACE, did you communicate/deliver/transmit a document/writing/code book/signal book/sketch/photograph/photographic negative/blueprint/plan/map/model/note/instrument/appliance/disk/ information relating to the national defense?

1b: To whom did you provide this information?

1c: Where did you first meet this individual?

1d: Did this individual identify himself in some way? Did he provide identification?

1e: Did the individual request you provide information to him relating to the national defense of the United States?

1f: Explain any discussions you had and what you ultimately agreed to do.

1g: Describe how the information or item you provided related to national defense.

2a: Was the information that you provided relating to national defense communicated/delivered/transmitted to a foreign government or to any faction or party or military or naval force within a foreign country?

2b: Was this individual a representative/officer/agent/employee/subject/citizen of the foreign government?

2c: How did you know that?

2d: Which foreign government?

2e: How did you provide the information to him?

2f: Was the information provided directly or indirectly?

2g: If it was indirectly (i.e. a drop off location), was this the site the individual indicated he would retrieve it from?

2h: Did you see him retrieve it or learn if it ultimately was retrieved?

3a: Did you provide this information with intent or reason to believe that such matter would be used to the injury of the United States or to the advantage of a foreign nation?

3b: Why did you believe it would be used to injury the United States or to the advantage of a foreign nation?

3c: What were the relations between the United States and the foreign government at the time you provided the information?

3d: Did you actually intend that the information you provided would be used to harm the United States or to provide an advantage to a foreign nation?

3e: As a member of the United States armed forces, why would you want the United States to be harmed or a foreign nation to be advantaged?

3f: Were you receiving money or favors in return for providing this information?

3g: Did anyone force or coerce you into providing the information?

3h: Could you have avoided committing espionage if you had wanted to?

3i: Was it a freely made decision on your part?
