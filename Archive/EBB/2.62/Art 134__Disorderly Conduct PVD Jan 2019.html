Article 134 – DRUNK AND DISORDERLY CONDUCT

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you were (drunk) (disorderly) (drunk and disorderly) (on board ship); and

(2) That, under the circumstances, the conduct of you were (to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces) (and) (of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces).

Definitions:

Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline is conduct that causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.

Service discrediting conduct is conduct that tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.

The term "disorderly" refers to conduct which is of such a nature as to affect the peace and quiet of persons who may witness it and who may be disturbed or provoked to resentment thereby. It includes conduct that endangers public morals or outrages public decency and any disturbance of a contentious or turbulent character.

The term "drunk" means any intoxication that is sufficient to impair the rational and full exercise of your mental or physical faculties. A person is drunk who is under the influence of an intoxicant so that the use of his faculties is impaired. Such impairment does not exist unless your conduct due to intoxicating liquors or drugs was such as to create the impression within the minds of observers that you were unable to act like a normal, rational person.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Were you at PLACE on DATE?

1b: Did you consume any alcoholic beverages that day?

1c: Where were you drinking?

1d: How much did you have to drink?

1e: Did you drink enough alcohol to impair your senses?

1f: Were you able to think, talk, see clearly? Walk straight?

1g: Do you have any recollection of what occurred after you began drinking?

1h: Have you read the reports of those who observed your behavior that night? Do you believe their accounts to be true?

1i: Were you disorderly in any way, as I have defined that term for you?

1j: If so, how?

1k: Where did this drunkenness/disorderly conduct occur?

1l: Was this in a public area where you might be seen by others?

2a: Was your conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline or of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces, or both?

2b: In what respects?

2c: Do you think that your conduct would lower the public's opinion of the service if someone in the civilian community had seen it?

2d: Do you believe and admit that your conduct discredited the service?

2e: Did anyone force or coerce you into being drunk/disorderly?

2f: Could you have avoided committing this misconduct if you had wanted?
