Article 134 – DISHONORABLY FAILING TO PAY DEBT

Elements:

(1) That you were indebted to (state the name of the person alleged) in the sum of (state the amount alleged) for (state the alleged debt);

(2) That this debt became due and payable (on) (about) (on or about) (state the date alleged);

(3) That (state the place alleged), from about __________ to about __________ while the debt was still due and payable, you dishonorably failed to pay this debt; and

(4) That, under the circumstances, the conduct of you were (to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces) (and) (of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces).

Definitions:

Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline is conduct that causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.

Service discrediting conduct is conduct that tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.

The failure to pay the debt must have been the result of more than mere negligence, that is the absence of due care. The failure to pay must be dishonorable. A failure to pay is "dishonorable" if it is fraudulent, deceitful, a willful evasion, in bad faith, deliberate, based on false promises, or results from a grossly indifferent attitude toward one's just obligations.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE at PLACE, were you indebted to VICTIM?

1b: At that time, how much did you owe VICTIM?

1c: From that date on, do you believe and admit that you would have incurred an indebtedness to VICTIM of $$$?

1d: Why did you owe victim $$$?

1e: What, if anything, did you receive from VICTIM for $$$?

2a: Did this debt became due and payable on DATE?

2b: Was there anything about your arrangements with VICTIM that would have made you not liable for $$$ on and after DATE?

3a: At PLACE, from about ____ to about ____, while the debt was still due and payable, did you fail to pay this debt?

3b: Why?

3c: Was your failure to pay this debt dishonorable as I’ve defined that term?

3d: How so?

3e: Were you receiving your full military pay and allowances during this period?

3f: Did you consciously and knowingly make the decision to use the pay you received from the government for other expenses?

3g: Was it your conscious and knowing decision not to pay VICTIM the $$$?

3h: What was your attitude insofar as your obligation to pay VICTIM the $$$ during this period?

3i: Do you feel that your other expenses justified or excused your nonpayment of the $$$?

3j: Do you admit and believe that your conduct during this period regarding this indebtedness was fraudulent, deceitful, a willful evasion, in bad faith, deliberate, based on false promises, or resulted from a grossly indifferent attitude toward your just obligations?

3k: Which one or ones and why?

3l: Do you admit and believe that your conduct was dishonorable, as I have defined that term for you?

3m: Did anyone or anything prevent you from paying the debt?

3n: Could you have paid the debt if you had wanted to?

4a: Under the circumstances, was your conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline in the Armed Forces or of a nature to bring discredit upon the Armed Forces?

4b: Which, or both?

4c. Explain why you believe this.
