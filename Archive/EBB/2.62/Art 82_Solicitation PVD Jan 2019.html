Article 82 – SOLICITATION

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you solicited or advised (state the name of the person(s) allegedly solicited or advised) to commit (specify the offense allegedly solicited or advised);

(2) That you specifically intended that (state the name of person allegedly solicited or advised) commit the offense of (specify the offense allegedly solicited or advised) [and]

NOTE 2: If the offense solicited or advised was attempted or committed, add the following element.

[(3)] That the offense (solicited) (advised) was (committed) (attempted) as the proximate result of the solicitation.

Definitions:

The word solicitation means any statement or any other act which may be understood to be a serious request to commit the offense of OFFENSE. The person solicited must know that the act requested is part of a criminal venture, although it is not necessary the person solicited agree to the request or act upon it. Other terms often used to describe solicitation include counseling, influencing, urging, tempting, commanding, enticing, inducing, or inciting another to commit an offense.

The word advise means any statement or any other act which may be understood to be a serious recommendation or suggestion to commit the offense of OFFENSE. The person advised must know that the act advised is part of a criminal venture, although it is not necessary the person advised agree to the advice or act upon it.

Proof that the offense of OFFENSE actually occurred is not required. However, you must have intended that SOLICITED PERSON commit every element of the offense OFFENSE. Those elements are as follows:

LIST ELEMENTS OF SOLICITED OFFENSE

To be guilty of this offense, you must have specifically intended that the offense of OFFENSE be committed. Statements or conduct under circumstances which were intended to be in jest do not constitute the offense.

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.
