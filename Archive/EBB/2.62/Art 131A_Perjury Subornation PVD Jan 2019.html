Article 131A – PERJURY-SUBORNATION

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you induced and procured (state the name of the alleged perjurer) to take an oath or its equivalent and to falsely (testify) (depose) upon such (oath) (affirmation) that (state the alleged perjured testimony);

(2) That the (oath) (affirmation) was administered to (state the name of the alleged perjurer) in a (matter) (__________) in which an oath or its equivalent was required or authorized by law;

(3) That the oath or its equivalent was administered by a person having authority to do so;

(4) That upon such oath or its equivalent (state the name of the alleged perjurer) willfully (made) (subscribed) a statement, to wit: (set forth the statement as alleged);

(5) That the statement was material;

(6) That the statement was false; and

(7) That you and (state the name of the alleged perjurer) did not then believe the statement to be true.

Definitions:

“Induce and procure” means to influence, persuade, or cause.

An oath is a formal, outward pledge, coupled with an appeal to the Supreme Being, that the truth will be stated.

An affirmation is a solemn and formal pledge binding upon one’s conscience, that the truth will be stated.

“Subscribe” means to write one’s name on a document for the purpose of adopting its words as one’s own expressions.

Material means important to the issue or matter of inquiry.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE at PLACE, did you induce and procure PERJURER to take an oath or its equivalent and to falsely (testify) (depose)?

1b: Who is PERJURER?

1c: How do you know him/her?

1d: What was the nature of the proceeding at which PERJURER testified or deposed falsely?

1e: How did you induce PERJURER to perjure himself?

1f: What, exactly, was the perjured testimony?

2a: Was an oath administered to PERJURER as authorized by law?

2b: Do you know what that oath was?

2c: Are you confident that it was an oath or its equivalent and authorized by law?

3a: Was the oath administered by a person having authority to do so?

3b: Who was that?

3c: Are you confident that person was authorized to administer an oath to PERJURER?

4a. Upon taking the oath, did PERJURER indicate he would tell the truth?

4b. Upon such oath or its equivalent, did PERJURER willfully (make) (subscribe) a statement?

4c: What statement(s) did PERJURER make after taking his/her oath?

5a: Do you believe that statement was material, or in other words that it was important to the issue or matter of inquiry?

5b: Why?

6a: Was the statement that PERJURER made a false statement?

6b: How do you know?

7a. Did you and PERJURER know the statement was false when PERJURER stated it?

7b: How do you know that?
