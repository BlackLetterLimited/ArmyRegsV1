Article 131 – PERJURY-FALSE TESTIMONY

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you (took an oath) (made an affirmation) in a (judicial proceeding) (a course of justice), that is: (state the judicial proceeding or course of justice alleged.);

(2) That the (oath) (affirmation) was administered to you in a (matter) (___________) in which an (oath) (affirmation) was (required) (authorized) by law;

(3) That the (oath) (affirmation) was administered by a person having the authority to do so;

(4) That upon such (oath) (affirmation) you willfully gave certain testimony, namely: (set forth the testimony alleged);

(5) That the testimony was material;

(6) That the testimony was false; and

(7) That you did not then believe the testimony to be true.

Definitions:

An oath is a formal, outward pledge, coupled with an appeal to the Supreme Being, that the truth will be stated.

An affirmation is a solemn and formal, external pledge, binding upon one’s conscience, that the truth will be stated.

Material means important to the issue or matter of inquiry

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On or about DATE at PLACE, did you testify in a court or judicial proceeding?

1b: What type of a court or judicial proceeding was it?

1c: Did you testify as a witness?

1d: Prior to giving your testimony, did you take an oath or make an affirmation?

1e: Which one was it?

1f: Describe the oath or affirmation.

2a: Was that oath or affirmation administered to you in a matter in which an oath or authorization was required or authorized by law? EXPLAIN.

2b: As a witness at that court or judicial proceeding, do you agree that you were required by law to take the oath or affirmation in order to testify?

3a: Who administered the oath or affirmation to you?

3b: Do you agree that person was authorized to do so?

3c: Did you understand that by taking this oath or affirmation you were promising to only make truthful statements at that court or judicial proceeding?

4a: After taking your oath or affirmation, did you testify in the court or judicial proceeding?

4b: Did you, in fact, willfully say essentially the following things: (LIST THE STATEMENTS ALLEGED)?

4c: Did you make a conscious decision to make each of the statements?

5a: Where these statements material to the court or judicial proceeding?

5b: In what respects were the statements material?

5c: Do you admit that these statements would have impacted the court proceeding at which you testified?

5d: In other words, the statements were material because the court would consider them in deciding the case?

6a: Were the statements you made false?

6b: Specifically, how were they false?

7a: At the time you made these statements during your testimony, did you believe any of them to be true?

7b: In your mind, were each of the statements false?

7c: Do you believe and admit that you lied when you made each of the statements?

7d: Did you think you had any legal justification or authorization for falsely making any of the statements?

7e: If you knew it was illegal to testify falsely at this court proceeding, why did you do so?

7f: Do you believe that your conduct in making the false statements at this court proceeding was wrongful?

7g: Could you have avoided committing this offense if you wanted to?
