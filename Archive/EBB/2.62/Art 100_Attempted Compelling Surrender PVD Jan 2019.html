Article 100 – ATTEMPTED COMPELLING SURRENDER

Elements:

(1) That (state the name and rank of the person alleged) was the commander of (state the name of the place, vessel, aircraft, military property, or body of members of the armed forces, as alleged);

(2) That (state the time and place alleged), you did a certain overt act that is, (state the act(s) alleged or raised by the evidence);

(3) That the act was done with the intent to compel (state the name and rank of the commander alleged) to (give up to the enemy) (abandon) the (state the name of the place, vessel, aircraft, military property, or body of members of the armed forces, as alleged);

(4) That the act amounted to more than mere preparation; that is, it was a direct movement toward the commission of the offense of compelling surrender; and

(5) That the act apparently tended to bring about the offense of compelling (surrender) (abandonment), (that is, the act apparently would have resulted in the actual commission of the offense of compelling (surrender) (abandonment) except for (a circumstance unknown to you) (an unexpected intervening circumstance) (__________) which prevented the completion of that offense).

Definitions:

While actual abandonment or surrender is not required, there must be some act done with this purpose in mind, even if it falls short of actual accomplishment.

"Abandon" means to completely separate oneself from all further responsibility to defend that (place) (vessel) (aircraft) (military property) (body of members of the armed forces). Stated differently, "abandon" means (relinquishing control) (giving up) (yielding) (leaving) because of threatened dangers or encroachments.

"Give up to the enemy" means surrender.

"Enemy" includes (not only) organized opposing forces in time of war (but also any other hostile body that our forces may be opposing) (such as a rebellious mob or a band of renegades) and includes civilians as well as members of military organizations.

"Enemy" is not restricted to the enemy government or its armed forces. All the citizens of one belligerent are enemies of the government and the citizens of the other.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Who was the commander of the (place, vessel, aircraft, military property, or body of members of the armed forces) at that time?

1b: Did you know at the time that COMMANDER was the commander of the (place, vessel, aircraft, military property, or body of members of the armed forces)?

1c: How did you know that COMMANDER was the commander of the (place, vessel, aircraft, military property, or body of members of the armed forces) at that time?

2: Describe the act you took to compel COMMANDER to surrender.

3a: In taking ACT did you intend to compel COMMANDER to give up to the enemy or abandon the (place, vessel, aircraft, military property, or body of members of the armed forces)?

3b: Did you know at the time that by taking ACT, COMMANDER would give up to the enemy or abandon the (place, vessel, aircraft, military property, or body of members of the armed forces)?

3c: And did you intend for him/her to do so?

4a: Was your action, in taking ACT, more than mere preparation?

4b: In other words, do you admit that taking ACT was a substantial step toward compelling COMMANDER to surrender?

5a: Would ACT have apparently tended to bring about the offense of compelling (surrender) (abandonment)?

5b: Would ACT have brought about compelling COMMANDER to surrender had it not been for (a circumstance unknown to you) (an unexpected intervening circumstance) (__________) which prevented the completion of that offense.

5c: Describe the (circumstance unknown to you) (unexpected intervening circumstance) (__________) that prevented the completion of the offense.
