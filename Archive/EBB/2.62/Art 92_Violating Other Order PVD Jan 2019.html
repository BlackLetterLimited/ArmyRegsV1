Article 92 – VIOLATING OTHER WRITTEN ORDER OR REGULATION OR FAILING TO OBEY OTHER LAWFUL ORDER

Elements:

(1) That a member of the armed forces, namely, (state the name and rank or grade of the person issuing the order or regulation), issued a certain lawful (written) (order) (regulation) (in the following terms:) (state the date and specific source of the alleged written order or regulation and quote the order or regulation or the specific portion thereof or, if not a written order, state the particular other order or the specific portion thereof);

(2) That you had knowledge of the (order) (regulation);

(3) That you had a duty to obey such (order) (regulation); and

(4) That (state the time and place alleged), you failed to obey this lawful (order) (regulation) by (state the manner alleged).

Definitions:

An order, to be lawful, must relate to specific military duty and be one that is authorized under the circumstances. An order is lawful if it is reasonably necessary to safeguard and protect the morale, discipline, and usefulness of the members of a command and is directly connected with the maintenance of good order in the services. It is illegal if it is unrelated to military duty; its sole purpose is to accomplish some private end; it is arbitrary and unreasonable; or it is given for the sole purpose of increasing the penalty for an offense which it is expected the accused may commit.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Did SOURCE issue a written (order)(regulation), specifically, (state the written order or regulation)?

1b: What were the terms of the order?

2a: Were you aware of the (order)(regulation)?

2b: How did you become aware?

2c: Approximately when did you become aware of it?

3a: Did the (order) (regulation) relate to a specific military duty? What duty?

3b: Was it reasonably necessary to safeguard and protect the morale, discipline, and usefulness of the members of a command? How so?

3c: Do you agree that the (order) (regulation) was directly connected with the maintenance of good order in the military?

3d: Do you agree that it was authorized under the circumstances?

3e: Do you admit that the (order) (regulation) was lawful?

3f: Did you have a duty to obey the (order) (regulation)?

4a: On or about (date of offense) at (place of offense), did you fail to obey it?

4b: Tell me about how you violated the written (order) (regulation).

4c: Were there any exceptions to the order? (If so) Did your situation fit any of them?

4d: Did anyone authorize you to violate the written (order) (regulation)?

4e: Did anyone or anything force you to violate it?

4f: Do you believe you had any legal justification or excuse for what you did?

4g: Could you have complied with the written (order) (regulation) if you had wanted to?
