APPENDIX G-2. FULL REHEARING

NOTE: Rehearings are governed by Article 63, UCMJ, and RCM 810. Many of the procedures are the same as in an original trial, but rehearings require certain modifications to the “standard” court-martial procedures. Therefore, the following script for a rehearing requires users flip back-and-forth between this Appendix and Chapter 2 of the Benchbook.

NOTE: The judge should conduct an early RCM 802 conference with counsel for both parties to ascertain the likely choices for counsel and forum, and to discuss special evidentiary considerations. An Article 39(a) session should be held promptly to consider such matters as: (a) motions for appropriate relief; (b) sufficiency and timeliness of the written notice of rehearing served upon the accused; (c) examination of prior appellate decisions (in this regard, the trial counsel should be cautioned that when announcing the general nature of the charge(s), only the charges and specifications subject to rehearing should be announced); and (d) evidentiary considerations. Again, the script for a rehearing requires the use of scripts from this Appendix and Chapter 2, as detailed below. The trial should proceed in open session as below.

MJ: Please be seated. The Court is called to order.

TC: Your Honor, Appellate Exhibit __ is the original charge sheet for this case, originally referred on __________. Appellate Exhibit __ is the (entry of judgment and statement of trial results) (promulgating order) for the prior proceedings, (entered by a Military Judge on __________) (issued by HQ, __________, dated __________). The __________ authorized a sentence rehearing in this case in its (published opinion located at __ MJ ___) (unpublished opinion marked as Appellate Exhibit __).

[FOR ARMY REHEARINGS: Appellate Exhibit __ is The Judge Advocate General’s memorandum designating this General Court martial Convening Authority for rehearings.]

[FOR OTHER SERVICES: Appellate Exhibit __ is the memorandum from __________ to the Commander, __________, designating (him) (her) as the convening authority authorized to order this sentence rehearing]

Appellate Exhibit __ is the order referring this case for a sentence rehearing.

NOTE: The judge should examine the convening order(s) and any amendments for accuracy. Only minor changes may be made at trial to the convening orders. Any correction that affects the identity of the individual concerned must be made by an amending or correcting order.

TC: This court-martial is convened by [Court-Martial Convening Order Number __] [Special Order Number __], Headquarters, __________, dated __________ (as (superseded) (amended) by [Court-Martial Convening Order Number __] [Special Order Number __], same Headquarters, dated __________) copies of which, as well as copies of the aforementioned Appellate Exhibits, have been furnished to the military judge, counsel, and the accused, and will be inserted in the record.

(TC: The following corrections are noted in the convening orders __________.)

NOTE: A record of every pre-referral proceeding conducted pursuant to Article 30a, UCMJ shall be prepared and such record shall be included in the record of trial. See RCM 309(e). Trial counsel should account for any such proceedings, or lack thereof, as follows:

TC: [No Article 30a proceedings were held in connection with this case]. [Judge (name) presided over (an) Article 30a proceeding(s) in connection with this case on (date(s)); specifically: ((an application for a pre-referral (investigative subpoena) (warrant) (order)) (a request for relief from subpoena or other process) (a matter referred by an appellate court) (an application to designate a person to assume the rights of an alleged victim under Article 6b) (an application for review of pretrial confinement) (an RCM 706 inquiry) (an RCM 909 capacity hearing) (A request for individual military counsel) (a victim’s petition for relief) (___________)) and the record of that proceeding will be added to the record].

TC: The prosecution is ready to proceed in the rehearing in the case of United States versus (Private) (___) __________.

TC: The accused and the following persons detailed to this court are present: __________, Military Judge; __________, Trial Counsel; and __________, Defense Counsel. The members (and the following persons detailed to this court) are absent __________.

TC: __________ has been detailed reporter to this court-martial and (has been previously sworn) (will now be sworn).

TC: (I) (All members of the prosecution) have been detailed to this court-martial by __________. (I am) (All members of the prosecution are) qualified and certified under Article 27(b) and sworn under Article 42(a), Uniform Code of Military Justice. (I have not) (No member of the prosecution has) acted in any manner that might tend to disqualify (me) (us) in this court-martial. (Further, (I am) (__________ (is) (are)) qualified and certified as a Special Trial Counsel under Article 24a(b)(1), UCMJ.)

NOTE: If any trial counsel needs to be sworn, the MJ will provide the following oath: “Do you (swear) (affirm) that you will faithfully perform all the duties of (trial) (assistant trial) counsel in the case now in hearing (so help you God)?”

G-2-1. RIGHTS TO COUNSEL

MJ: __________, at your original trial, the military judge explained your rights to counsel to you. Regardless of the choices that you made at your original trial with regard to counsel, you have all of the following rights during this rehearing.

You have the right to be represented by __________, your detailed military defense counsel. Your detailed military counsel (is a) (are) lawyer(s), certified by The Judge Advocate General as qualified to act as your defense counsel (and (is a) (are) member(s) of the (Army’s Trial Defense Service) (Navy-Marine Corps’ Defense Service Organization) (Air Force’s Trial Defense Division) (Coast Guard’s Defense Services Division). Your detailed military defense counsel’s services are provided at no expense to you.

You also have the right to be represented by a military counsel of your own selection, provided that the counsel you request is reasonably available. If you were represented by military counsel of your own selection, then your detailed defense counsel would normally be excused. However, you could request that your detailed counsel continue to represent you, but your request would not have to be granted. Do you understand that?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: In addition to your military defense counsel, you have the right to be represented by a civilian counsel at no expense to the government. Civilian counsel may represent you along with your military defense counsel or you could excuse your military counsel and be represented only by your civilian counsel. Do you understand that?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: Again, I want to stress that you are not limited in exercising any of these choices by any choices you made at your first trial or while your case was on appeal. Do you understand that?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: Do you have any questions about your rights to counsel?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: By whom do you wish to be represented?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: And by (him) (her) (them) alone?

ACC: (Responds.)

NOTE: If the accused elects pro se representation, see paragraph 2-7-2, PRO SE REPRESENTATION. The judge must be aware of any possible conflict of interest by counsel, and if a conflict exists, the judge must obtain a waiver from the accused or order new counsel appointed for the accused. See paragraph 2-7-3, WAIVER OF CONFLICT-FREE COUNSEL.

NOTE: If the original defense counsel from trial is not present, the judge should inquire or explain as applicable why the attorney-client relationship has ceased (e.g., former defense counsel left active duty or accused claimed ineffective assistance of counsel against former defense counsel.) In any situation where it appears the accused may have a legal right to the assistance of a former defense counsel, the judge should obtain from the accused an affirmative waiver of that former defense counsel’s presence.

(MJ: __________ is no longer on active duty and cannot be detailed by military authority to represent you at this rehearing. However, you could attempt to retain __________ as civilian counsel. Accordingly, __________ has been detailed to represent you at this rehearing. Do you wish to proceed with this hearing without __________ and with only __________ as your counsel? Do you expressly consent to not having __________ represent you at this rehearing?)

(MJ: Because you have made allegations after trial that __________ (was) (were) ineffective in (his) (her) (their) former representation of you, (he has not) (she has not) (they have not) been detailed to represent you at this rehearing. Accordingly, __________ has been detailed to represent you at this rehearing. Do you wish to proceed with this rehearing without __________ and with only __________ as your counsel? Do you expressly consent to not having __________ represent you at this rehearing?)

MJ: Defense Counsel, please announce your detailing and qualifications.

DC: (I) (All detailed members of the defense) have been detailed to this hearing by __________. (I am) (All detailed members of the defense are) qualified and certified under Article 27(b) and sworn under Article 42(a), Uniform Code of Military Justice. (I have not) (No member of the defense has) acted in any manner that might tend to disqualify (me) (us) in this proceeding.

NOTE: If any military defense counsel needs to be sworn, the judge will provide the following oath: “Do you swear or affirm that you will faithfully perform all the duties of defense counsel in the case now in hearing (so help you God)?”

CDC: I am an attorney and licensed to practice law in the state(s) of __________. I am a member in good standing of the (__________) bar(s). I have not acted in any manner that might tend to disqualify me in this proceeding.

NOTE: In all cases with a civilian defense counsel, the judge will provide the following oath:

MJ: Do you, __________, (swear) (affirm) that you will faithfully perform the duties of individual defense counsel in the case now in hearing (so help you God)?

CDC: (Responds).

G-2-2. JUDICIAL DETAILING AND ANNOUNCEMENT OF CHARGE(S)

MJ: I have been properly certified and sworn, and detailed (myself) (by __________) to this hearing. I am not aware of any matter that might be a ground for challenge against me (I was the judge for the __________ portion of this case.) (I was not the judge for any prior proceedings in this case, whether pretrial, trial, or post-trial.) (__________). Does either side desire to question or to challenge me?

TC/DC: (Respond.)

MJ: Counsel for both sides appear to have the requisite qualifications, and all personnel required to be sworn have been sworn. Trial Counsel will announce the general nature of the charge(s).

TC: The general nature of the charge(s) in this case is __________. The charge(s) (was) (were) preferred by __________, (and) forwarded with recommendations as to disposition by __________. (An Article 32 preliminary hearing was conducted by __________.) (The Article 32 preliminary hearing was waived.)

NOTE: If the accused waived the Article 32 preliminary hearing, the judge should ensure that it was a knowing and voluntary waiver. Paragraph 2-7-8, ARTICLE 32 WAIVER COLLOQUY, may be used as a guide.

MJ: Defense Counsel, do you have any challenges to the jurisdiction of this sentence rehearing?

DC: (Responds.)

G-2-3. FORUM RIGHTS

MJ: __________, at your original trial, the military judge also explained your forum rights to you. Regardless of the choice that you made at your original trial with regard to forum, you have all of the following rights during this rehearing.

NOTE: Forum Rights. Insert forum rights colloquy from Chapter 2, paragraphs 2-1-3 through 2-1-6 here. The judge must advise the accused of the accused’s forum rights using the appropriate procedural guides contained in Chapter 2, paragraphs 2-1-3 through 2-1-6. After doing so, continue below.

MJ: Again, I want to stress that you are not limited in exercising any of these forum choices by any choice you made at your first trial. Do you understand that?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: Do you understand the choices that you have?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: By what type of court do you wish to be tried?

ACC: (Responds.)

NOTE: If the accused elects trial before military judge alone, continue with paragraph G-2-3 below. If the accused elects trial before members, go to the next NOTE.

NOTE: Arraignment. If the accused elects trial before members, insert the arraignment script from Chapter 2, paragraph 2-1-7, ARRAIGNMENT, beginning with: “MJ: The accused will now be arraigned…,” here. At the conclusion of the arraignment, if the accused pleads not guilty, go to paragraph G-2-4 below. If the accused pleads guilty, go to Chapter 2, paragraph 2-2-1 and follow the script to Chapter 2, Section VI; then go to paragraph G-2-5 below.

G-2-3. FULL REHEARING BEFORE MILITARY JUDGE ALONE

MJ: Is there a written request for trial by military judge alone?

DC: There is (not).

MJ: Does the accused have a copy?

DC: (Responds).

MJ: __________, Appellate Exhibit __ is a request for trial by military judge alone. Is that your signature on this exhibit?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: At the time you signed this request, did you know I would be the military judge in your case?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: Is your request a voluntary one? By that, I mean are you making this request of your own free will?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: If I approve your request for trial by me alone, you give up your right to be tried by a court composed of members. Do you understand that?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: Do you still wish to be tried by me alone?

ACC: (Responds.)

MJ: Your request is approved. (Judge should indicate so by signing and dating the written request, if one exists).

MJ: The court is assembled.

NOTE: Arraignment. If the accused elects trial by judge alone, insert the arraignment script from Chapter 2, paragraph 2-1-7, ARRAIGNMENT, beginning with: “MJ: The accused will now be arraigned…,” and follow the appropriate guidance therein, based on the plea.

G-2-4. FULL REHEARING BEFORE MEMBERS (CONTESTED)

NOTE: Go to and use the script in Chapter 2, paragraph 2-5, PRELIMINARY INSTRUCTIONS, but insert the following in place of the instructions and NOTEs in paragraph 2-5, from its beginning until just before the judge’s instruction that begins with, “Under the law, the accused is presumed to be innocent of the offense(s)….” Then, beginning at that point in the PRELIMINARY INSTRUCTIONS, continue to completion of the script, as in an original trial.

MJ: Trial Counsel, has the (flyer (Army/AF)) (cleansed charge sheet (Navy/MC/CG)) been marked as an appellate exhibit?

TC: (Responds.)

MJ: Trial Counsel, does the (flyer (Army/AF)) (cleansed charge sheet (Navy/MC/CG)) reflect only the offense(s) for which a full rehearing has been authorized?

TC: (Responds.)

MJ: Defense Counsel, do you have any objection to Appellate Exhibit __?

DC: (Responds.)

MJ: Do both sides agree that I should inform the members that this is a full rehearing, so they’ll not be confused or distracted by references to events or proceedings that happened long ago?

TC/DC: (Respond.)

MJ: Bailiff, call the members.

NOTE: Whenever the members enter the courtroom, all persons except the MJ and reporter shall rise. The members are seated alternately to the right and left of the president according to rank.

MJ: You may be seated. The court-martial of United States v. __________ is called to order. Trial counsel, please announce the convening order(s) and persons present.

TC: The court is convened by [Court-Martial Convening Order Number __] [Special Order Number __], Headquarters __________ dated __________ (as (superseded) (amended) by __________), (a copy) (copies) of which (has) (have) been furnished to each member of the court. The accused and the following persons detailed to this court-martial are present:

__________, Military Judge;

__________, Trial Counsel;

__________, Defense Counsel; and

__________, __________, __________, and __________, Court Members.

NOTE: If detailed members have been excused from appearing at this session in accordance with RCM 911(b) (2024 MCM), RCM 911(c) requires that they must be accounted for on the record as follows:

(TC: All other members as reflected on the convening order(s) have been excused from being present at this session.)

(TC: The following person(s) (is) (are) absent: __________, __________, __________.)

TC: The prosecution is ready to proceed with trial in the case of the United States versus (Private) (___) __________.

MJ: The members will now be sworn. All persons in the courtroom, please rise. Members, please face the trial counsel and raise your right hand. Trial counsel, administer the oath.

TC: Do you swear or affirm that you will answer truthfully the questions concerning whether you should serve as a member of this court-martial; that you will faithfully and impartially try, according to the evidence, your conscience, and the laws applicable to trials by court-martial, the case of the accused now before this court; and that you will not disclose or discover the vote or opinion of any particular member of the court upon a challenge or upon the findings or sentence unless required to do so in the due course of law, so help you God?

MBRS: (Respond.)

MJ: Please be seated. The court is assembled.

Members, I will begin by explaining my role, your role, and the general order of events for this court-martial.

My duty as military judge is to ensure this trial is conducted in a fair, orderly, and impartial manner according to the law. I preside over open sessions, rule upon objections, and instruct you on the law applicable to this case. You are required to follow my instructions on the law and may not consult any other source as to the law pertaining to this case unless it is admitted into evidence. This rule applies throughout the trial, including periods of recess and your closed session deliberations.

There has been a prior trial in this case. This is what is known as a “rehearing” and is being conducted because the prior trial was conducted improperly. I bring this to your attention for one reason only. There has been a time gap between the alleged offense(s) and today. There may be references to a “prior trial” or “first trial.” Documents may appear old or outdated. I bring this to your attention only to remove any confusion or speculation from your mind and to allow you to concentrate on what you hear in court during this rehearing.

You will not be told of the results of that prior trial. As members, it is your duty to hear the evidence and to determine whether the accused is guilty or not guilty (IF SENTENCING BY MEMBERS IS AN OPTION: and, if required, to adjudge an appropriate sentence) based only on what legal and competent evidence is presented for your considerations in this trial.

The fact that there has been a prior trial is not evidence of guilt (IF SENTENCING BY MEMBERS IS AN OPTION: , nor is it evidence that you can use for sentencing, if sentencing is required). The fact that there has been a prior trial must be totally disregarded by you.

NOTE: Continue with Chapter 2, paragraph 2-5, PRELIMINARY INSTRUCTIONS, beginning with the paragraph that begins with “Under the law, the accused is presumed to be innocent of the offense(s)…,” through completion of the script, as in an original trial.

G-2-5. FULL REHEARING BEFORE MEMBERS (FOLLOWING GUILTY PLEA)

NOTE: If permitted by law, “G-2-5. Full Rehearing Before Members (Following Guilty Plea)” and “Section VI: Court Members (Sentencing Only)” may be used if the judge accepted the accused’s guilty plea and the members will determine the sentence. Prior to 28 December 2023, courts-martial involving members sentencing an accused in the context of a full guilty plea were rare. If an accused is “convicted of only offenses committed after December 27, 2023, then the accused shall be sentenced by a military judge in accordance with RCM 1002(a)(2) [Sentencing Parameters and Criteria].” RCM 925(c)(2), 2024 MCM. Therefore, instances of an accused being sentenced by the members in the context of a full guilty plea will become even more rare and, in many cases, not legally permissible. Therefore, the trial scripts for “FULL REHEARING BEFORE MEMBERS (FOLLOWING GUILTY PLEA)” and “COURT MEMBERS (SENTENCING ONLY)” has been removed from the Benchbook. If a military justice practitioner needs to use these scripts, copies may be found in prior versions of the Military Judges’ Benchbook on the US Army Trial Judiciary’s website (https://www.jagcnet.army.mil/USATJ).
