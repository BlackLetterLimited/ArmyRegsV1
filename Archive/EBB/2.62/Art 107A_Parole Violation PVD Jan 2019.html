Article 107A – PAROLE VIOLATION

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you were a prisoner as the result of a court-martial conviction or other criminal proceeding;

(2) That you were on parole;

(3) That there were certain conditions of parole that you were bound to obey:

(4) That you knew you were on parole and the conditions of your parole agreement; and

(5) That, while in such status, you violated the conditions of parole by doing an act or failing to do an act, to wit: (state the manner of the violation).

Definitions:

“Prisoner” refers only to those in confinement resulting from conviction at a court-martial or other criminal proceeding.

“Parole” is defined as “word of honor.” A prisoner on parole, or parolee, has agreed to adhere to a parole plan and conditions of parole.

A “parole plan” is a written or an oral agreement made by the prisoner prior to parole to do or refrain from doing certain acts or activities. A parole plan may include a residence requirement stating where and with whom a parolee will live, and a requirement that the prisoner have an offer of guaranteed employment.

“Conditions of parole” include the parole plan and other reasonable and appropriate conditions of parole, such as paying restitution, beginning or continuing treatment for alcohol or drug abuse, or paying a fine ordered executed as part of a prisoner’s (court-martial sentence) (sentence in another criminal proceeding).

In return for giving his/her “word of honor” to abide by a parole plan and conditions of parole, the prisoner is granted parole.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Prior to DATE, were you a prisoner?

1b: Where were you incarcerated?

1c: Why were you incarcerated?

1d: Were you actually convicted of an offense?

1e: When were you convicted?

1f: How long were you incarcerated?

2a: On DATE at PLACE, were you on parole?

2b: When had you been paroled?

2c: Who had authorized the parole?

3a: Were conditions placed on your parole?

3b: What were they?

3c: Who issued those conditions?

4a: Were you informed of the conditions before being placed on parole?

4b: How were you informed?

4c: Did you understand the conditions of parole before you were paroled?

5a: Did you violate the conditions of parole?

5b: What did you do or fail to do?

5c: Did you understand that you were violating your parole when you ACT/FAIL TO ACT?

5d: Did anyone force or coerce you into violating your parole?

5e: Could you have complied with your parole conditions if you had wanted to?

5f: Was it a freely made decision on your part to violate parole?

5g: Do you believe you had any legal justification or excuse for violating your parole?
