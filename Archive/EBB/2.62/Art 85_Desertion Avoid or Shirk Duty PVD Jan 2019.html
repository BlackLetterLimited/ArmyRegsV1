Article 85 – DESERTION WITH INTENT TO AVOID HAZARDOUS DUTY OR TO SHIRK IMPORTANT SERVICE

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), the accused quit (his) (her) (unit) (organization) (place of duty), that is, (state the name of the unit, organization, or place of duty);

(2) That the accused did so with intent to (avoid a certain duty) (shirk a certain service), that is, __________;

(3) That the duty to be performed was (hazardous) (important);

(4) That the accused knew that (he) (she) would be required for such duty; (and)

(5) That the accused remained so absent until __________; [and]

NOTE 1: Aggravating factors alleged. In the event one or more of the aggravating factors are alleged, the military judge must advise the court members of the aggravating factors as elements.

((6)) That the accused’s absence was in time of war; [and]

((7)) That the accused’s absence was terminated by apprehension.

Definitions:

“Quit” means to go from or remain absent from without proper authority.

[See MCM for examples of “hazardous duty” or “important service.”]

This offense requires that you actually knew of the hazardous duty or important service.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: What was your unit (organization)(place of duty) on DATE?

1b: Where was it located?

1c: Were you with your unit on DATE?

1d: How did your absence from UNIT begin?

1e: Where were you?

2a: Why weren’t you with your UNIT on DATE?

2b: Was it your intent to (avoid a certain duty) (shirk a certain service)?

2c: What was the nature of this (duty)(service)?

2d: Why did you want to avoid/shirk this duty/service?

2e: Was it a freely made decision on your part to leave your UNIT?

3a: Did you think this duty was hazardous? Why?

3b: (Describe how this duty would expose you to danger, risk or peril.)

3c: Did you think this service was important? Why?

3d: (Describe how this service was more significant than ordinary military service.)

4a: Did you have a duty to be with UNIT and to perform this hazardous duty or important service on DATE?

4b: How did you know you had to perform this duty/service?

4c: Did you know that you would be required for such duty/service with UNIT?

5a: When did you return to UNIT?

5b: Did you have contact with any military or civilian authorities while you were absent from your UNIT? If so, explain who, when how and what was discussed.

5c: Did anything or anyone prevent you from returning to your UNIT?

5d: Did you have any physical or mental infirmity that prevented you from returning to your UNIT?

5e: Could you have returned to your UNIT earlier if you had wanted to?

6a: Do you agree that your absence was in a time of war?

7a: Were you later apprehended? When? Where?

7b: Who apprehended you?

7c: Why were you apprehended?

7d: Did the authorities learn of your unauthorized absence status from someone other than you?

7e: How did they learn of it?

7f: Were you in the process of turning yourself into military or civilian authorities at the time?

7g: Did you say or do anything at the time that you intended to turn yourself in?

7h: Did you contact military or civilian authorities to tell them where they could locate you and that you wanted to turn yourself in?
