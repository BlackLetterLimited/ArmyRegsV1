Article 94 – ATTEMPTED MUTINY

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you did a certain act; that is, (state the act(s) alleged or raised by the evidence);

(2) That the act was done with specific intent to commit the offense of mutiny;

(3) That the act amounted to more than mere preparation; that is, it was a direct movement toward the commission of the offense; and

(4) That the act apparently tended to effect the commission of the offense of mutiny; that is, the act apparently would have resulted in the actual commission of mutiny except for (a circumstance unknown to you) (an unexpected intervening circumstance) (_________) which prevented completion of that offense.

Definitions:

To constitute an attempt, there must be a specific intent to commit the offense accompanied by an overt act which directly tends to accomplish the unlawful purpose. Preparation consists of devising or arranging the means necessary for the commission of the attempted offense. The acts which you did must amount to a substantial step and a direct movement toward the commission of the intended offense of mutiny. A substantial step is one that is strongly corroborative of your criminal intent and is indicative of your resolve to commit the offense. Proof that the offense of mutiny actually occurred or was completed by you is not required. However, it must be proved beyond reasonable doubt that, at the time of the act charged in the specification, you intended every element of the offense of mutiny. These elements are:

Read for mutiny by creating violence or a disturbance:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), the accused created (violence) (a disturbance) by (state the manner alleged); and

(2) That the accused created this (violence) (disturbance) with intent to (usurp) (override) lawful military authority.

(“Violence” means the exertion of physical force.) (“Disturbance” means the interruption of or interference with a state of peace or order.) (“Usurp” means to seize and to hold by force or without right.) (“Override” means to set aside or supersede.)

(This offense may be committed by (one person acting alone) (or) (more than one person).)

Or

Read for mutiny by refusing to follow orders or to perform duty:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), the accused refused to (obey the orders of __________ to __________) (perform (his) (her) duty as __________);

(2) That the accused in refusing to (obey the order) (perform this duty) acted in concert with (another) (other) person(s), namely, (__________) (and) (__________) (others whose names are unknown); and

(3) That the accused in pursuance of a common intent with another did so with intent to (usurp) (override) lawful military authority.

This offense involves collective insubordination and requires some combination of two or more persons acting together in resisting lawful military authority. “In concert with” means together with, in accordance with a common intent, design, or plan, regardless of whether this intent, design, or plan was developed at some earlier time. There must be concerted action with at least one other person who also shares the accused’s intent to (usurp) (and) (override) lawful military authority. (It is not necessary that the act of insubordination be active or violent.) It consists of a persistent and joint (refusal) (failure) to (obey orders) (perform duty) with an insubordinate intent, that is, an intent to (usurp) (and) (override) lawful military authority. (“Usurp” means to seize and to hold by force or without right.) (“Override” means to set aside or supersede.)

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On or about (date), at or near (place), did you do a certain act?

1b: What was that act?

2a: When you did this act, did you do it with the specific intent to commit the offense of mutiny?

2b: Did you intend every element of that offense as I have defined it for you?

2c: Was it mutiny by creating violence or a disturbance? Or was it mutiny by refusing to follow orders or to perform duty?

2d: For mutiny by creating violence or a disturbance:

Did you intend to create violence or a disturbance?

How so?

And at the time you intended to create this violence or disturbance, did you intend to usurp or override military authority?

2e: For mutiny by refusing to follow orders or to perform duty:

Did you refuse to obey the orders of _________? Or did you refuse to perform your duty(ies)?

Was _______ a person with the authority to give you orders? How so?

Were you required to follow those orders?

What were those orders (or what was your duty)?

Did you specifically intend to refuse to follow these orders or to perform your duty?

Did you intend to act in concert with another (others) in so doing?

Who did you intend to act in concert with?

Did you and the other person (people) have the common intent to usurp or override military authority?

3a: Do you believe that this act amounted to more than mere preparation and was in fact a direct movement toward the commission of the offense?

3b: Explain why.

4a: Did this act tend to effect the commission of the offense of mutiny? That is, would it have resulted in the actual commission of mutiny but for a circumstance unknown to you at the time or an unexpected intervening circumstance that prevented completion of the offense?

4b: What was that circumstance?

4c: Was it unknown to you at the time, or was it one that you did not expect?

4d: Did anyone force or coerce you to commit the offense of attempted mutiny?

4e: Were your act(s) voluntary? That is, could you have avoided committing these act(s) if you had wanted to?
