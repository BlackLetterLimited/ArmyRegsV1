Article 109A – TAKING THE MAIL

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you took certain mail matter, to wit: (letter(s)) (postal card(s)) (package(s)) (__________) addressed to (state the name of the addressee);

(2) That such taking was wrongful;

(3) That the (letter(s)) (postal card(s)) (package(s)) (__________) (was) (were) taken out of the (post office) (orderly room of __________) (unit mail box of __________) (__________) by you before (it) (they) (was) (were) (delivered to) (actually received by) the addressee; and

(4) That such taking was with the intent to obstruct the correspondence or pry into the business or secrets of any person or organization.

Definitions:

“Wrongful” means without legal justification or excuse.

“Mail matter” means any matter deposited in a postal system of any government or any authorized depository thereof or in official mail channels of the United States or any agency thereof, including the armed forces. The value of mail matter is not an element of the offense.
