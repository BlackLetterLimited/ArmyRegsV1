Article 106 – IMPERSONATING AN OFFICER

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you impersonated (a) (an) [(officer) (noncommissioned officer) (petty officer) (agent of superior authority) (of the) (Army) (Navy) (Marine Corps) (Air Force) (Coast Guard)] [__________] [(official of the Government of __________]; [(and)]

(2) That this impersonation was wrongful and willful; [(and)]

NOTE 1: Intent to defraud alleged. If the aggravating factor of intent to defraud is alleged, give element (3a) below:

[(3a)] That you did so with the intent to defraud (state the name of the alleged victim) by (state the manner in which the victim was allegedly defrauded); [(and)]

NOTE 2: If the accused is charged with impersonating an official of a certain government without an intent to defraud, give element (3b) below:

[(3a)] That you committed one or more acts which exercised or asserted the authority of the office you claimed to have by __________; [(and)]

NOTE 3: Impersonating by wearing rank and insignia. If impersonation by wearing rank and insignia is alleged, give element (4) below:

[(4)] That the impersonation alleged was by wearing in public (the rank and insignia) (__________) of a(n) (officer) (petty officer) (noncommissioned officer) (__________).

Definitions:

The term “impersonate” means to assume or to act the as the person or in the role of another.

The term “willful” means with the knowledge that one is falsely holding one’s self out as such.

There is no requirement that you or anyone else benefit from your impersonation. There is also no requirement that anyone actually be deceived by your conduct.

NOTE 4: Intent to defraud alleged. If the aggravating factor of intent to defraud is alleged, give the definition:

“Intent to defraud” means an intent to obtain an article or thing of value through a misrepresentation and to apply it to one’s own use and benefit or the use and benefit of another either temporarily or permanently.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Did you impersonate (a)(an) (officer) (noncommissioned officer) (petty officer) (agent of superior authority) (of the) (Army) (Navy) (Marine Corps) (Air Force) (Coast Guard)] [__________] [(official of the Government of __________]?

1b: Where were you when you did this?

1c: How did you impersonate OFFICER?

2a: Was this conduct done intentionally?

2b: Did you understand you were impersonating (a) (an) (officer) (noncommissioned officer) (petty officer) (agent of superior authority) (of the) (Army) (Navy) (Marine Corps) (Air Force) (Coast Guard)] [__________] [(official of the Government of __________]?

2c: Were you an OFFICER at this time?

2d: Was your conduct willful and wrongful?

2e: Did you understand at the time that your conduct was wrongful?

2f: Did anyone force or coerce you into this conduct?

2g: Could you have avoided doing this if you had wanted to?

2h: Why did you do it?

2i: Was this a freely made decision on your part?

2j: Did you have any legal justification or excuse for your conduct?

NOTE 5: Intent to defraud alleged. If the aggravating factor of intent to defraud is alleged, ask the following questions:

3a: Was the conduct was with the intent to defraud (state the name of the alleged victim)?

3b: How, exactly, did you intend to defraud this victim?

NOTE 6: If the accused is charged with impersonating an official of a certain government without an intent to defraud, ask the following questions:

3a: Did you exercise or assert the authority of the office of GOVERNMENT OFFICIAL?

3b: How did you do this? Describe what you did.

NOTE 7: Impersonating by wearing rank and insignia. If impersonation by wearing rank and insignia is alleged, ask the following questions:

4a: What rank and insignia did you wear?

4b: Did you wear it in public? Where and under what circumstances?
