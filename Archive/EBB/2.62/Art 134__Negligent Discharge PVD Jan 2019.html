Article 134 – NEGLIGENT DISCHARGE OF FIREARM

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you discharged a firearm, to wit: (a service rifle) (__________);

(2) That such discharge was caused by the negligence of you ; and

(3) That, under the circumstances, the conduct of you were (to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces) (and) (of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces).

Definitions:

Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline is conduct that causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.

Service discrediting conduct is conduct that tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.

The term negligence means the absence of ordinary care.

It is an act or failure to act of a person who is under a duty to use due care which demonstrates a lack of care which a reasonably careful person would have used under the same or similar circumstances.

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE at PLACE, did you discharge a firearm, specifically, (a service rifle) (__________)?

1b: Whose weapon was this?

1c: How did you come to possess it?

1d: Was the weapon loaded?

1e: Did you know it was loaded?

1f: Why did you discharge the firearm?

1g: What did you do that caused the firearm to discharge?

2a: Was that discharge caused by your negligence?

2b: Were you under a duty to use due care?

2c: Did you demonstrate a lack of care for the safety of others?

2d: Would a reasonably careful person have used (under similar circumstances) greater care?

2e: What would a reasonably careful person have done, or not done, differently?

2f: How did you actually discharge the firearm?

2g: Did you believe you had a justification or excuse?

2h: Were you ordered by a superior to behave (or respond) this way?

2i: Did some circumstance outside your control force you to do this?

3a: Under the circumstances, was your conduct to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces or of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces?

3b: In what way?

3c: Did anyone force or coerce you into this?

3d: Was it a freely made decision on your part?

3e: Could you have avoided discharging the firearm if you had wanted to?

3f: Did you understand your conduct was wrongful?

3g: Do you believe you had any legal authorization or justification for discharging the firearm?
