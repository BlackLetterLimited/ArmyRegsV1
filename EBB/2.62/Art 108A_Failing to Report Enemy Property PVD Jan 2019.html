Article 108A – FAILURE TO REPORT AND TURN OVER CAPTURED OR ABANDONED PROPERTY

Elements:

(1) That certain captured or abandoned public or private property came into the possession, custody, or control of you , namely, (describe the property alleged);

(2) That (state the time and place alleged), you failed to give notice of its receipt and failed to turn over to proper authority, without delay, the (captured) (abandoned) (public) (private) property; and

(3) See NOTEs 1 and 2 below.

NOTE 1: Firearm or explosive alleged. Give element (3a) when it is alleged that a firearm or explosive was sold or disposed of.

(3a) That the (state the property alleged) was (a firearm) (an explosive).

NOTE 2: Item NOT a firearm or explosive. Give element (3b) when the item is not a firearm or explosive.

(3b) That the property was of the value of $ __________ (or some lesser amount, in which case the finding should be in the lesser amount).

Definitions:

“Abandoned” refers to property which the enemy has relinquished, given up, discarded, or left behind.

“Enemy” includes organized opposing forces in time of war, any hostile body that our forces may be opposing, such as a rebellious mob or a band of renegades, and includes civilians as well as members of military organizations. “Enemy” is not restricted to the enemy government or its armed forces. All the citizens of one belligerent are enemies of the government and all the citizens of the other.

“Proper authority” means any authority competent to order the disposition of the (captured) (abandoned) property.

“Firearm” means any weapon which is designed to or may be readily converted to expel any projectile by the action of an explosive.

“Explosive” means gunpowders, powders used for blasting, all forms of high explosives, blasting materials, fuses (other than electrical circuit breakers), detonators, and other detonating agents, smokeless powders, any explosive bomb, grenade, missile, or similar device, and any incendiary bomb or grenade, fire bomb, or similar device. "Explosive" includes ammunition.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Did you come into the possession, custody, or control of enemy property?

1b: What exact property did you possess or control?

1c: Do you believe that this was enemy property as I have defined the term for you?

1d: Why do you believe this was enemy property?

2a: Was there a designated authority that you were supposed to turn the enemy property into?

2b: Who was that designated authority?

2c: Did you have any authority to keep this enemy property?

2d: When were you supposed to turn in this enemy property?

2e: Do you agree that you did not turn it in without delay?

2f: Did you inform anyone you believed to be a proper authority of the possession of this property?

[3a1:] Was the item you possessed a (firearm)(explosive) as I have defined the term for you?

[3a2:] Why do you believe this?

[3b1:] Do you believe the value of the item was more than $1000 US dollars?

[3b2:] Why do you believe this?

[3b3:] Was there some report that listed the value of this item?

[3b4:] Do you believe that report was accurate?

[3c1:] Do you agree that the enemy property was of some value?

[3c2:] How much do you believe it was worth?

4a: Do you believe you have any legal justification or excuse for failing to give notice to proper authorities about your (possession)(control) of this enemy property?

4b: Could you have given proper notice and turned in this property if you had wanted to?
