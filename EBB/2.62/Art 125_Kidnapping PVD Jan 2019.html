Article 125 – KIDNAPPING

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you (seized) (confined) (inveigled) (decoyed) (carried away) (state the name of the alleged victim);

(2) That you then held (state the name of the alleged victim) against that person’s will; and

(3) That you did so wrongfully.

Definitions:

"Inveigle" means to lure, lead astray, or entice by false representations or other deceitful means. For example, a person who entices another to ride in a car with a false promise to take the person to a certain designation has inveigled the passenger into the car.

"Decoy" means to entice or lure by means of some fraud, trick, or temptation. For example, one who lures a child into a trap with candy has decoyed the child.

"Held" means detained. The holding must be more than a momentary or incidental detention. For example, a robber who holds the victim at gunpoint while the victim hands over a wallet, or a rapist who throws his victim to the ground, does not, by such acts, commit kidnapping. On the other hand, if, for example, before or after such robbery or rape, the victim is involuntarily transported some substantial distance, as from a housing area to a remote area of the base or post, this may be kidnapping, in addition to robbery or rape.

"Against the person's will" means that the victim was held involuntarily. The involuntary nature of the detention may result from force, mental or physical coercion, or from other means, including false representations. If the victim is incapable of having a recognizable will, as in the case of a very young child or a mentally incompetent person, the holding must be against the will of the victim's parents or legal guardian. Evidence of the availability or nonavailability to the victim of some means of exit or escape is relevant to the voluntariness of the detention, as is evidence of threats or force, or lack thereof, by the accused to detain the victim.

The accused must have specifically intended to hold the victim against the victim's will to be guilty of kidnapping. An accidental detention will not suffice. The holding need not have been for financial or personal gain or for any other particular purpose. (It may be an aggravating circumstance that the kidnapping was for ransom, however.)

"Wrongfully" means without justification or excuse. (For example, a law enforcement official may justifiably apprehend and detain, by force if necessary, a person reasonably believed to have committed an offense.)

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE at PLACE, did you (seize) (confine) (inveigle) (decoy) or (carry away) VICTIM?

1b: Who is VICTIM?

1c: How did you know VICTIM?

1d: Explain how, on DATE, you came to (seize) (confine) (inveigle) (decoy) or (carry away) VICTIM.

2a: Once you (seized) (confined) (inveigled) (decoyed) or (carried away) VICTIM, did you then hold VICTIM against his/her will?

2b: How did you do this?

2c: Where did you take VICTIM?

2d: By what means did you transport VICTIM?

2e: Did you keep the victim confined or bound in any way?

2f: Did VICTIM indicate that he/she did not want to be confined?

2g: How did VICTIM act?

2h: What, if any, comments did VICTIM make?

2i: Did VICTIM request to be released?

2j: How long did you hold VICTIM before he/she was released?

2k: How was VICTIM released?

3a: Did you specifically intend to hold VICTIM against VICTIM’s will?

3b: So would you agree that your conduct was willful?

3c: Did anyone force or coerce you into seizing or holding VICTIM?

3d: Could you have avoided doing this if you had wanted to?

3e: Was this a freely made decision on your part to commit this misconduct?

3f: Did you understand at the time you seized and held VICTIM that your conduct was wrongful?

3g: Why did you believe that?

3h: Do you believe that you had any justification or authorization for seizing and holding VICTIM?

3i. Did you do it in self-defense or in defense of another?
