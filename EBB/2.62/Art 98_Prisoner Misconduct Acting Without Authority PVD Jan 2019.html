Article 98 – PRISONER MISCONDUCT – ACTION WITHOUT AUTHORITY TO THE DETRIMENT OF ANOTHER

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you acted without proper authority in a manner contrary to law, custom, or regulation by (state the act(s) alleged and the resulting detriment allegedly suffered).

(2) That the act was committed while you were in the hands of the enemy in time of war;

(3) That (this) (these) act(s) of you (was) (were) done with the intent of securing favorable treatment of you by (his) (her) captors; and

(4) That other prisoners, either military or civilian, held by the enemy suffered some detriment because of you ’s act(s).

Definitions:

“Enemy” includes organized forces of the enemy in time of war, any hostile body that our forces may be opposing, such as a rebellious mob or a band of renegades, and includes civilians as well as members of military organizations. “Enemy” is not restricted to the enemy government or its armed forces. All the citizens of one belligerent are enemies of the government and all the citizens of the other.

"Detriment" includes, but is not limited to, closer confinement, reduced rations, physical punishment, or other harm.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On (date) at (place) did you act without proper authority in a manner contrary to law, custom, or regulation by (state the act(s) alleged and the resulting detriment allegedly suffered).

1b: Explain.

1c. What law, custom, or regulations was your act(s) contrary to?

2a: When you committed this act(s), were you in the hands of the enemy in time of war?

2b: Describe the restraints or other methods used to keep you in the hands of the enemy.

2c: How do you know that it was a time of war?

3a: Did you commit this act(s) with the intent to secure favorable treatment from your captors?

3b: What favorable treatment did you wish to secure?

4a: Did other prisoners held by the enemy suffer some detriment because of you act(s)?

4b: Did anyone or anything force you or coerce you into committing this act?

4c: Could you have avoided committing this act if you had wanted to?
