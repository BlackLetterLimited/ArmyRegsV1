Article 91 – DISRESPECT TOWARD WARRANT, CHIEF, OR PETTY OFFICER

Elements:

(1) That (state the time alleged), you were (an enlisted service member) (a warrant officer);

(2) That (state the time and place alleged), you :

(a) (did) (omitted to do) (a) certain act(s), namely, (state the act(s) or behavior alleged); or

(b) used certain language, namely, (state the words alleged);

(3) That you ’s (behavior) (language) was directed toward and within the (sight) (and) (or) (hearing) of (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged);

(4) That you , at the time, knew that (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged) was a (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer;

(5) That (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged) was then in the execution of his/her office; (and)

(6) That, under the circumstances, by such (behavior) (language), you (treated with contempt) (was disrespectful toward) (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged); [and]

NOTE 1: If victim is alleged to have been the superior of the accused. If the specification alleges that the victim was the superior noncommissioned officer or petty officer of the accused, the military judge must instruct on the following two elements:

[(7)] That (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged) was the superior (noncommissioned) (petty) officer of you at the time; and

[(8)] That you , at the time, knew that such person was (his) (her) superior (noncommissioned) (petty) officer.

Definitions:

A (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer is in the execution of (his) (her) office when that officer is doing any act or service required or authorized to be done by statute, regulation, the order of a superior, by custom of the service, or military usage.

“Superior (noncommissioned) (petty) officer” of the accused includes any (noncommissioned) (petty) officer who is superior in rank to the accused.

“Contempt” means insulting, rude, and disdainful conduct, or otherwise disrespectfully attributing to another qualities of meanness, disreputableness, or worthlessness.

“Disrespect” means behavior which detracts from the respect due to a (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer. It may consist of acts or language (and it is not important whether they refer to a (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer as an officer or as a private individual, provided the behavior is disrespectful and the (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer is in the execution of his/her office at the time of the commission of the charged offense).

Disrespect by words may be conveyed by disgraceful names or other contemptuous or denunciatory language toward and within the (sight) (or) (hearing) of the (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer.

Disrespect by acts may be demonstrated by an obvious disdain, rudeness, indifference, gross impertinence, undue and excessive familiarity, silent insolence or other disgraceful, contemptuous, or denunciatory conduct toward and within the (sight) (or) (hearing) of the (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE, were you an enlisted service member/warrant officer?

1b: What was your rank?

1c: When had you enlisted?

1d: Are you confident you were on active duty?

1e: Were you receiving military pay?

2a. On DATE, did you (commit or omit a certain act or acts, to wit BEHAVIOR ALLEGED) (use certain language, to wit: WORDS ALLEGED)?

2b: Explain the circumstances.

3a: To whom was the ACT/OMISSION/LANGUAGE directed?

3b: Was it within the sight or hearing of a certain warrant, noncommissioned, or petty officer?

3c: Who was that?

3d: What was VICTIM’S rank?

3e: Where was VICTIM in proximity to you?

3f: Do you believe VICTIM could hear/see your ACT/OMISSION/LANGUAGE?

3g: Why do you believe this?

3h: Did VICTIM show any reaction? If so, explain.

4a: Did you know VICTIM was a warrant, noncommissioned, or petty officer?

4b: Was he/she wearing a uniform with his rank?

4c: Did you work for VICTIM?

4d: Had you previously seen VICTIM in uniform?

4e: Did someone tell you VICTIM was a warrant, noncommissioned, or petty officer?

5a: Was VICTIM in the execution of office at the time you did this?

5b: How did you know?

5c: Did you know at the time that VICTIM was in the execution of office?

6a: Do you believe that in doing/omitting/saying what you did that you were disrespectful to VICTIM?

6b: Do you believe that in doing/omitting/saying what you did

that you treated VICTIM with contempt?

6c: In other words, do you think your conduct/language showed an obvious disdain, rudeness, indifference, gross impertinence, undue and excessive familiarity, silent insolence or other disgraceful, contemptuous, or denunciatory conduct toward VICTIM?

6d: Describe which ones and why you believe so.

6e: Did VICTIM do or say anything to make you believe that he/she was not acting in role as Warrant/Noncommissioned/Petty Officer?

6f: Do you believe your conduct was wrongful?

6g: Did anyone force or coerce you into this conduct?

6h: Could you have avoided doing this if you had wanted to?

6i: Was it a freely made decision on your part to commit this conduct?

IF SUPERIOR ALLEGED ASK QUESTIONS 7 AND 8

7a: Was VICTIM your superior noncommissioned, or petty officer at the time?

7b: How do you know that now?

8a: Did you know that VICTIM was your superior noncommissioned, or petty officer at the time the incident occurred?

8b: How did you know that at the time?
