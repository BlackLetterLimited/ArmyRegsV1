Article 88 – CONTEMPT TOWARDS OFFICIALS

Elements:

(1) That you were a commissioned officer of the United States Armed Forces:

(2) That (state the time and place alleged), you (used orally and publicly) (caused to be published or circulated writings containing) certain words against the:

(a) (President) (Vice President) (Congress) (Secretary of __________); or

(b) (Governor) (legislature) of the (State of __________) (Commonwealth of __________) (Territory of __________) (__________a possession of the United States), a (State) (Commonwealth) (possession) in which you were then (on duty) (present); and

(3) That these words were (state the words alleged) or words to that effect;

(4) That, by an act of you , these words came to the knowledge of a person other than you ; and

(5) That the words used were contemptuous (in themselves) (or) (by virtue of the circumstances under which they were used).

Definitions:

Neither “Congress” or “legislature” includes its members individually.

“Governor” does not include “lieutenant governor”

The official or legislature against whom the words are used must be occupying on of the offices or be one of the legislatures named in the article at the time of the offense.

It is immaterial whether the words are used against the official in an official or private capacity.

It is not a violation of this article if adverse criticism, if not personally contemptuous, is made in the context of political discussion.

The truth or falsity of the statement is immaterial.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE, were you a commissioned officer of the United States Armed Forces?

1b: When did you obtain your commission?

1c: What was your commissioning source?

2a: On DATE at PLACE, did you use certain words, to wit: LANGUAGE ALLEGED?

2b: Did you do so orally and publicly?

2c: If not, did you cause the words to be published or circulated in writings?

2d: Who were the words against?

2e: Do you believe and admit that the words were against the: (a) (President) (Vice President) (Congress) (Secretary of __________); or (b) (Governor) (legislature) of the (State of __________) (Commonwealth of __________) (Territory of __________) (__________a possession of the United States), a (State) (Commonwealth) (possession) in which you were then (on duty) (present)?

2f: Which one?

3: And, do you admit that you used WORDS ALLEGED?

4a: Did those words become known to someone besides yourself?

4b: Whom?

4c: How do you know that?

5a: Were the words contemptuous in themselves or by virtue of the circumstances under which they were used?

5b: Which one, or both?

5c: Describe why you believe that.

5d: Did anyone or anything force or coerce you to use the contemptuous words?

5e: Did you have any legal justification or excuse for your conduct?

5f: Was it a freely made decision on your part to use these words?

5g: Could you have avoided doing so if you had wanted to?

5h: Do you believe and admit that your conduct was wrongful?
