Article 134 – NEGLIGENT HOMICIDE

Elements:

(1) That (state the name or description of the alleged victim) is dead;

(2) That his/her death resulted from the (act) (failure to act) of you , to wit: (state the act or failure to act alleged), (state the time and place alleged);

(3) That the killing by you were unlawful;

(4) That the (act) (failure to act) of you which caused the death amounted to simple negligence; and

(5) That, under the circumstances, the conduct of you were (to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces) (and) (of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces).

Definitions:

Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline is conduct that causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.

Service discrediting conduct is conduct that tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.

The killing of a human being is unlawful when done without legal justification or excuse.

“Simple negligence” is the absence of due care, that is, (an act) (or) (failure to act) by a person who is under a duty to use due care which demonstrates a lack of care for the safety of others which a reasonably careful person would have used under the same or similar circumstances.

The (act) (failure to act) alleged must not only amount to simple negligence but it must also be a proximate cause of the death. This means that the death of (state the name of the alleged victim) must have been the natural and probable result of your negligent (act) (failure to act).
