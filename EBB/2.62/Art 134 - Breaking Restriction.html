ELEMENTS:

In specification __ of Charge __, you are charged with the offense of Breaking Restriction in Violation of Article 134 of the UCMJ. By pleading guilty to this offense, you are admitting that the following elements are true and accurately describe what you did:

That (PERSON) ordered you to be restricted to the limits of (PLACE, specifically the LIMIT);

That (PERSON) was authorized to order this restriction;

That you knew of your restriction and the limits thereof;

That, on (DATE at PLACE), you went beyond the limits of the restriction before you had been released therefrom by proper authority; and

That, under the circumstances, your conduct was (to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces) (was of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces.)

Definitions

In considering those elements, you should use and understand the following definitions:

Restriction is the moral restraint of a person imposed by an order directing a person to remain within certain specified limits. “Restriction” includes restriction resulting from imposition of either nonjudicial punishment or the sentence of a court-martial, and administrative restriction in the interest of training, operations, security, or safety.

“Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline” is conduct which causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.

“Service discrediting conduct” is conduct which tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.
