ELEMENTS

In specification __ of Charge __, you are charged with the offense of failure to obey a lawful order, in violation of Article 92, UCMJ. By pleading guilty to this offense, you are admitting that the following elements are true and accurately describe what you did:

That a member of the armed forces, namely, (state the rank/name of the person alleged), issued a certain lawful order to (state the order given);

That you had knowledge of the order;

That you had a duty to obey the order; and

That (DATE / PLACE ALLEGED), you failed to obey the order.

Definitions

In considering those elements, you should use and understand the following definitions:

To be lawful an order must relate to specific military duty and emanate from your superior or from someone you had a duty to obey regardless of rank, such as a sentinel or a member of the armed forces police. The order must require you to do or stop doing a particular thing either at once or at a future time. An order is lawful if reasonably necessary to safeguard and protect the morale, discipline, and usefulness of the members of a command and is directly connected with the maintenance of good order in the services.

An order is illegal if it is unrelated to military duty, its sole purpose is to accomplish some private end, it is arbitrary and unreasonable, or it is given for the sole purpose of increasing the punishment for an offense which it is expected you might commit.
