Article 87 – MISSING MOVEMENT

Elements:

(1) That the accused was required in the course of duty to move with (state the ship, aircraft, or unit alleged);

(2) That the accused knew of the prospective movement of the (aircraft) (unit) (ship);

(3) That (state the time and place alleged), the accused missed the movement of the (aircraft) (unit) (ship); and

(4) That the accused missed the movement through (design) (neglect).

Definitions:

“Movement” means a major transfer of (a) (an) (aircraft) (unit) (ship) involving a substantial distance and period of time. The word does not include practice marches of short duration and distance, nor minor changes in the location of an aircraft, unit, or ship. You must have actually known of the prospective movement that was missed, though knowledge of the exact hour or the exact date of the scheduled movement is not required.

“Movement” may also mean the deployment of one or more individual service members as passengers aboard military or civilian aircraft or watercraft in conjunction with temporary or permanent changes of duty assignments.

Failure of a service member to make a routine movement aboard a commercial transportation, however, does not violate Article 87 when such failure is unlikely to cause foreseeable disruption of military operations.

The term "through design" means on purpose, intentionally, or according to plan and requires specific intent to miss the movement.

The term "through neglect" means the omission to take such measures as are appropriate under the circumstances to ensure presence with your unit at the time of a scheduled movement, or doing some act without giving attention to its probable consequences in connection with the prospective movement, such as a departure from the vicinity of the prospective movement to such a distance as would make it likely that you could not return in time for the movement.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a. On DATE, what was your (unit)(ship)(aircraft)?

1b. Were you required to move with UNIT on that date?

2a. Did you know your unit was getting underway on that date?

2b. How did you know of the movement?

2c. When were you given the notification, and did you have adequate time to prepare for the move?

2d. Where was UNIT going on that date?

2e. Where was your unit deploying to?

2f. Was it more than just a minor change in the location of UNIT?

2g. Was there ever any doubt in your mind that your unit was moving out on that date?

3a. On DATE, did you miss UNIT's movement?

3b. Why?

3c. Could you have made your unit's movement on DATE if you had wanted to?

3d. Did you have any justification or excuse for missing UNIT's movement on that date?

3e. Did you have any mental or physical infirmity that caused you to miss movement?

(MISS MOVEMENT BY DESIGN)

4a. Did you deliberately miss your unit's movement on that date? Why?

4b. Did you actively plan to miss UNIT's movement on that date?

4c. When did you form the intent to miss the movement?

4e. Did you intend to miss the movement continuously until the movement was made?

4f. Where were you when your unit moved?

(MISS MOVEMENT BY NEGLECT)

4a. Did you miss the movement by neglect?

4b. How did you miss it by neglect?

4c. If you had used more care, would you have missed the movement?

4d. Did you use less care than a person of ordinary prudence would have used under the same circumstances?

4e. Was it your own fault that you missed the movement?

4f. Was your negligence the cause of your missing this movement?

4g. Was it physically impossible for you to make that movement?

4h. If you had wanted to make that movement, could you have made it?
