Article 90 – WILLFULLY DISOBEY SUPERIOR COMMISSIONED OFFICER

Elements:

(1) That you received a certain lawful command to (state the terms of the command allegedly given) from (state the name and rank of the alleged superior commissioned officer);

(2) That, at the time, (state the name and rank of the alleged superior commissioned officer who allegedly gave the command) was the superior commissioned officer of you ;

(3) That you at the time knew that (state the name and rank of the alleged superior commissioned officer) was (his) (her) superior commissioned officer; and

(4) That (state the time and place alleged), you willfully disobeyed the lawful command.

Definitions:

“Willful disobedience” means an intentional defiance of authority.

The term "superior commissioned officer" includes your commanding officer, even if that officer is inferior in rank to you. "Superior commissioned officer" also includes any commissioned officer in the same armed force as you who is superior in rank and not inferior in command to you.

A commissioned officer of another armed force would not be a superior commissioned officer of yours just because of higher rank, but the term "superior commissioned officer" does include any commissioned officer of another armed force who is properly placed in the chain of command or in a supervisory position over you.

To be lawful, the command must relate to specific military duty and be one which the superior commissioned officer was authorized to give you. The command must require you to do or stop doing a particular thing either at once or at a future time.

A command is lawful if reasonably necessary to safeguard and protect the morale, discipline, and usefulness of the members of a command and is directly connected with the maintenance of good order in the services.

A command is illegal if it is unrelated to military duty, its sole purpose is to accomplish some private end, it is arbitrary and unreasonable, or it is given for the sole purpose of increasing the punishment for an offense which it is expected you may commit.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Did you receive a certain lawful command to (state the terms of the command allegedly given) from (state the name and rank of the alleged superior commissioned officer)?

1b: Describe exactly what you were told to do or not to do.

1c: Did you hear VICTIM give you this command?

1d: Did you understand what you were (not) supposed to do?

1e: Did you believe the command was a lawful one?

1f: Why?

2a: Was VICTIM your superior commissioned officer at the time?

2b: Who was VICTIM in relation to you and your job?

3a: Did you know at the time that VICTIM was your superior commissioned officer?

3b: How did you know this?

3c: Did you see him in uniform?

3d: Did you work for him?

3e: What was his rank?

3f: What was your rank at the time?

4a: Did you willfully disobey the lawful command?

4b: How did you disobey it?

4c: When and where did this take place?

4d: Do you believe and admit that you had a duty to obey the command?

4e: Why didn’t you obey it?

4f: Was this an intentional act on your part?

5a: Do you believe that you had any legal justification or excuse for your actions?

5b: Could you have avoided disobeying the command if you had wanted to?

5c: Did anyone or anything force or coerce you to disobey it?

5d: Was it a freely made decision on your part?
