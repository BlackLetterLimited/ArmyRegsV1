Article 92 – Violation of a Lawful General Regulation

Intro

MJ: ___________, I am going to explain the elements of the offense(s) to which you have pled guilty. By “elements” I mean those facts which the prosecution would have to prove beyond a reasonable doubt before you could be found guilty if you had pled not guilty. When I state each element, ask yourself two things: First, is the element true and second, whether you wish to admit that it is true. After I list the elements for you, be prepared to talk to me about the facts regarding the offense(s).

MJ: Do you have a copy of the charge sheet(s) in front of you?

First, I have some standard questions:

Standard Questions (all offenses)

What is your full name?

When did you join the Air Force?

What is your rank?

For how long (Length of enlistment)?

NOTE: Instruct member regarding impact of end of service on pay?

Are you presently on active duty in the Air Force?

Have you ever been discharged? Released?

What is your present unit / organization?

From between [starting date of offense] and [last date of offense]: to what military unit were you assigned?

From between [starting date of offense] and [last date of offense]: where was your unit located?

Were you on active duty between [starting date of offense] and [last date of offense]?

ELEMENTS

In specification __ of Charge __, you are charged with the offense of violating a lawful general regulation, in violation of Article 92, UCMJ. By pleading guilty to this offense, you are admitting that the following elements are true and accurately describe what you did:

That there was in existence a certain lawful general regulation in the following terms: (state the date and specific source of the alleged general order or regulation and quote the order or regulation or the specific portion thereof);

That you had a duty to obey such regulation; and

That (DATE / PLACE ALLEGED), you violated this lawful general regulation by (state the specific acts and any state of mind or intent alleged).

Definitions

In considering those elements, you should use and understand the following definitions:

General regulations are those regulations which are generally applicable to an armed force and which are properly published by (the President) (the Secretary of (Defense) (Homeland Security) (or) (a military department). General regulations also include those regulations which are generally applicable to the command of the officer issuing them throughout the command or a particular subdivision thereof and which are issued by an officer having general court-martial jurisdiction or a general or flag officer in command or a commander superior to one of these.

A general regulation issued by a commander with authority to do so retains its character as a general regulation when another officer takes command, until it expires by its own terms or is rescinded by separate action.

A general regulation is lawful unless it is contrary to the Constitution, the laws of the United States, or lawful superior orders or for some other reason is beyond the authority of the official issuing it.

NOTE: The lawfulness of a general regulation is a matter for determination by the MJ. However, it is a good practice to ask the accused whether the general regulation is lawful. The instruction above is directly from the MCM rather than from the MJ’s Benchbook.

Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

Do you have any questions about any of them?

Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

Do you believe and admit that the elements (and definitions taken together) correctly describe what you did?

MJ: At this time, I want you to tell me why you are guilty of the offense listed in specification __ of charge __. Tell me what happened.

Follow Up Questions

Are you familiar with (regulation XXX, para. XXX, dated XXX) which is at issue in this case?

Do you agree and admit that (state the regulation) meets the definition of a General Regulation as I’ve defined that term for you?

Do you agree and admit the (state the regulation) was a lawful general regulation?

Was (state the regulation) in effect on (date of offense)?

Do you agree and admit that you had a duty to obey (regulation)?

What did the regulation require you to do / refrain from doing?

On DATE at LOCATION, did you violate (state the regulation)?

How specifically did you violate the regulation?

Make sure to elicit specific facts from the accuse that explains each aspect of the violation of the regulation. Follow up with specific inquiries based on the regulation at issue. (E.g., Did you intend to alter your mood or function when you drank the cough medicine? Did you engage in a sexual relationship with a trainee?)

If the regulation was conditional: Do you believe any of the conditions applied to you? If yes, what were the conditions and how did they apply?

Final Questions

Did anyone authorize you to violate (state the regulation in issue)?

Did you believe you were authorized to violate it?

Did anything / anyone force you to violate it?

Do you believe you had any legal justification or excuse for violating it?

Could you have avoided violating this lawful general regulation if you had wanted to?

Do you admit, that on DATE, at LOCATION, you violated (regulation), which you had a duty to obey, by (state the nature of the allegation)?

MJ: Do counsel believe any further inquiry is required?

POTENTIAL ISSUES

Is the regulation in issue a punitive regulation? "[I]f a regulation does not contain language establishing that it is a punitive regulation, a violation of the regulation is not a criminal offense under Article 92(1)." U.S. v. Shavrnoch, 49 M.J. 334 (C.A.A.F. 1998). See also U.S. v. LeBlanc, 74 M.J. 650 (AFCCA 2015) and AFI 33-360.

Is the regulation actually a “general” regulation? Don’t assume it is. Who issued it?
