Article 134 – BIGAMY

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you married (state the name of the person you allegedly bigamously married);

(2) That this marriage was wrongful in that you then had a living, lawful husband/wife, namely, (state the name of the alleged lawful spouse); and

(3) That, under the circumstances, the conduct of you were (to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces) (and) (of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces).

Definitions:

Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline is conduct which causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.

Service discrediting conduct is conduct which tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: Did you on DATE, at PLACE, marry SPOUSE2?

1b: Was there a ceremony? Who officiated?

1c: Did you receive proof that you were actually married to SPOUSE2? (i.e. marriage license)

2a: Was this marriage wrongful?

2b: Why?

2c: At the time you married SPOUSE2, were you married to SPOUSE1?

2d: When and where did you marry SPOUSE1?

2e: Did you receive proof that you were actually married to SPOUSE1? (i.e. marriage license)

2f: When you married SPOUSE2, did you know that you were still married to SPOUSE1?

2g: Did you have any legal justification or excuse for marring SPOUSE2 when you were still married to SPOUSE1?

2h: Did anything or anyone force you to commit this act of bigamy?

2i: Could you have avoided committing the crime of bigamy if you had wanted?

3a: Do you believe that your conduct was either to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces or was of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces?

3b: Which?

3c: Why do you believe this?
