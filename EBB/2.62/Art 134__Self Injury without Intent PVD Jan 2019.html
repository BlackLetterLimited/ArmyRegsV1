Article 134 – SELF-INJURY WITHOUT INTENT TO AVOID SERVICE

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you intentionally inflicted injury upon (himself) (herself) by (state the manner alleged); (and)

(2) That, under the circumstances, the conduct of you were (to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces (was of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces); [and]

NOTE 1: Aggravating factors alleged. If the offense was committed in time of war or in a hostile fire pay zone, add the following element:

[(3)] That the offense was committed (in time of war) (in a hostile fire pay zone).

Definitions:

Conduct prejudicial to good order and discipline is conduct, which causes a reasonably direct and obvious injury to good order and discipline.

Service discrediting conduct is conduct, which tends to harm the reputation of the service or lower it in public esteem.

The term intentionally means the act was done willfully or on purpose.

The term inflict means to cause, allow, or impose.

The injury may be inflicted by nonviolent as well as violent means and may be accomplished by any act or omission that produces, prolongs, or aggravates a sickness or disability. (Thus, voluntary starvation that results in a disability is a self-inflicted injury.) (Similarly, the injury may be inflicted by another at your request.)

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE at PLACE, did you intentionally inflict injury upon yourself?

1b: What exactly did you do and why did you do it?

1c: Did you inflict this injury upon yourself on purpose?

1d: Was anyone else involved in this event?

1e: Why did you do it?

1f: Did anyone force or coerce you into doing it?

1g: Could you have avoided doing it if you had wanted to?

1h: Was this a freely made decision on your part to injure yourself?

1i: Did you understand that in injuring yourself, your conduct was wrongful?

1j: Do you believe you had any legal justification or authorization for injuring yourself?

1k: Do you agree that you were actually injured by your actions?

2a: Under the circumstances, was your conduct to the prejudice of good order and discipline in the armed forces or of a nature to bring discredit upon the armed forces?

2b: In what way?

NOTE 2: Aggravating factors alleged. If the offense was committed in time of war or in a hostile fire pay zone, ask the following questions:

3a. Was the offense committed (in time of war) (in a hostile fire pay zone)?

3b. Explain which one.

3c: Did you know that at the time?

3d: How do/did you know this?
