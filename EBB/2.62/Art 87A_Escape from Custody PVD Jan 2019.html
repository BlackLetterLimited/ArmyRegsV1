Article 87A – ESCAPE FROM CUSTODY

Elements:

(1) That you were apprehended by (state the name and status of the person who apprehended ##the accused##);

(2) That (state the name and status of the person who apprehended ##the accused##) was authorized to apprehend you; (and)

(3) That (state the time and place alleged), you freed yourself from the restraint of your custody before being released therefrom by proper authority; [and]

NOTE 2: Accused’s belief in authority of apprehending individual. If there is any evidence from which it may justifiably be inferred that the accused may not have believed that the person from whose custody he/she allegedly escaped was empowered to hold him/her in custody, give element (4) below:

[(4)] That you had reason to believe that (state the name and status of the person from whose custody ##the accused## allegedly escaped) was empowered to hold you in his/her custody.

II. DEFINITIONS

The term "apprehension" means to take a person into custody; that is, to place a restraint on a person's freedom of movement. The restraint may be physical and forcible, or it may be imposed by clearly informing you that you are being taken into custody, if that is followed by your submission to the apprehending authority. Once a person has submitted to an apprehension or has been forcibly taken into custody, continuing custody may consist of control exercised in the presence of the prisoner by official acts or orders. You may be said to have reason to believe that PERSON was lawfully empowered to hold you in custody when the circumstances that were known to you would have caused a reasonable person in the same circumstances to believe that you were in lawful custody.
