Article 131 – PERJURY-SUBSCRIBING FALSE STATEMENT

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you subscribed a certain statement, specifically (set forth the statement alleged) in a (judicial proceeding) (course of justice), specifically (state the proceeding alleged);

(2) That in the (declaration) (certification) (verification) (statement), under penalty of perjury, you (declared) (certified) (verified) (stated) the truth of that certain statement;

(3) That you willfully subscribed the statement;

(4) That the statement was material;

(5) That the statement was false; and

(6) That you did not then believe the statement to be true.

Definitions:

An oath is a formal, outward pledge, coupled with an appeal to the Supreme Being, that the truth will be stated.

An affirmation is a solemn and formal, external pledge, binding upon one’s conscience, that the truth will be stated.

“Subscribe” means to write one’s name on a document for the purpose of adopting its words as one’s own expressions.

Material means important to the issue or matter of inquiry.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On or about DATE at PLACE, did you subscribe a certain statement in a (judicial proceeding) or (course of justice)?

1b: What type of proceeding was it?

1c: Did you, in fact, subscribe to the following statement: (LIST THE STATEMENTS ALLEGED)?

2a: When you made the statement: STATEMENT ALLEGED, did you know you were doing so under penalty of perjury?

2b: Explain.

2c: Did you verify the truth of the statement: STATEMENT ALLEGED?

2d: What words did you use to verify the truth of the statement(s)?

3a: Did you make a conscious decision to subscribe to the statement: STATEMENT ALLEGED?

3b: Did you make the statement(s) willfully?

4a: Were these statements material to the proceeding?

4b: Do you admit that these statements would have impacted the proceeding?

4c: In other words, the statements were material because the court or someone in authority would consider them in deciding what to do in the case?

5a: Were the statements you made false?

5b: Specifically, how were they false?

6a: At the time you subscribed to these statements, did you believe any of them to be true?

6b: At the time, did you know that each of the statements was false?

6c: Do you agree that you lied when you subscribed to each of the statements?

6d: Did you think you had any legal justification or authorization for falsely subscribing to any of the statements?

6e: Why did you do so?

6f: Do you believe that your conduct in subscribing to false statements at this proceeding was wrongful?

6g: Could you have avoided committing this offense if you wanted to?
