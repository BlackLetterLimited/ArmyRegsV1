ELEMENTS

In specification __ of Charge __, you are charged with the offense of dereliction of duty, in violation of Article 92, UCMJ. By pleading guilty to this offense, you are admitting that the following elements are true and accurately describe what you did:

That you had (a) certain prescribed duty duties, that is: (State the nature of the duties alleged);

That you actually knew of the assigned duty duties; and

That (state the time and place alleged) you were willfully derelict in the performance of (that duty) (those duties).

Definitions

In considering those elements, you should use and understand the following definitions:

“Divers occasions” means on two or more occasions.

A duty may be imposed by regulation, lawful order, or custom of the service. A person is “derelict” in the performance of duty when (he) (she) willfully fails to perform it. “Dereliction” is defined as a failure in duty, a shortcoming, or delinquency.

“Willfully” means intentionally. It refers to the doing of an act knowingly and purposely, specifically intending the natural and probable consequences of the act.
