APPENDIX H: ARTICLE 66(F)(3) [FORMERLY DUBAY] HEARING PROCEDURES

NOTE: Scope of this appendix. In accordance with Article 66(f)(3), UCMJ, when an appellate court determines that additional proceedings are warranted, the appellate court may order a hearing as may be necessary to address a substantial issue. These hearings generally require a judge to make specific findings of fact and conclusions of law on a particular issue.

NOTE: DuBay Hearings. Prior to the statutory authorization of these hearings in Article 66(f)(3), the Court of Military Appeals had judicially created such hearings in US v. DuBay 37 CMR 411 (1967). Some practitioners still colloquially refer to these hearings as “DuBay Hearings.”

In any situation where it appears the appellant may have a legal right to the assistance of a former defense counsel, the MJ should obtain from the appellant an affirmative waiver of that former defense counsel's presence.
