Article 131G – WRONGFUL INTERFERENCE WITH AN ADVERSE ADMINISTRATIVE PROCEEDING

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and date alleged), you wrongfully did (a) certain act(s), that is, (state the act(s) alleged);

(2) That you did so in the case of (himself) (herself) (__________) against whom you had reason to believe there were or would be (an) adverse administrative proceeding(s) pending; and

(3) That the act(s) (was) (were) done with the intent to (influence) (impede) (obstruct) the conduct of the administrative proceedings, or otherwise obstruct the due administration of justice.

Definitions:

The term “wrongfully” means without legal justification or excuse. One can wrongfully interfere with an adverse administrative proceeding in relation to an administrative proceeding involving himself.

While it is required that you had the specific intent to (influence) (impede) (obstruct) the adverse administrative proceeding, there need not be an actual obstruction of the administrative proceeding.

The term “adverse administrative proceeding” includes any administrative proceeding or action, initiated against a service member by the Navy, Marine Corps, Department of the Navy, or the Department of Defense, or an agency of the Department of Defense, that could lead to discharge, loss of special or incentive pay, administrative reduction in grade, loss of a security clearance, bar to reenlistment, or reclassification.

Proceedings initiated by non-Department of Defense or non-Department of the Navy agencies are not adverse administrative proceedings.

It is not necessary that administrative proceedings be pending or even that an investigation be underway.

You do not have to know that administrative proceedings have been initiated or begun. It is sufficient that you had reason to believe there was or would be adverse administrative proceedings against (you)(______) or that some official of the military would be investigating (your)(_____’s) actions with the purpose of determining the appropriateness of an adverse administrative proceeding.

Asking that one not reveal or report that an incident occurred is not a wrongful interference with an adverse administrative proceeding unless you knew or had reason to believe that there were or would be adverse administrative proceedings pending and your acts were done with the intent to interfere with those proceedings.

If you advised a potential witness of his/her legal right to remain silent merely to inform the witness about possible self-incrimination, that would not amount to a specific intent to interfere with an adverse administrative proceeding. However, if this advice was given for a corrupt purpose, such as a desire to protect yourself or others from the prospective witness’ possibly damaging statements, a corrupt motive exists and you had a specific intent to interfere with an adverse administrative proceeding.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy/U.S. Marine Corps on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On DATE, at PLACE, did you do ACT?

1b: How did you do this?

1c: Did you have any legal justification or excuse for doing ACT?

1d: Were you authorized by anyone to do ACT?

1e: Did anyone force you to perform this ACT?

1f: Was your decision to perform this ACT a freely made decision?

1g: Could you have avoided performing this ACT if you had wanted to?

1h: Do you believe doing ACT on DATE at PLACE was wrongful?

2a: At the time you did this ACT did you have reason to believe that there was or would be an adverse administrative proceedings pending against someone?

2b: Against whom?

2c: What type of proceedings?

2d: How did you know this?

2e: What did you think might happen or had reason to believe might happen as a result of these proceedings?

2f: Do you believe and admit that this action constitutes an adverse administrative proceeding as I defined it to you?

3a: What was your purpose in doing ACT?

3b: Did you specifically intend to wrongfully influence or impede the adverse administrative proceedings or otherwise obstruct the due administration of justice?

3c: How so?

3d: While I told you it is not required, did you actually impede this adverse administrative proceedings?
