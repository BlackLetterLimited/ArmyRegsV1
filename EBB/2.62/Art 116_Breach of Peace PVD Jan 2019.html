Article 116 – BREACH OF THE PEACE

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged, you (caused) (participated in) an act of a violent or turbulent nature by (state the manner alleged); and

(2) That the peace was thereby unlawfully disturbed.

Definitions:

A breach of the peace is an unlawful disturbance of the peace by an outward demonstration of a violent or turbulent nature. It consists of acts or conduct that disturb the public tranquility or impinge upon the peace and good order to which the community is entitled. (Engaging in an affray and unlawful discharge of firearms in a public street are examples of conduct which may constitute a breach of the peace.) (Loud speech and unruly conduct may also constitute a breach of the peace by the speaker. A speaker may also be guilty of causing a breach of the peace if the speaker uses language which can reasonably be expected to produce a violent or turbulent response and a breach of the peace results.)

The word “community” includes within its meaning a (military organization) (post) (camp) (ship) (aircraft) (station) (__________).

“Turbulent” means noisy, boisterous, or violent disturbances.

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a. On or about DATE, did you in fact engage in ACTS in a public place at or near PLACE?

1b. Do you believe that you had any legal justification or excuse for this conduct?

1c. Do you believe and admit that your conduct was of a violent or turbulent nature, as I’ve defined that for you?

1d. Which one (violent)(turbulent) nature?

1e. Why was this conduct of a violent or turbulent nature?

1f. Where exactly did your conduct occur?

1g. Do you think that this act could be observed by people traversing through this area?

2a. Was there any shouting, yelling, or cursing while this conduct was ongoing?

2b. Given the place where this conduct occurred, did you believe and admit that the peace of that community would be disturbed by such conduct?

2c. Were police or law enforcement authorities called as a result of your conduct?

2d. Were you in fact apprehended or detained by law enforcement authorities as a result of this conduct?

2e. Did anyone force or coerce you into committing this misconduct? In other words, did you breach the peace of your own free will?

2f. Could you have avoided this conduct, if you had wanted to?

2g. Do you believe you have any legal justification or excuse for your actions?
