Article 109 – DESTROYING OR DAMAGING NONMILITARY PROPERTY

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), you willfully and wrongfully (damaged) (destroyed) certain personal property, that is (describe the property alleged) by (state the manner alleged);

(2) That the property (destroyed) (damaged) was the property of (state the name of the owner alleged); and

(3) [That the property was of the value of $__________ (or of some lesser value, in which case the finding should be in the lesser amount)] [That the damage was in the amount of $__________ (or of some lesser amount, in which case the finding should be in the lesser amount)].

Definitions:

An act is done “willfully” if it is done intentionally or on purpose.

Property may be considered destroyed if it has been sufficiently injured to be useless for the purpose for which it was intended, even if it has not been completely destroyed.

Property may be considered “damaged” if it has been physically injured in any way.
