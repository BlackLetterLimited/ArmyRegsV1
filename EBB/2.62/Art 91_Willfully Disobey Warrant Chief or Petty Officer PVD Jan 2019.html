Article 91 – WILLFULLY DISOBEY WARRANT, CHIEF, OR PETTY OFFICER

Elements:

(1) That (state the time alleged), you were (an enlisted service member) (a warrant officer);

(2) That you received a certain lawful order to (state the terms of the order allegedly given) from (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged);

(3) That you , at the time, knew that (state the name and rank or grade of the person alleged) was a (warrant) (noncommissioned) (petty) officer;

(4) That you had a duty to obey the order; and

(5) That (state the time and place alleged), you willfully disobeyed the lawful order.

Definitions:

“Willful disobedience” means an intentional defiance of authority.

To be lawful, the order must relate to specific military duty and be one which a (noncommissioned) (warrant) (petty) officer was authorized under the circumstances to give. The order must require you to do or stop doing a particular thing either at once or at a future time. An order is lawful if it is reasonably necessary to safeguard and protect the morale, discipline, and usefulness of the members of a command and is directly connected with the maintenance of good order in the service.

An order is illegal if it is unrelated to military duty; its sole purpose is to accomplish some private end; it is arbitrary and unreasonable; or it is given for the sole purpose of increasing the punishment for an offense which it is expected you may commit.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a: On or about DATE, were you an (enlisted service member/warrant officer)?

1b: What rank were you?

2a: On this date, did you receive an order from PERSON?

2b: What was the order?

2c: Was the order given orally or in writing?

2d: Did you understand the order?

3a: Who is PERSON and what was (his)(her) position?

3b: Did you know that PERSON was a (warrant/NCO/petty officer)?

3c: How?

4a: Do you believe that PERSON was authorized to give you the order under the circumstance?

4b: Did you have a duty to obey the order?

4c: When were you supposed to obey the order?

4d: Did the order relate to a specific military duty?

4e: Was it a lawful order?

4f: Why did you believe it was a lawful order?

5a: Did you obey the order?

5b: Could you have obeyed the order if you had wanted to?

5c: Do you believe and admit that your disobedience was willful?

5d: Explain.

5e: Did anyone force or coerce you into disobeying the order?

5f: Was it a freely made decision of yours to disobey the order?

5g: Did you have permission from anyone of authority not to obey the order?

5h: Do you believe you had any legal justification or excuse for not obeying the order?
