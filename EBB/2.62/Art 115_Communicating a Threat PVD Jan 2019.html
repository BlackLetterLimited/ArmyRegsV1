Article 115 – COMMUNICATING A THREAT

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged) you communicated certain language, to wit: (state the language alleged), or words to that effect, expressing a present determination or intent to injure the person, property, or reputation of another person, presently or in the future;

(2) That the communication was made known to (state the name of the person threatened, or a third person, as alleged); and

(3) That the communication was wrongful.

Definitions:

The language used must, under the circumstances, be such that a reasonable person would understand it as expressing a present determination or intention to injure the person, property, or reputation another, presently or in the future. Proof that you actually intended to do the injury threatened is not required.

A communication is “wrongful” if the accused transmitted it for the purpose of issuing a threat or with the knowledge that it would be viewed as a threat.

A communication is not “wrongful” if it is made in jest or as idle banter, or for an innocent or legitimate purpose.
