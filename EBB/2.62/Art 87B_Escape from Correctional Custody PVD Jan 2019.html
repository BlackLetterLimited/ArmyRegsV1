Article 87B – ESCAPE FROM CORRECTIONAL CUSTODY

Elements:

(1) That you were duly placed in correctional custody at (state the place of correctional custody alleged) by a person authorized to do so;

(2) That, while in such correctional custody, you were under physical restraint; (and)

NOTE 2: When accused’s knowledge of correctional custody status is in issue. Element 3 below must be given if there is any evidence from which it may justifiably be inferred that the accused may not have known of his/her correctional custody status and its limits. If given, Instruction 7-3, Circumstantial Evidence (Knowledge), is ordinarily applicable. See also Instruction 5-11-1, Ignorance or Mistake of Fact, for additional instructions which may be appropriate when such issue arises.

[(3)] That you knew of this correctional custody and the limits of the physical restraint imposed upon you;[and]

(3) or (4) That (state the time and place alleged), you freed yourself from the physical restraint of this correctional custody before you had been released therefrom by proper authority.

Definitions:

“Correctional custody” describes the physical restraint of a person during duty or nonduty hours (or both) imposed as a punishment under Article 15, Uniform Code of Military Justice. Any completed casting off of this restraint before being set free by proper authority is escape from correctional custody. An escape is not complete until a person has, at least momentarily, freed (himself) (herself) from the restraint of the custody (so, if the movement toward an escape is opposed, or if immediate pursuit follows before the escape is actually completed, there will be no escape until the opposition is overcome or the pursuit is shaken off).

(An escape may be accomplished either with or without force or trickery, and either with or without the consent of the custodian.)

NOTE 2: Status of person ordering correctional custody. Whether the status of the person ordering correctional custody authorized that person to impose correctional custody is a question of law to be decided by the military judge. Whether the person who imposed correctional custody had such status is a question of fact to be decided by the fact finder. The following instruction may be appropriate:

Any commander in the accused’s chain of command whose authority has not been restricted by higher authority is authorized to impose correctional custody under Article 15, Uniform Code of Military Justice.

Inquiry:

0a: Are you currently on active duty in the U.S. Navy?

0b: On [DATE OF EARLIEST OFFENSE] were you a member of the U.S. Navy on active duty?

0c: Have you been discharged or released from active duty since that date?

0d: In the specification is your name, rank, rate, unit, and branch of service correctly stated and spelled?

0e: Do you understand the elements and definitions as I have read them to you?

0f: Do you have any questions about any of them?

0g: Do you understand that your plea of guilty admits that these elements accurately describe what you did?

0h: Do you believe and admit that the elements and definitions taken together correctly describe what you did?

0i: Tell me in your own words why you believe you are guilty of this offense.

1a. Were you placed in correctional custody at (state the place of correctional custody) by a person authorized to do so?

1b. Who was this person?

1c. What was his/her rank?

1d. By what agency was he/she employed?

1e. Did he/she identify himself and explain why he/she was placing you in correctional custody?

1f. Had you done something which warranted being placed in custody?

1g. Did you believe he/she had the authority to place you in correctional custody?

2a. While in correctional custody, were you under physical restraint?

2b. Explain how you were restrained.

(Ask questions 3a through 3f if the Accused’s knowledge is in issue)

3a. At the time, did you understand that you were being placed in correctional custody?

3b. Did you understand the limits of your restraint?

3c. What were they?

3d. How did you know?

3e. Who informed you?

3f. Were you informed verbally or in writing?

4a. Did you, at some point after being physically restrained, free yourself?

4c. Did you free yourself before anyone with proper authority freed you from correctional custody or relieved you from restraint?

4d. Did you believe that anyone had given you the authority to free yourself?

4e. Did you feel you had any justification or authorization to free yourself before being freed by proper authorities?

5a. Explain in your own words how you freed yourself after being physically restrained.

5b. Could you have stayed in correctional custody if you had wanted to?

5c. Did you understand your conduct was wrongful?

5d. Was it a freely made decision on your part to free yourself?
