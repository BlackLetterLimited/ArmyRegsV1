Article 107 – False Official Statement

Intro

MJ: ___________, I am going to explain the elements of the offense(s) to which you have pled guilty. By “elements” I mean those facts which the prosecution would have to prove beyond a reasonable doubt before you could be found guilty if you had pled not guilty. When I state each element, ask yourself two things: First, is the element true and second, whether you wish to admit that it is true. After I list the elements for you, be prepared to talk to me about the facts regarding the offense(s).

ELEMENTS

In specification __ of Charge __, you are charged with the offense of False Official Statement, in violation of Article 107, UCMJ. By pleading guilty to this offense, you are admitting that the following elements are true and accurately describe what you did:

That (state the time and place alleged), you (signed a certain official document) (made to PERSON a certain official statement), that is: (describe the statement or document alleged);

That such (document)( statement) was (totally false) (false in that __);

That you knew it to be false at the time you (signed)( made) it; and

That the false (document)( statement) was made with the intent to deceive.

Definitions

In considering those elements, you should use and understand the following definitions:

“Divers occasions” means two or more occasions.

"Intent to deceive" means to purposely mislead, to cheat, to trick another, or to cause another to believe as true that which is false.

A statement is official when the maker is either acting in the line of duty or the statement directly relates to the maker's official military duties, or where the receiver is either a military member carrying out a military duty when the statement is made or a civilian necessarily performing a military function when the statement is made.
