Article 120b – SEXUAL ASSAULT OF A CHILD (for offenses occurring on and after 1 January 2019)

Elements:

(1) That (state the time and place alleged), the accused committed (a) sexual act(s) upon (state the name of the alleged victim), by (penetrating) (intentionally touching, not through the clothing,) (state name of the alleged victim)’s (vulva) (penis) (anus) (mouth) with (the accused’s (penis) (state other body part)) (a (state object)); (and)

(2) That at the time, (state the name of the alleged victim) had attained the age of 12 years, but had not attained the age of 16 years; [and]

NOTE 2: Penetration by object or body part other than penis or intentionally touching unclothed genitalia. If the penetration occurs by something other than the Accused’s penis or if sexual act consists of intentionally touching the unclothed genitalia, add the following element.

[(3)] That the accused did so with an intent to abuse, humiliate, harass, or degrade any person or to arouse or gratify the sexual desire of any person.

Definitions:

“Child” means any person who has not attained the age of 16 years.

The term “sexual act” has the meaning given in Article 120 except that the term “sexual act” also includes the intentional touching, not through the clothing, of the genitalia of another person who has not attained the age of 16 years with an intent to abuse, humiliate, harass, degrade, or arouse or gratify the sexual desire of any person.

The “vulva” is the external genital organs of the female, including the entrance of the vagina and the labia majora and labia minora. “Labia” is the Latin and medically correct term for “lips.”
