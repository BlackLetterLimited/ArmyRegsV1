NOTE: SENTENCING FORUM: Only one set of sentencing rules shall apply in a court-martial. RCM 902A(a) (2019 MCM), RCM 925(a) (2024 MCM). The judge must use one of the following options to advise the accused of sentencing forum options. Careful attention must be paid to the dates on which the offenses are alleged to have occurred. The dates of the alleged offenses will dictate which option must be used. There are three relevant time periods: (1) before 1 January 2019, (2) 1 January 2019 through 27 December 2023, and (3) on or after 28 December 2023. Offenses can occur in one or more of these time periods. Judges may need to refer to the 2019 and 2024 MCMs to determine the correct sentencing rules.

-OPTIONS 1A, 1B, 1C: “If convicted of any offense committed on or before December 27, 2023, the accused shall be sentenced in accordance with the Rules for Court-Martial in effect prior to December 28, 2023.” RCM 925(c)(1) (2024 MCM). If all offenses are alleged to have occurred on or before 27 December 2023, then OPTION 1A, 1B, or 1C should be used, as appropriate.

-OPTIONS 2A, 2B, 2C, 2D: “If convicted of only offenses committed after December 27, 2023, the accused shall be sentenced by a military judge in accordance with RCM 1002(a)(2) [Sentencing Parameters and Criteria].” RCM 925(c)(2) (2024 MCM). If at least one offense is alleged to have occurred on or after 28 December 2023, then OPTION 2A, 2B, 2C, or 2D should be used, as appropriate.

2-1-5. SENTENCING RIGHTS

NOTE: Depending on the judge’s interpretation of the sentencing rules on and after 28 December 2023, this paragraph only potentially applies in general and special courts-martial with specifications alleging at least one offense before 1 January 2019 and at least one offense on or after 1 January 2019. Before 28 December 2023, if an accused was charged with at least one offense before 1 January 2019 and at least one offense on or after 1 January 2019, then the accused had the option of selecting which sentencing rules apply: the pre-1 January 2019 rules OR the rules that went into effect on 1 January 2019. See RCM 902A (2019 MCM). The Accused was required to make this election before being arraigned (“…before the accused is arraigned, the military judge shall ascertain … whether the accused elects to be sentenced under the sentencing rules in effect on 1 January 2019, which shall apply to all offenses regardless of the date of the alleged offense”). RCM 902A(b) (2019 MCM). This election was legally significant because it informed the parties on, among other things, whether unitary or segmented sentencing applied and whether the parties should use a “pretrial agreement” or a “plea agreement” if the court-martial resulted in a negotiated guilty plea. Prior to 28 December 2023, judges used the colloquy in this paragraph, if applicable, to secure the accused’s sentence rule election before arraignment.

On 28 December 2023, the sentence rules applicable to general and special courts-martial changed again. Effective 28 December 2023, RCM 902A (2019 MCM) was removed and RCM 925 (2024 MCM) became effective. RCM 925(c)(1) (2024 MCM) provides that if an accused is “convicted of any offense committed on or before December 27, 2023, the accused shall be sentenced in accordance with the [RCMs] in effect prior to December 28, 2023. The military judge shall inquire into the accused’s election of sentencing rules after the announcement of findings and before any matter is presented in the presentencing phase.” Therefore, when an accused is convicted of any offense committed before 28 December 2023, RCM 925 provides that: (1) the accused shall be sentenced in accordance with the rules in effect before 28 December 2023 (possibly including, if at least one offense occurred before 1 January 2019, the requirement to elect, BEFORE ARRAIGNMENT, which sentencing rules apply) AND (2) the judge shall inquire into the accused’s election of sentencing rules AFTER THE ANNOUNCMENT OF FINDINGS. Therefore, if an accused is charged with at least one offense occurring before 1 January 2019 and at least one offense occurring on or after 1 January 2019, then the judge will need to determine whether, after 28 December 2023, the Accused must make this sentence rule election before arraignment OR after the announcement of findings. One option is to do both: (1) secure the election before arraignment AND (2) ask the accused again after the announcement of the findings (possibly allowing the accused to change the election). When presented with this fact pattern, judges are encouraged to conduct their own research on this issue and seek input from counsel.

Finally, RCM 925(c)(2) (2024 MCM) provides that if an accused is “CONVICTED of ONLY offenses committed AFTER December 27, 2023, the accused shall be sentenced by a military judge in accordance with RCM 1002(a)(2) [Sentencing Parameters and Criteria]” (emphasis added).

MJ: Because you are charged with offenses allegedly occurring both before 1 January 2019 and on or after 1 January 2019, the sentencing rules in effect prior to 1 January 2019 apply to this court-martial. However, you may elect to be sentenced under the sentencing rules that became effective on 1 January 2019. Your election regarding the applicable sentencing rules is irrevocable, unless I find good cause for a later request to withdraw the election. 2-1-7. RANDOMIZATION OF THE COURT-MARTIAL PANEL ON OR AFTER 28 DECEMBER 2023

NOTE: For courts-martial arraigned on or after 28 December 2023, the judge shall randomize the members in accordance with RCM 911 (2024 MCM) and Annex 2 of EO 14103. When randomization is appropriate, the “judge, or a designee thereof, shall randomly assign numbers to the members detailed by the convening authority.” See RCM 911 (2024 MCM) and Annex 2 of EO 14103. RCM 911 also provides that the judge must accomplish this randomization “[p]rior to assembly” and “at an open session of the court-martial.” Therefore, a judge may choose to conduct this randomization during the initial session through arraignment; e.g., before the forum rights explanation above or, if not immediately proceeding to trial, shortly before recessing the initial session. If the judge conducts the randomization during the initial session through arraignment, this paragraph provides guidance for accomplishing the randomization required by RCM 911. The judge may instead choose to wait until a later Article 39(a) session to accomplish the RCM 911 randomization. If the judge conducts this randomization at a later session, Miscellaneous Procedural Guide 2-7-29 provides a substantively identical guide for accomplishing the randomization. .
