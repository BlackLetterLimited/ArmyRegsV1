ELEMENTS:

In specification __ of Charge __, you are charged with the offense of Rape of a Child who has not attained the age of 12 years in violation of Article 120b of the UCMJ. By pleading guilty to this offense, you are admitting that the following elements are true and accurately describe what you did:

That (state the location and date of the offense), you committed a sexual act upon VICTIM, by causing penetration, however slight, of VICTIM’S (vulva) (anus) (mouth) by your (penis) (body part) (object); and

That at the time of the sexual act, VICTIM had not attained the age of 12 years; and;

NOTE: If the penetration occurs by something other than the Accused’s penis, add the following element.

That you did so with an intent to abuse, humiliate, harass, or degrade any person or to arouse or gratify the sexual desire of any person.

Definitions

In considering those elements, you should use and understand the following definitions:

"Child" means any person who has not attained the age of 16 years. The prosecution is not required to prove that you knew the age of VICTIM at the time the alleged sexual act(s) occurred.

IF APPLICABLE: The "vulva" is the external genital organs of the female, including the entrance of the vagina and the labia majora and labia minora. "Labia" is the Latin and medically correct term for "lips."

POTENTIAL ISSUES

Mistake of fact as to age is not a defense when the child is under the age of 12 years. See MCM, Art 120b (a.)(d)(1): “It is not a defense that the accused reasonably believed that the child had attained the age of 12 years.”
